# Domain: Commercial (AR) — Arabic Business Correspondence & Commerce

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** Arabic-speaking business executive, procurement officer, sales manager, or commercial correspondent in Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt, Jordan, or Lebanon.
**Secondary Readers:** Foreign business partners dealing with Arabic-language invoices, LCs, and trade documents; bank trade finance officers.
**Reader Expectations:**
- Requires **formal register** (اللغة الرسمية للتخاطب التجاري) with specific greetings and closings.
- Demands **respect for hierarchy** — titles (سعادة، صاحب السمو، صاحب المعالي، الأستاذ، السيد) used correctly.
- Expects **فاتورة ضريبية/تجارية** (tax/commercial invoice) compliant with local VAT/TAX laws.
- Neutral toward **English commercial terms** when integrated (LC, B/L, FOB, CIF) but expects Arabic expansion at first use.
- Highly sensitive to **deadline phrasing** — فيه (within), بحلول (by), اعتباراً من (effective from), لا يتجاوز (no later than).

## Decision Framework

### Framework 1: Business Letter Openings and Closings

| Letter Type | Standard Opening | Standard Closing |
|---|---|---|
| General business (formal) | تحية طيبة وبعد، | وتفضلوا بقبول فائق الاحترام، |
| To a company / department | السادة / شركة ... المحترمون، السلام عليكم ورحمة الله وبركاته، وبعد | وتفضلوا بقبول وافر الاحترام والتقدير، |
| To an individual (respected) | سعادة الأستاذ / الدكتور ... المحترم، السلام عليكم ورحمة الله وبركاته | مع جزيل الشكر والتقدير، |
| High official | صاحب السمو / صاحب المعالي / سعادة ... حفظه الله | وتفضلوا بقبول فائق الاحترام والامتنان، |
| Reply to inquiry | إشارة إلى استفساركم الكريم بخصوص ... نود إفادتكم بما يلي: | راجين لكم دوام التوفيق، |

## Standard Conventions

### 1. Business Letter Formatting Standards

1. **Paper:** A4 (210 × 297 mm) — standard across all Arab businesses.
2. **Letterhead:** Top-center or top-right. Includes: company name, logo, CR number, VAT number, PO Box, phone, email, website.
3. **Date format:** يوم ___ شهر ___ سنة ميلادية / هجرية. Or numeric: ___/___/____
4. **Margins:** Right 3 cm, left 2.5 cm, top 3 cm, bottom 2 cm.
5. **Font:** Simplified Arabic (MS Word) or Times New Roman. Body 12 pt. Title/headers 14 pt bold.
6. **Spacing:** 1.15 line spacing. Single space between paragraphs.
7. **Indentation:** 0.5 cm first line indent for paragraphs.
8. **Alignment:** Right-aligned for Arabic text. Left-aligned for English enclosures/quotations.
9. **Signature:** Right side. Name typed under signature, title, date. Company stamp overlays signature.
10. **Enclosures notation:** المرفقات / المرفق listed left-aligned at bottom.
11. **CC notation:** نسخة إلى: Name / Title listed in order of seniority.
12. **File reference:** رقم الملف / رقم الخطاب on left top corner after the letterhead.

### 4. E-Invoicing (ZATCA KSA) — Specific Requirements

1. **Phase 1 (Integration):** E-invoices generated and stored electronically (Dec 2021).
2. **Phase 2 (Integration + QR):** E-invoices with QR codes and XML/PDF generation (Jan 2023).
3. **Phase 3 (Peppol BIS):** Mandatory B2B e-invoicing via Peppol network — phased from 2026.
4. **Simplified Tax Invoice:** For B2C (retail) — limited fields, QR code mandatory.
5. **Full Tax Invoice:** For B2B — all fields above required.
6. **QR Code content:** Seller name, VAT number, date, total, VAT amount. Must be base64 encoded in TLV format.
7. **XML/PDF dual format:** Both required. XML for parsing, PDF for human reading.
8. **Archival:** 6 years (tax law retention). Must be readable and untampered.
9. **Penalties:** Late e-invoicing integration: SAR 5,000–50,000 per violation.
10. **UUID:** Each invoice must have a unique UUID per ZATCA specifications.

### 5. Moroccan / Maghreb Business Conventions

1. **Business language:** French is dominant followed by Arabic. Arabic correspondence increasingly used in public sector.
2. **Decimal comma:** , (comma) is the decimal separator. ١٠٠,٥٠ (100.50).
3. **Work week:** Algeria/Libya: Sat–Wed. Morocco/Tunisia: Mon–Fri.
4. **Addressee format in Maghreb:** السيد المدير / السيد رئيس القسم (Monsieur le Directeur style).
5. **Invoice VAT:** Morocco TVA 20%, Algeria TVA 19%, Tunisia TVA 19%. All require Arabic or bilingual invoices.
6. **Customs:** All Maghreb countries use the Single Customs Declaration (document unique douanier) in French/Arabic.
7. **Commercial register:** السجل التجاري / Registre de Commerce — bilingual document.
8. **Company types:** SARL (شركة ذات مسؤولية محدودة), SA (شركة مساهمة), SNC (شركة تضامنية).
