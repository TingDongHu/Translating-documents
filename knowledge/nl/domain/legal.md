# Juridisch Nederlands — Domain File: Legal Translation

## 1. Reader Model

### Primary Reader: Dutch Legal Professional

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Advocaat (attorney), notaris (civil-law notary), bedrijfsjurist (in-house counsel), rechter (judge), juridisch adviseur (legal advisor) |
| **Language Expectation** | Formal juridisch Nederlands; precise legal terminology consistent with the Burgerlijk Wetboek (BW), Wetboek van Burgerlijke Rechtsvordering (Rv), and Algemene wet bestuursrecht (Awb). Archaic formulations (*ingevolge*, *krachtens*, *zulks*) expected where appropriate. |
| **Knowledge Level** | Fully conversant with Dutch civil law, contract law, and corporate law. Expects exact code references (art. 7:670 BW). |
| **Tolerance for Ambiguity** | Extremely low. Every clause and subclause must be unambiguous and legally binding. Any ambiguity is a potential liability. |
| **Formatting Expectation** | Numbered articles (articlenummers), leden (subsections), aanhef (preamble), partij-aanduiding (party designations). Formal opening and closing phrases. |
| **Key Concern** | Legal enforceability, jurisdictional correctness, avoidance of interpretation disputes. The Dutch text must hold up in Dutch court. |

### Secondary Reader: International Business Partner or Their Counsel

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Foreign lawyer reviewing the Dutch version, or a bilingual business executive |
| **Language Expectation** | Expects the Dutch text to be a faithful mirror of the English source in structure and legal effect, using accepted Dutch legal concepts even when they diverge from common-law equivalents |
| **Key Concern** | That the Dutch version does not create unintended legal obligations absent in the English version |

### 3.2 Spelling and Style Rules for Legal Dutch

1. **Article references**: Format as *art. 3:33 BW*, *art. 6:248 lid 1 BW*, *artt. 7:670-672 BW*. Plural = *artt.*
2. **Capitalisation**: Legal document titles capitalised (*Deze Overeenkomst*, *Partijen*). However, in descriptive text use lowercase. Be consistent per document.
3. **Sentence length**: Legal Dutch tolerates longer sentences than English, but still prefer clarity. Break complex English sentences into shorter Dutch sentences where necessary.
4. **Modal verb precision**: *Moeten* for direct obligations, *dienen te* for formal obligations, *zullen* for future actions (not obligations), *mogen* for permissions, *kunnen* for possibilities.
5. **Passive voice**: More common in legal Dutch than English. *Wordt* passive is fine. *Zal worden* for future passive.
6. **Word order**: Dutch legal Dutch often uses SOV in subordinate clauses (standard Dutch). Maintain correct verb-final positioning in *dat*-clauses.
7. **Pronouns**: *Zijn/haar* for companies (both accepted). Use *hij/zijn* for natural persons unless gender-specific.
8. **Abbreviations in legal texts**: Define at first use. Common: *m.b.t.* (met betrekking tot), *o.a.* (onder andere), *c.q.* (casu quo), *i.v.m.* (in verband met), *t.a.v.* (ten aanzien van).
9. **Latin terms**: Use Dutch equivalents where they exist in BW. Keep Latin only where the principle is cross-jurisdictional.
10. **Numeric conventions**: Use decimal comma (3,14). Thousands dot or space (1.234 or 1 234). Money amounts in EUR format: *EUR 1.234,56*.

### 3.3 Document Structure for Dutch Legal Documents

**Contract structure (standaard indeling):**
1. **Aanhef** — Party identification, date, recitals (*Overwegingen*)
2. **Begripsbepalingen** — Definitions
3. **Artikel 1** onwards — Operative provisions
4. **Slotbepalingen** — Final provisions (applicable law, jurisdiction, notice)
5. **Ondertekening** — Signature block with *Ten blijke waarvan*

**Witness clause:** *Deze overeenkomst is ondertekend door de daartoe naar behoren gemachtigde vertegenwoordigers van Partijen op de data vermeld naast hun handtekeningen.*

**Signature block structure:**

*Namens [Partij A]*
*Naam: ____________________*
*Functie: ____________________*
*Datum: ____________________*
*Handtekening: ____________________*

**Notarial deed structure:**
1. **Aanhef** — Date, notaris name, appearing personen
2. **Identificatie** — Identity verification
3. **Verklaringen** — Declarations
4. **Slotsom** — Reading aloud and signature

### 3.4 Quality Assurance for Legal Translation

1. **Cross-reference all article numbers** — The BW book and article numbers in the translation must match the Dutch original. Verify using *wetten.overheid.nl*.
2. **Verify court names** — Always use the Dutch court name: Gerechtshof (not Court of Appeal), Hoge Raad (not Supreme Court of the Netherlands unless translated internationally), Rechtbank (not District Court).
3. **Check gender of legal persons** — In Dutch, *de* for both B.V. and N.V. (de besloten vennootschap, de naamloze vennootschap). Use *zij/haar* for legal entities consistently.
4. **Consistency of defined terms** — Once a term is defined (e.g., *de Overeenkomst*), always use the defined term with capital letter throughout.
5. **Verify translations of EU legal texts** — EU directives have official Dutch versions in the *Publicatieblad van de Europese Unie* (PB EU). Retrieve these rather than translating from scratch.
6. **Latin terms** — Check whether the Dutch legal system uses the same Latin term or a Dutch equivalent. When in doubt, use the Dutch term.
7. **Numerical dates** — Dutch format: *1 januari 2024* (without a leading zero) or *1-1-2024*. Never *January 1, 2024*.
8. **Time indications** — Use *24-uursnotatie* (14:30 not 2:30 PM). Unless the context explicitly requires AM/PM.
9. **Currency** — *EUR* precedes the amount: EUR 1.000,00. The euro symbol (EUR) or euro sign (*euro 1.000,00*) are both correct.
10. **Double-check false friends** — Pay special attention to words like *executie*, *consideratie*, *conditie*, *default*, *penalty*, *estate* — their Dutch meanings often differ from English.
