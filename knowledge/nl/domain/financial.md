# Financieel Nederlands — Domain File: Financial Translation

## 1. Reader Model

### Primary Reader: Dutch Financial Professional

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Accountant (registeraccountant / AA), financial controller, CFO, financial analyst, auditor, belastingadviseur (tax advisor),簿记员 |
| **Language Expectation** | Precise financial terminology consistent with Dutch GAAP (RJ-Richtlijnen), Titel 9 Boek 2 BW (annual accounts), and IFRS as adopted in the EU. Specific accounting terminology differs from both US GAAP and UK practice. |
| **Knowledge Level** | High — fully conversant with balance sheets, P&L, cash flow, consolidation, deferred tax, etc. Expects correct Dutch titles for financial statements and accounts. |
| **Tolerance for Ambiguity** | Zero tolerance for numeric errors, classification errors, or terminology that misstates the financial position. |
| **Formatting Expectation** | Structured financial statements with specific Dutch formatting: balance sheet in *activa/passiva* format, P&L in function or cost nature layout. Formal annual report structure (*bestuursverslag*, *jaarrekening*, *controleverklaring*). |
| **Key Concern** | Accurate representation of financial position, compliance with Dutch GAAP (RJ) or IFRS, tax treatment correctness, correct account classifications |

### Secondary Reader: Tax Authority (Belastingdienst)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Tax inspector, customs officer, tax auditor |
| **Language Expectation** | Terminology consistent with the Wet op de vennootschapsbelasting 1969, Wet op de inkomstenbelasting 2001, and Uitvoeringsbesluiten. Specific tax terms must be precisely correct. |
| **Key Concern** | Correct tax treatment, proper classification of income/expenses, accurate tax base calculation |

### Tertiary Reader: Investor / Shareholder

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Aandeelhouder, belegger, financial journalist |
| **Language Expectation** | Clear, comprehensible financial Dutch. Annual report language must be accessible yet precise. |
| **Key Concern** | Transparency, comparability with prior periods, meaningful narrative reporting |

### 3.2 Spelling and Formatting Rules

1. **Currency format**: *EUR 1.234.567,89* — EUR symbol before amount, periods as thousands separator, comma as decimal separator.
2. **Date formatting**: *31 december 2024* (in running text). *31-12-2024* in tables.
3. **Numbers in text**: Write out numbers under 20 within running text. Use digits for financial figures always.
4. **Negative amounts**: Indicated with *-* (minus sign) before the amount or parentheses *(* *) around the amount. Be consistent.
5. **Percentage**: *procent* (written out) or *%* (symbol). *procentpunt* = percentage point.
6. **Accounting equation**: *Bezittingen = Verplichtingen + Eigen vermogen* (not assets = liabilities + equity in English).
7. **Chart of accounts**: Dutch companies often use a 4- or 5-digit account numbering system. Never translate account numbers.
8. **Capitalisation in financial statements**: Use Sentence case for financial statement line items. Some firms use lower case throughout. Follow the client's existing style.
9. **Abbreviations**: Use standard Dutch abbreviations: *o.a.* (onder andere), *m.b.t.* (met betrekking tot), *i.v.m.* (in verband met), *t.a.v.* (ten aanzien van), *d.m.v.* (door middel van).
10. **Comparative figures**: Always label as *20XX* or *vorig boekjaar* in column headings. Never abbreviate as *PY* (prior year) in Dutch versions.

### 3.3 Annual Report Structure

**Dutch annual report (jaarverslag) — typical structure:**

1. **Bestuursverslag** (Management Board Report / Directors' Report)
   - Algemene informatie / General information
   - Samenvatting resultaten / Summary of results
   - Strategie en vooruitzichten / Strategy and outlook
   - Risicobeheer / Risk management
   - Maatschappelijk verantwoord ondernemen / CSR
   - Corporate governance
   - Financieel overzicht / Financial review

2. **Jaarrekening** (Annual Accounts / Financial Statements)
   - **Balans per 31 december 20XX** / Balance Sheet
   - **Winst-en-verliesrekening over 20XX** / Income Statement
   - **Kasstroomoverzicht over 20XX** / Cash Flow Statement
   - **Toelichting op de jaarrekening** / Notes
     - Algemene toelichting / General notes
     - Grondslagen voor waardering en resultaatbepaling / Accounting policies
     - Toelichting op de balans / Notes to the balance sheet
     - Toelichting op de winst-en-verliesrekening / Notes to the income statement
   - **Overige gegevens** / Other information
     - Controleverklaring / Auditor's report
     - Statutaire bepalingen / Articles of association provisions
     - Voorstel winstbestemming / Profit appropriation proposal

### 3.4 Quality Assurance for Financial Translation

1. **Zero-tolerance for numeric errors**: All figures, totals, subtotals, percentages, and financial data points must match the source exactly. Financial translation errors have legal and regulatory consequences.
2. **Verify currency codes**: EUR (not euro or euro), USD, GBP, CHF, etc. Use ISO 4217 codes in formal financial statements.
3. **Consistency with prior periods**: If a term was used differently in a prior-year translation, maintain the same term for comparability.
4. **Check RJ vs. IFRS context**: If the document states "in accordance with Dutch GAAP (RJ)", use RJ terminology. If IFRS, use IFRS terminology (often closer to English). Do not mix.
5. **Verify legal entity names**: Company names, fund names, and legal entity identifiers (LEI) are NEVER translated.
6. **Cross-reference Boek 2 BW**: For annual report terminology, verify terms against Titel 9 Boek 2 BW where possible using *wetten.overheid.nl*.
7. **Ratio definitions**: When translating ratio definitions, verify the numerator and denominator components match. A *current ratio* in US GAAP may be calculated differently in Dutch practice.
8. **Tax terminology**: Tax terms must be verified against the Wet VPB 1969, Wet IB 2001, or the Belastingdienst website. Incorrect tax terminology can lead to filing errors.
9. **Audit terminology**: For audit reports, verify against the NBA (Nederlandse Beroepsorganisatie van Accountants) templates for *controleverklaringen*. These have prescribed wording.
10. **Footnotes and disclosures**: Ensure that cross-references to specific notes are updated correctly (*zie toelichting X*) and that note numbering matches.
