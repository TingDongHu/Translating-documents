# Legal Domain: German Translation Standards

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** German lawyer (Rechtsanwalt), corporate legal counsel (Justiziar/in), or notary (Notar/in). The reader expects precise legal terminology (Rechtssprache), formal register, and strict adherence to German legal conventions. They read analytically, scrutinizing every modal verb, clause boundary, and definitional phrase. Ambiguity is unacceptable — German legal German tolerates complexity over vagueness. The reader is accustomed to long sentences (Schachtelsätze) but expects grammatically impeccable nesting with correct case assignments.

**Secondary Reader:** Judge (Richter/in) or court clerk (Rechtspfleger/in), reviewing a contract or legal opinion for enforceability under German law (BGB). They examine the text for conformity with §§ 305–310 BGB (AGB-Recht), the principle of good faith (Treu und Glauben, § 242 BGB), and statutory form requirements (Formzwang, §§ 125–129 BGB). Standard-form contracts (AGB) are subject to strict interpretation rules — ambiguity is resolved against the drafter (§ 305c Abs. 2 BGB).

**Tertiary Reader:** International client or business partner reading a German-law-governed document with translation support. The text must remain faithful to the German legal original such that the German version prevails in case of dispute. Where a bilingual contract exists, each version must be cross-verified for consistent legal effect.

**Reader Priorities:**
1. Correct legal terminology (e.g. "Verzug" not "Verspätung" for "default"; "Mangel" not "Fehler" for "defect")
2. Modal verb precision (muss / hat zu / ist zu / kann / darf / soll — each carries specific legal force under German law)
3. Sentence structure: German legal sentences are long but must parse correctly with nested clauses (Schachtelsätze) and proper case government (Rektion)
4. Definitional consistency: every defined term (Begriff) must be used identically throughout the entire document
5. Citation accuracy (Paragrafzeichen §, Absatz, Satz, Nummer, Buchstabe per German citation conventions)
6. Register: formal (Sie, Nominalstil, genitive attributes, no contractions, no Anglicisms)
7. Notarisation conventions where applicable (Beurkundungsgesetz)
8. Gender-neutral legal language: use of gender star (Beteiligte*r) or paired forms (Antragsteller/Antragstellerin) per Bundesrat guidance

### Table 1: German Legal Modal Verbs (Modalverben im Rechtsdeutsch)

| English Modal | German Legal Equivalent | Force Level | BGB Context | Example |
|---------------|------------------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| shall (obligation) | muss / hat ... zu / ist ... zu | Binding obligation (zwingend) | Legal duty, statutory requirement | "Der Verkäufer hat die Ware zu liefern." |
| shall (declarative) | ist / wird / gilt als | Declaratory (feststellend) | Definitions, recitals, descriptions | "Dieser Vertrag unterliegt deutschem Recht." |
| must not / shall not | darf nicht / ist nicht zu / ist untersagt | Prohibition (Verbot) | Express prohibition | "Eine Abtretung ist ohne Zustimmung nicht zulässig." |

| may not (no permission) | kann nicht / ist nicht berechtigt | Absence of right (fehlende Berechtigung) | No entitlement | "Der Mieter ist nicht berechtigt, Untervermietung vorzunehmen." |
| should (recommendation) | sollte / soll / wird empfohlen | Recommendation (Soll-Vorschrift) | Non-binding expectation | "Der Schuldner soll die Zahlung innerhalb von 14 Tagen leisten." |

| shall (conditional) | hat ... zu, falls / ist ... zu, wenn | Conditional obligation | Obligation triggered by condition | "Ist die Ware mangelhaft, hat der Käufer Nacherfüllung zu verlangen." |

## Standard Conventions

### Sentence Length and Complexity

German legal sentences are typically longer than English. The traditional German legal sentence (Schachtelsatz) places multiple subordinate clauses between the finite verb (position 2) and the clause-final predicate. In translation, respect this convention but apply readability limits:
- Maximum 3-4 subordinate clauses per sentence for contracts
- Where the source sentence has extreme nesting (>4 clauses), break into 2-3 shorter sentences
- Avoid misplaced reflexive pronouns: "sich" must refer unambiguously to its subject
- Use "der/die/das" (not "welcher/welche/welches") for relative clauses in legal texts

### Use of Genitive

German legal German prefers genitive attributes over prepositional phrases:
- "die Pflichten des Verkäufers" (preferred) over "die Pflichten von dem Verkäufer"
- "aufgrund dieses Vertrags" over "wegen diesem Vertrag"
- "mangels Nachweises" over "weil kein Nachweis erbracht wurde"

### Capitalization

- All nouns are capitalized (standard German rule)
- Defined terms: capitalize adjectives used as part of a defined term: "die Wesentliche Vertragsverletzung"
- Legal terms in definitions: "der Liefergegenstand", "die Vertragsparteien"
- Official bodies: "der Bundesgerichtshof", "das Landgericht"
- Pronouns: "Sie" (formal you) always capitalized; "Ihr" capitalized

### Punctuation

- Commas are mandatory before subordinate clauses (Nebensätze) introduced by "dass", "weil", "obwohl", "wenn", etc.
- No comma before "und" / "oder" between main clauses in legal texts (optional in general German)
- Colons introduce definitions or lists: "Der Begriff 'Liefergegenstand' bezeichnet..."
- Semicolons separate items in complex enumerations within a single §
- Parentheses: round brackets for citations; square brackets only for translator additions

### Gender-Neutral Legal Language

Following the 2018 Bundesrat recommendations and the 2021 amendments to the Bundesgleichstellungsgesetz:
- Use paired form: "der/die Antragsteller/in" or "der Antragsteller und die Antragstellerin"
- Gender star (Gendersternchen): "Arbeitnehmer*innen" — accepted in some contexts, avoid in highly formal court documents
- Colon: "Mitarbeiter:innen" — increasingly used in private-sector contracts
- Neutral forms: "die antragstellende Person" / "die Mitglieder des Vorstands" instead of "Vorstandsmitglieder"
- Be consistent throughout the document; do not mix systems

### Numbers in Legal Texts

- Decimal comma: 3,14 % (not 3.14 %)
- Thousand separator: period or thin space: 12.345,00 EUR
- Currency amounts: "EUR 12.345,00" (standard in international contracts) or "12.345,00 EUR"
- Percentage: always with a space: "5 %" (not "5%")
- Dates: "15. Juni 2025" or "15.06.2025" (not "June 15, 2025")
- Legal deadlines: "binnen 14 Tagen" not "innerhalb von 14 Tagen" in formal legal German
