# Administrative Domain: German Translation Standards

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** German civil servant (Beamter, Sachbearbeiter in einer Behörde) or administrative clerk (Verwaltungsangestellter). The reader expects conformity with Verwaltungssprache (officialese), correct citation of administrative regulations (Verwaltungsvorschriften, Satzungen, Erlasse), and use of standard form templates. The reader processes documents within a framework of established procedures (Geschäftsgang) and legal requirements. Ambiguity means rejection or delay. The civil servant relies on procedural templates (Musterbescheide) and established legal formulations (stehende Formulierungen) — departures from standard phrasing are flagged as potential errors.

**Secondary Reader:** Citizen or applicant who is the subject of the administrative act (Verwaltungsakt). The Verwaltungsverfahrensgesetz (VwVfG) requires that administrative acts be intelligible to the affected party (§ 37 Abs. 1 VwVfG: "Ein Verwaltungsakt muss inhaltlich hinreichend bestimmt sein."). The VwVfG also requires administrative acts to be comprehensible. The translation must balance legal precision with sufficient clarity that the citizen can understand their rights and obligations. The modern Verwaltungssprache reform movement (Bürokratieabbau, verständliche Verwaltung, Plain German) makes readability an explicit requirement.

**Tertiary Reader:** Administrative court judge (Verwaltungsrichter/in) reviewing the legality of the administrative act. They check for proper form, correct Rechtsbehelfsbelehrung, complete reasoning, and proper Ermessensausübung. The administrative act's translation must preserve every procedural detail — an incorrect Rechtsbehelfsbelehrung may extend the appeal deadline (§ 58 VwGO).

**Reader Priorities:**
1. Correct administrative act structure (Verwaltungsakt: Behördenkopf, Tenor/Verfügungssatz, Begründung, Rechtsbehelfsbelehrung, Unterschrift, Dienstsiegel)
2. Official forms and templates (Vordrucke mit amtlichen Bezeichnungen)
3. Proper authority names, jurisdictional divisions, and official titles (Amtsbezeichnungen)
4. Rechtsbehelfsbelehrung (right to appeal) — exact statutory wording required per § 58 VwGO
5. Aktenzeichen (file reference) format and Behördennummer conventions
6. Sovereign terminology (Hoheitsakte, Ermessen, Auflage, Bedingung, Widerrufsvorbehalt)
7. Register of officialese (Amtsdeutsch): Norminalstil, passive constructions, fixed administrative phrases
8. Signature and seal conventions (i. A., i. V., Dienstsiegel, Beglaubigungsvermerk)

## Standard Conventions

### Amtssprache Register Guide

German administrative language (Amtssprache / Verwaltungssprache) occupies a formal register distinct from both legal German and everyday business language. Key characteristics:

1. **Nominalstil (nominal style):** Verbs are converted to nouns. "Sie beantragen" becomes "auf Ihren Antrag hin". "Wir prüfen" becomes "nach Prüfung".
2. **Passive constructions:** Both Vorgangspassiv (werden) and Zustandspassiv (sein) are used extensively. "Der Bescheid wird erlassen." / "Der Antrag ist genehmigt."
3. **Extended participial phrases:** Attributive extended participles before nouns. "Der von Ihnen am 15. Januar 2025 gestellte Antrag auf Erteilung einer Baugenehmigung..."
4. **Fixed prepositional phrases:** "unter Bezugnahme auf", "in Anbetracht dessen", "unter Berücksichtigung von", "im Hinblick auf", "mit Wirkung zum"
5. **Functional verb structures (Funktionsverbgefüge):** Verbs combined with nouns. "in Vollzug setzen" (instead of "vollziehen"), "zur Entscheidung bringen" (instead of "entscheiden"), "in Kenntnis setzen" (instead of "informieren")

### Gender-Neutral Administrative Language

Since the 2018 Bundesrat recommendations and the enactment of gender-equitable language guidelines for federal authorities (Gemeinsame Geschäftsordnung der Bundesministerien, GGO):
- Use paired forms: "der Antragsteller/die Antragstellerin" or "der/die Antragsteller/in"
- Use gender-neutral alternatives: "die antragstellende Person", "die Mitglieder des Ausschusses"
- Use the gender star (Gendersternchen) sparingly: accepted in citizen-facing communications, avoided in highly formal court documents
- GGO § 42 Abs. 3: "In Rechtsvorschriften ist die sprachliche Gleichbehandlung von Frauen und Männern zu beachten."

### Specific Administrative Vocabulary Rules

- "Bescheid" is the general term for a written administrative decision; "Verwaltungsakt" is the legal term under VwVfG
- "Widerspruch" (administrative appeal) vs. "Klage" (court action) — never interchange
- "Ermessen" is discretion; "Ermessensfehler" (discretionary error) is a specific legal ground for challenge
- "Auflage" is not a condition but an ancillary obligation (stipulation/reservation)
- "Bedingung" is a suspension or resolution condition (if uncertain event occurs)
- "Frist" is always a deadline; "Termin" is a specific date for an appointment or hearing

### Formal Address in Correspondence

All administrative correspondence uses the formal "Sie" (always capitalized). The citizen is addressed as "Sie", not "du" even in digital communication. The authority refers to itself as "wir" (the office) or "die [Behördenname]" (the authority). Do not use "ich" for an individual officer except in the signature line.

**Example:**
"Wir haben Ihren Antrag vom 15. Januar 2025 geprüft und teilen Ihnen mit, dass..."
Not: "Ich habe Ihren Antrag geprüft..."

### Number, Date, and Time Conventions

- Dates: "15. Januar 2025" or "15.01.2025" (never month-first)
- Times: "14:30 Uhr" (24-hour format)
- Phone: "+49 711 1234-567" (per DIN 5008)
- File reference: "Az.: 123-456/789" — always include in reply
- Fees: "1.234,50 EUR" (decimal comma)
- Percentages: "5 %" (space before %)
