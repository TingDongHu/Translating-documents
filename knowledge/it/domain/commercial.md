# Commercial Domain (Settore Commerciale)

## Reader Model: Destinatario della Traduzione Commerciale

### Primary Reader Profiles

| Profile | Description | Language Expectation | Review Lens |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Imprenditore / Titolare d'impresa** (Business Owner) | Native Italian speaker, may read English for business but needs correspondence in Italian. Pragmatic, time-constrained. | Expects professional register. Wants clear, actionable communication. Rejects excessively bureaucratic language, appreciates conciseness. | Skims for key terms, payment amounts, deadlines. Focuses on commercial impact of text. |
| **Responsabile commerciale / Sales Manager** | Manages B2B relationships, negotiates contracts, sends and receives offers. | Requires correct business letter formulas (opening, closing, subject line). Expects consistency in company name references. | Checks offer validity period, pricing breakdown, delivery terms. Confirms tone matches relationship stage. |
| **Addetto acquisti / Procurement Officer** | Evaluates supplier offers, issues purchase orders, manages vendor relationships. | Needs precise terminology for Incoterms, payment terms, delivery dates. Lacks tolerance for vague language. | Verifies quantities, unit prices, total amounts, Incoterms classification, payment deadlines. |
| **Responsabile amministrativo** (Administrative Manager) | Processes invoices, handles payments, manages accounting records. | Requires fattura formats compliant with Italian tax law (IVA, reverse charge, split payment). | Checks VAT treatment, withholding tax, stamp duty (imposta di bollo). Verifies numerical cross-sums. |
| **Segretaria/o di direzione** (Executive Assistant) | Drafts and translates business correspondence, manages communications. | Needs reliable reference for letter formulas, subject lines, email etiquette. | Uses templates from this guide to ensure formal correctness. |

### Reading Scenarios and Document Types

| Document Type | Typical Length | Urgency | Key Translation Challenge |
|--------------|---------------|---------|-------------------------|
| Lettera commerciale / Business letter | 1-2 pp. | High | Correct opening/closing formulas; register matching sender-recipient relationship |
| Offerta / Quotation | 2-10 pp. | High | Pricing terms, scope of supply, validity periods, Incoterms |
| Ordine d'acquisto / Purchase Order | 1-3 pp. | High | Quantity, unit price, delivery date, PO number cross-reference |
| Contratto di vendita / Sales Agreement | 5-20 pp. | Medium | Balancing commercial brevity with legal precision |
| Fattura / Invoice | 1-2 pp. | Critical | VAT compliance, Italian tax requirements, numerical accuracy |
| Nota di credito / Credit Note | 1 p. | Critical | Reason code linking to original invoice, VAT correction |
| E-mail commerciale | 1-2 paragraphs | High | Tone calibration (formal vs. semi-formal); subject line conventions |
| Lettera di intenti / Letter of Intent | 2-5 pp. | High | Non-binding vs. binding provisions; translating "subject to contract" |
| Condizioni generali di vendita / Terms and Conditions | 5-15 pp. | Medium | Boilerplate translation; consistency with Italian Civil Code |

### Cultural Context: Italian Business Communication

Italian business culture values **relational communication**. Unlike German or Nordic directness, Italian correspondence often includes polite framing, greetings, and acknowledgments before getting to the point. The translator must calibrate:

1. **Register formality**: "Spett.le" (Spettabile) for companies, "Egregio/a" for individuals. "Gentile" is becoming more common, especially in email.
2. **Relationship stage**: First contact is very formal; existing relationships use "Cordiali saluti" or "Cari saluti".
3. **Regional variation**: Northern Italian companies may be more direct; Southern companies more formal.
4. **Email vs. letter**: Email has relaxed some conventions but formal email still mirrors letter structure.

### Table 5: Invoice Terminology (Fattura)

| English Term | Italian Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Invoice | Fattura | Standard commercial invoice. "Fattura differita" = deferred invoice (issued monthly). "Fattura pro-forma" = pro-forma invoice. |
| Credit note | Nota di credito | Issued to correct an invoice or cancel a charge. |
| Debit note | Nota di debito | Issued to charge additional amounts. |
| Invoice number | Numero fattura / fattura n. | Sequential numbering is mandatory under Italian tax law. Format: year + sequential number (e.g., 2025/INV/001). |
| Invoice date | Data fattura | Date of issue. Under Italian law, must be within the month of delivery for deferred invoices. |
| Tax point / Supply date | Data di effettuazione / Data della consegna | Date when the supply occurred. For "fattura differita", must be indicated. |
| Due date | Scadenza / Data di scadenza | Payment deadline. |
| Seller / Supplier | Cedente / Fornitore | "Cedente" is the formal tax term for the seller on an invoice. "Fornitore" is commercial. |
| Buyer / Customer | Cessionario / Cliente | "Cessionario" is the formal tax term. "Cliente" is commercial. |
| VAT / IVA | IVA (Imposta sul Valore Aggiunto) | Italian VAT rates: 22% (ordinaria), 10% (ristrutturazioni, turismo), 5% (servizi sociali), 4% (beni di prima necessita). |
| VAT number | Partita IVA (p. IVA) | 11-digit number. Format: IT + 11 digits (for EU transactions). |
| Tax code | Codice Fiscale (C.F.) | 16-character alphanumeric code for individuals. Also required for some companies. |
| Reverse charge | Inversione contabile / Reverse charge | VAT mechanism where the buyer accounts for VAT. Used in specific sectors (construction, electronics, etc.). |
| Withholding tax | Ritenuta d'acconto | Applied to certain services (e.g., professional fees). Typically 20% or 4%. |
| Stamp duty | Imposta di bollo | Required on invoices over EUR 77.47 if the invoice is issued as a paper copy or if the client requests a paper copy. EUR 2.00 per invoice. |
| Split payment | Scissione dei pagamenti | Public administration pays VAT directly to the tax authority. |
| Amount due | Importo a debito | Total amount payable. |
| Net amount | Imponibile | Amount before VAT. |
| Gross total | Totale fattura | Amount after VAT. |
| Unit price | Prezzo unitario | Price per unit of measure. |
| Quantity | Quantita | Number of units. |
| Discount | Sconto | May be "sconto incondizionato" (unconditional) or "sconto condizionato" (conditional). |
| Shipping costs | Spese di trasporto | Often listed as a separate line item. |

## Standards and Conventions

### Italian Commercial Documentation Standards

| Standard / Regulation | Area | Relevance |
|----------------------|------|-----------|
| **D.P.R. 633/1972** | IVA (VAT) regulation | Foundation of Italian invoicing rules. Mandatory elements for valid invoices. |
| **D.Lgs. 231/2002** | Late payment | Implements EU Late Payment Directive. Sets interest rates and remedies. |
| **D.Lgs. 127/2015** | Electronic invoicing (Fatturazione Elettronica) | Mandatory e-invoicing for most B2B transactions via Sistema di Interscambio (SdI). |
| **D.M. 55/2013** | FatturaPA | Format for electronic invoices to Italian Public Administration. |
| **UNI 10793** | Business letter formatting | Guidelines for Italian business correspondence layout. |
| **ISO 8601** | Date and time format | International standard for date formatting. |

### Verification Checklist for Commercial Translations

1. **Italian letter formulas**: Opening (Spett.le / Egregio / Gentile) and closing (Distinti saluti / Cordiali saluti) correctly matched to context.
2. **Invoice compliance**: All mandatory Italian invoice elements present (Partita IVA, Codice Fiscale, IVA rate, imponibile, totale).
3. **Currency format**: EUR with decimal comma and period thousands separator. "EUR" precedes the amount.
4. **Date format**: DD/MM/YYYY in commercial documents; written-out format "25 maggio 2026" in letters.
5. **Payment terms**: Clear mapping of net terms, discounts, and late interest. Italian legislative references correct.
6. **Incoterms**: Correct Italian equivalents used, with original English abbreviation preserved.
7. **Number agreement**: Quantities, prices, and amounts cross-total correctly after conversion.
8. **VAT terms**: IVA rates correctly assigned. Reverse charge and split payment identified and correctly labeled.
9. **Tone calibration**: Opening/closing register matches the relationship stage (first contact vs. established relationship).
10. **Company forms**: Foreign legal entity forms preserved with Italian equivalents in parentheses where needed.
