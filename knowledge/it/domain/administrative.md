# Administrative Domain (Settore Amministrativo)

## Reader Model: Destinatario della Traduzione Amministrativa

### Primary Reader Profiles

| Profile | Description | Language Expectation | Review Lens |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Funzionario pubblico** (Public Officer) | Works in a municipality, region, or national ministry. Reads and produces official documents daily. Deeply familiar with Italian administrative law. | Expects bureaucratic register (burocratese) with correct formulas. Documents must follow the format prescribed by L. 241/1990 and DPR 445/2000. | Checks protocollo number, document classification, signature legality, and procedural correctness. Rejects informal language. |
| **Segretario comunale** (Municipal Secretary) | Coordinates administrative acts, verifies compliance with regulations. Acts as a reviewer of administrative documents. | Requires precise legal-administrative terminology. Needs distinctions between "decreto", "determinazione", "delibera", "ordinanza". | Verifies the correct type of administrative act is identified. Checks formal attestation formulas. |
| **Cittadino utente** (Citizen / Service User) | Reads public administration communications, forms, notices. May not be familiar with bureaucratic language. | Needs clarity. While the original may use burocratese, translations intended for the public should favor plain Italian (semplificazione del linguaggio amministrativo). | Looks for key information: deadlines, costs, required documents, contact information. |
| **Responsabile procedimento** (Procedure Manager) | Appointed under L. 241/1990 to manage a specific administrative procedure. | Needs procedural terminology correct: avvio del procedimento, comunicazione, termini, conclusione. | Verifies the procedure timeline, notification requirements, and decision-making authority. |
| **Traduttore amministrativo** (Administrative Translator) | Translates documents between Italian and other languages for use by Italian PA or foreign entities. | Needs explicit mapping between administrative concepts across legal systems. | Uses decision tables to resolve structural differences between administrative systems. |

### Reading Scenarios and Document Types

| Document Type | Typical Length | Urgency | Key Translation Challenge |
|--------------|---------------|---------|-------------------------|
| Determina / Administrative determination | 3-10 pp. | High | Formal act structure; preamble (premesse) + reasons + decree (dispositivo) |
| Delibera / Resolution | 5-20 pp. | High | Corporate or council resolution format; voting records |
| Decreto / Decree | 2-20 pp. | High | Hierarchical level of decree (presidenziale, ministeriale, dirigenziale) |
| Circolare / Circular | 2-10 pp. | Medium | Interpretative vs. normative circular; addressee classification |
| Modulo / Form | 1-5 pp. | High | Field labels, instructions, declarations; simplicity is critical |
| Certificato / Certificate | 1-2 pp. | Very High | Fixed formulas; DPR 445/2000 format; no translation for official use |
| Autocertificazione / Self-declaration | 1-2 pp. | High | DPR 445/2000 Art. 46-47; formula must match exactly |
| Bando / Notice (public tender) | 10-100 pp. | High | Regulatory references; deadlines; requirements; exclusion grounds |
| Verbale / Official minutes | 3-20 pp. | Medium | Reporting verbs; passive constructions; approval formulas |
| Istanza / Application | 2-10 pp. | High | Addressing the correct authority; subject line; deadline compliance |
| Parere / Opinion | 5-30 pp. | Medium | "Favorevole" vs. "contrario"; conditional formulas |
| Notifica / Notification | 1-5 pp. | Very High | Service of process; delivery confirmation; time limits |

### The Challenge of Burocratese (Bureaucratic Language)

Italian administrative language -- "burocratese" -- has distinct characteristics that must be preserved or simplified depending on the target reader:

1. **Nominalization**: Preference for nouns over verbs ("effettuare la consegna" instead of "consegnare").
2. **Passive constructions**: Heavy use of "si" passivante and passato remoto ("si dispone", "viene approvato").
3. **Formal connectives**: "Atteso che", "considerato che", "visto che", "preso atto che".
4. **Archaic formulas**: "Il sottoscritto", "la sottoscritta", "il/la medesimo/a", "il/la predetto/a".
5. **Circumlocution**: Complex expressions for simple concepts.
6. **Verbal noun clusters**: "Procedere alla nomina" for "nominare".
7. **Abbreviations**: "c.a." (cortese attenzione), "p.c." (per conoscenza), "ns." (nostro), "vs." (vostro).

The translator must decide: preserve the formal register (for official translations) or simplify (for citizen-facing communications).

### Language Simplification Guidelines (Direttive sulla Semplificazione)

In recent years, Italian public administration has moved toward clearer language (Circolare 22/2001 of Ministero della Funzione Pubblica). Key principles:

| Principle | Rule | Burocratese (Avoid) | Simplified (Prefer) |
|-----------|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Short sentences | Max 20-25 words per sentence | "Il sottoscritto, considerato che..." | "Io, considerando che..." |
| Active voice | Prefer active over passive | "Viene disposta la convocazione..." | "Convocchiamo..." |
| Concrete verbs | Avoid nominalizations | "Effettuare la notifica" | "Notificare" |
| Clear references | Avoid "medesimo", "suddetto" | "Il medesimo ufficio" | "Questo ufficio" |
| Positive phrasing | Say what IS, not what IS NOT | "Non e ammessa la deroga" | "La deroga non e permessa" |
| Simple connectors | Avoid "atteso che", "visto che" | "Atteso che il termine e decorso" | "Il termine e scaduto" |

### Verification Checklist for Administrative Translations

1. **Protocol format**: Protocollo number, date, and classification reproduced correctly for outgoing official documents.
2. **Act type correctly identified**: "Decreto" vs. "Delibera" vs. "Determina" vs. "Ordinanza" distinguished and consistently translated.
3. **Structural completeness**: Preamble (premesse/visto/considerato), reasons (motivazione), operative part (dispositivo), place, date, signature -- all present.
4. **Formula accuracy**: Standard formulas ("si comunica che", "si allega", "il sottoscritto") correctly matched to English originals.
5. **Legal references**: Law numbers and dates (L. 241/1990, DPR 445/2000, D.Lgs. 267/2000, etc.) correctly cited in context.
6. **Deadline terminology**: "Entro 30 giorni", "termine perentorio", "sospensione dei termini" -- correctly translated.
7. **Pronoun register**: "Lei" (formal) used consistently for addressing individuals; "Voi" for addressing multiple recipients.
