# Legal Domain (Settore Giuridico)

## Reader Model: Destinatario della Traduzione Giuridica

### Primary Reader Profiles

| Profile | Description | Language Expectation | Review Lens |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Avvocato / Legale** (Lawyer) | Native Italian speaker, reads EN legal documents routinely but expects precise Italian legal terminology. | Requires terminology consistent with Codice Civile, Codice di Procedura Civile, and Italian case law. Rejects anglicisms where an Italian legal term exists. | Scans for correct legal institution names, article references, and procedural terms. Verifies that the translated document would be enforceable under Italian law. |
| **Giudice / Magistrato** (Judge) | Reads translated evidence or foreign judgments in Italian proceedings. | Requires formal, register-appropriate language. Procedural terminology must match the Italian Code of Civil/Criminal Procedure. | Checks that the translation faithfully represents the source without adding interpretation. Admissibility depends on precision. |
| **Notaio** (Notary) | Involved in real estate, corporate, and inheritance translations. | Requires absolute precision in property descriptions, corporate names, and legal entity designations. | Verifies that translations of foreign legal concepts include appropriate caveats ("istituto giuridico equivalente a..."). |
| **Consulente legale aziendale** (Corporate Legal Counsel) | Reviews contracts, NDAs, compliance documents. Bilingual but needs Italian version for Italian counterparties. | Expects contract structure aligned with Italian conventions. Defined terms must be handled carefully. | Cross-references definitions, representations, warranties. Checks boilerplate for consistency. |
| **Traduttore giuridico** (Legal Translator) | Professional translator specializing in legal texts. Consults this guide. | Needs explicit mapping between common law and civil law concepts, with warnings about non-equivalent institutions. | Uses decision tables to resolve structural differences between legal systems. |

### Reading Scenarios and Document Types

| Document Type | Typical Length | Urgency | Key Translation Challenge |
|--------------|---------------|---------|-------------------------|
| Contratto / Agreement | 5-50 pp. | High | Defined terms, representations vs. Italian legal categories, governing law clauses |
| Atto giudiziario / Court filing | 5-100 pp. | High | Procedural terminology specific to common law or Italian system |
| Parere legale / Legal opinion | 5-30 pp. | Medium | Nuance in legal reasoning, hypothetical constructions |
| Sentenza straniera / Foreign judgment | 10-100 pp. | Medium | Court structure unfamiliar to Italian system, non-equivalent remedies |
| Verbale / Minutes of meeting (legal context) | 2-20 pp. | High | Formal register, passive constructions, reporting verbs |
| Procura / Power of attorney | 1-5 pp. | Very High | Strict formal requirements; translation may need legalization/apostille |
| Certificato / Certificate (birth, marriage, etc.) | 1-2 pp. | Very High | Fixed forms; exact match to Italian civil status terminology |

### The Fundamental Challenge: Civil Law vs. Common Law

Italian law is a **civil law system** based on the Codice Civile (1942) and codified statutes. English legal documents often originate from **common law** jurisdictions (UK, US, Australia, etc.) where law develops through judicial precedent. This structural difference means:

1. **Direct equivalence is rare**: An English "trust" has no exact equivalent in Italian law (the closest is "trust" itself, imported via the Hague Convention). A "power of attorney" maps roughly to "procura" but the scope differs.
2. **Remedies differ**: Common law "specific performance" does not map cleanly to Italian "esecuzione in forma specifica". Equitable remedies (injunction, estoppel) may need explanatory translations.
3. **Procedural roles differ**: "Barrister" is not "avvocato" -- the role is bifurcated in English law. Use "barrister" with an Italian gloss.
4. **Document structure differs**: English contracts are typically longer and more explicit than Italian ones, which rely more on Codice Civile default provisions.

### Table 2: Contract Structure (Struttura del Contratto)

| English Section | Italian Equivalent | Notes |
|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Parties | Le parti / Soggetti | "Tra" + party names, typically "La societa XYZ S.p.A., rappresentata da..." |
| Recitals / Whereas | Premesse | "Considerato che..." or "Premesso che..." numbered with letters (A, B, C). |
| Definitions | Definizioni | Less common in Italian contracts than English ones. Used in international contracts translated to Italian. |
| Representations and Warranties | Dichiarazioni e garanzie | Not a native Italian category; increasingly used in M&A contracts. The Italian guarantee system is in Art. 1476-1497 cc. |
| Covenants | Obblighi / Impegni / Covenant | "Covenant" often kept in English in Italian legal English with parenthetical translation. |
| Conditions Precedent | Condizioni sospensive | Art. 1353 cc: "sospensiva" = condition that suspends effectiveness until met. |
| Conditions Subsequent | Condizioni risolutive | Art. 1353 cc: "risolutiva" = condition that terminates upon occurrence. |
| Termination | Risoluzione / Recesso | "Risoluzione" = termination for breach/non-performance (Art. 1453 cc). "Recesso" = withdrawal (Art. 1373 cc). |
| Governing Law | Legge applicabile / Foro competente | "Il presente contratto e regolato dalla legge italiana." Also "foro competente" = jurisdiction. |
| Entire Agreement | Accordo integrale / Clausola di completezza | "Il presente contratto costituisce l'intero accordo tra le parti..." |

| Limited Liability Company (US) / Ltd (UK) | S.r.l. (Societa a Responsabilita Limitata) | Private limited company. "LLC" kept as is, with Italian equivalent in parentheses. |

| Subsidiary | Societa controllata / Filiale | "Controllata" (controlled entity) is the precise legal term. "Figlia" is colloquial. |

### Table 5: Court and Procedural Terminology

| English Term | Italian Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Court | Tribunale / Corte | "Tribunale" is the ordinary court. "Corte d'Appello" = Court of Appeal. "Corte di Cassazione" = Supreme Court of Cassation. |
| Claimant / Plaintiff | Attore | The party bringing the claim. "Ricorrente" for special proceedings. |
| Defendant | Convenuto | Civil defendant. "Imputato" in criminal proceedings. |
| Judgment | Sentenza | Final judgment. "Ordinanza" = order. "Decreto" = decree. |
| Appeal | Appello | "Proporre appello" = to file an appeal. |
| Lawsuit | Causa / Azione giudiziaria | "Causa civile" = civil lawsuit. "Procedimento" = proceedings. |
| Discovery (US) / Disclosure (UK) | Produzione documentale | No exact equivalent. Italian civil procedure has "produzione dei documenti" and "interrogatorio formale". Full discovery does not exist. |
| Deposition | Deposizione / Testimonianza | A sworn out-of-court testimony. "Deposizione" is the formal term in Italian. |
| Affidavit | Dichiarazione giurata / Affidavit | "Affidavit" is recognized. "Dichiarazione sostitutiva di atto notorio" (DPR 445/2000) is the Italian functional equivalent. |
| Injunction | Ingiunzione / Provvedimento cautelare | "Ingiunzione" = court order to do/not do something. "Provvedimento cautelare" = interim measure. |
| Damages | Danni / Risarcimento | "Danni patrimoniali" = financial losses. "Danni non patrimoniali" = non-economic damages. "Risarcimento" = compensation. |

### Table 6: Contractual Verbs and Performatives

| English Verb | Italian Equivalent | Form | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|------|-------|
| The Parties agree | Le parti convengono / concordano | Present indicative | "Convenire" is formal and common in Italian contracts. |
| The Company hereby undertakes | La societa si obbliga con il presente atto | Reflexive + formal modifier | "Si obbliga" is the standard performative. |
| The Seller warrants and represents | Il Venditore dichiara e garantisce | Present indicative, double verb | Standard formula in M&A. |
| Shall (obligation) | Dovra / e tenuto a | Future or present "e tenuto a" | "Dovra" (future) is used for ongoing obligations. "E tenuto a" is stricter. |
| May (permission) | Puo / ha facolta di | Present | "Ha facolta di" is more formal. |
| Shall not (prohibition) | Non puo / non deve / e vietato | Present or past participle | "Non puo" = may not. "Non deve" = must not. "E vietato" = is prohibited (strongest). |
| Deem | Si considera / si intende | Impersonal "si" + verb | "Si considera" is standard. "Il contratto si considera concluso quando..." |
| Indemnify | Tenere indenne / Manlevare | Infinitive + from | "Tenere indenne il Compratore da qualsiasi perdita..." |

### Table 7: Boilerplate Clauses

| English Clause | Italian Equivalent | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement... | Il presente Contratto costituisce l'intero accordo... | "Presente" is the standard formal modifier for documents. |
| No amendment shall be effective unless in writing... | Qualsiasi modifica al presente Contratto dovra essere redatta per iscritto... | "Per iscritto" = in writing. "Modifica" = amendment. |
| This Agreement shall be governed by Italian law. | Il presente Contratto e regolato dalla legge italiana. | Use "e regolato da" not "e governato da" (calque). |

| Force Majeure | Forza maggiore | Art. 1218 cc. "Cause di forza maggiore" or "caso fortuito". |
| Severability | Clausola di salvaguardia / Nullita parziale | "Se una qualsiasi disposizione del presente Contratto dovesse essere dichiarata nulla o inefficace..." |
| Notices | Comunicazioni / Notifiche | "Tutte le comunicazioni tra le parti dovranno essere inviate a..." |
| Counterparts | Controfile / Copie conformi | "Il presente Contratto puo essere sottoscritto in due copie... | 
| Waiver | Rinuncia / Deroga | "La mancata richiesta di adempimento di una obbligazione non costituisce rinuncia." |

### Table 9: False Friends in Legal Contexts

| English Term | Italian False Friend | Correct Italian Legal Term | Explanation |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------------|-------------|
| Action (legal) | Azione (can be correct but limited) | Causa / Procedimento / Azione | "Azione" works in civil procedure ("azione di risarcimento") but "azione" alone is too general. |
| Attorney | Attuario (= actuary) | Avvocato / Procuratore | "Avvocato" for attorney-at-law. "Procuratore" is a specific role (procuratore legale). "Attorney" in US = "avvocato". |
| Sentence (criminal) | Sentenza (= any judgment) | Condanna / Pena | "Sentenza" is any judgment. For criminal punishment, use "condanna" or "pena". |
| Liquidated damages | Danni liquidati (= established damages) | Clausola penale | "Danni liquidati" is a descriptive phrase. The correct legal institution is "clausola penale" (Art. 1382 cc). |
| Mortgage | Mutuo (= loan) | Ipoteca | "Mutuo" is the loan agreement. "Ipoteca" is the security interest. |
| Solicitor | Sollecitatore (= one who solicits) | Avvocato / Procuratore legale | "Solicitor" is best kept in English with explanation: "solicitor (avvocato inglese che svolge attivita di consulenza)." |
| Magistrate | Magistrato (can be correct) | Giudice / Magistrato | "Magistrato" is correct in Italy (public prosecutor is "magistrato requirente"). |
| Ordinance | Ordinanza (= court order) | Regolamento / Ordinanza comunale | For municipal legislation, use "regolamento comunale". "Ordinanza" is a specific administrative act. |

### Table 10: Legal Citation Format

| English Citation | Italian Equivalent | Rule |
|-----------------|-------------------|------|
| Article 2043 of the Italian Civil Code | Art. 2043 c.c. / Articolo 2043 del Codice Civile | Abbreviated in footnotes: "Art. 2043 c.c." Full name in running text. |
| Section 5(2) of the Companies Act 2006 | Art. 5, comma 2, del Companies Act 2006 | For foreign statutes, keep original title, add "del" in Italian text. |
| 123 BVerfGE 45 (German court) | BVerfG, sentenza n. 123 del 2005 | Foreign court decisions: keep original case number, add Italian descriptive terms. |
| Roe v. Wade, 410 U.S. 113 (1973) | Roe c. Wade, 410 U.S. 113 (1973) | "c." (contro) for "v." or "vs." Keep US citation format intact, only translate "v." to "c." |
| Smith v. Jones [2020] EWCA Civ 123 | Smith c. Jones [2020] EWCA Civ 123 | Neutral citation kept in original. "c." replaces "v." in Italian contexts. |
| Article 1(2)(a) | Articolo 1, comma 2, lettera a) | Italian subdivision: "comma" for subsection, "lettera" for subparagraph. |
| paragraph 3 | paragrafo 3 / comma 3 | "Paragrafo" for larger subdivisions; "comma" for numbered subsections within an article. |

| English Usage | Italian Equivalent | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------|-------|

### Italian Legal Translation Conventions

| Convention | Rule | Example |
|------------|------|---------|
| Gender of legal person | Company is feminine: "la Societa" | "La Societa ha sede in Milano." |
| Legal entity abbreviations | S.p.A., S.r.l., S.n.c., S.a.s. | Write without spaces between letters: "S.p.A." not "S. p. A." |
| Article references | Use "Art." with capital A, then number | "Art. 2043 c.c." in footnotes; "Articolo 2043" in running text. |
| Comma subdivision | "comma" for numbered subsection | "comma 2" not "sezione 2". |
| Court name capitalization | Capitalize court names | "Tribunale di Milano", "Corte d'Appello di Roma". |
| Citation of laws | D.Lgs. = Decreto Legislativo; L. = Legge; D.P.R. = Decreto del Presidente della Repubblica | "D.Lgs. 81/2008" not "Decreto Legislativo 81 del 2008" in abbreviated form. |
| Notarial authentication | Use "copia conforme" format | "La presente copia conforme all'originale composta di X pagine." |

### Verification Checklist for Legal Translations

1. **Codice Civile accuracy**: All Italian legal terms traceable to Codice Civile provisions.
2. **Entity names preserved**: Foreign company names and entity types are kept, with Italian equivalents glossed.
3. **Defined terms consistency**: Every defined term (capitalized in EN) mapped to same IT term throughout.
4. **Boilerplate adequacy**: Standard clauses translated to functionally equivalent Italian formulas.
5. **False friends eliminated**: No "attualmente" for "actually", "eventualmente" for "eventually", "sensibile" for "sensitive", etc.
6. **Citation format correct**: Article/comma/lettera structure used for Italian legal references.
7. **Cultural equivalence signposted**: Non-equivalent institutions (trust, discovery, consideration) flagged with explanatory gloss.
8. **Register consistency**: Formal register maintained throughout; "il presente" used for "this" when referring to the document.
9. **Jurisdiction-aware**: Governing law clause correctly translated based on the source legal system.
10. **Notarial/certification correct**: Where applicable, translation includes appropriate certification language for Italian use.
