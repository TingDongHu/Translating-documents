# Domain: Legal (Persian — FA)

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Iranian lawyers (وکلا), judges (قضات), legal advisors (مشاوران حقوقی), noters (دفاتر اسناد رسمی)
- **Secondary Reader**: Corporate legal counsel, contract managers, human resources specialists
- **Tertiary Reader**: Courts (دادگاه‌ها), arbitration tribunals (هیئت‌های داوری), enforcement offices (ادارات اجرای احکام)

### Reader Expectations
1. **Terminological Precision**: Every legal term must use its codified equivalent from Iranian law; near-synonyms must not be confused.
2. **Iranian Civil Code (قانون مدنی) Compliance**: Contract law terminology must adhere to the Civil Code provisions.
3. **Islamic Jurisprudence (فقه اسلامی)**: Many Iranian legal concepts derive from Shia Islamic jurisprudence; terms like شرعی (shar'ī), فقهی (faqihī) carry specific legal weight.
4. **Official Correspondence Format**: Petitions (دادخواست), contracts (قرارداد), notices (اخطاریه) follow specific templates.
5. **French Legal Influence**: Iranian civil law was heavily influenced by the French Civil Code; many legal terms have French-origin equivalents still in use.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Legal terminology accuracy | Critical | Legal invalidity, case loss |
| Civil Code reference alignment | Critical | Contractual dispute |
| Official correspondence format | High | Document rejection |
| Islamic jurisprudence terms | High | Misinterpretation, religious offense |
| SOV sentence structure | Medium | Unnatural text |
| French-origin term recognition | Medium | Confusion for trained lawyers |

### Table L7: Common Translation Pitfalls

| English Source | Incorrect Persian | Correct Persian | Warning |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------|
| "Legal entity" | شخص حقوقی | شخص حقوقی (correct) — verify context for "legal person" vs "legal entity" | "شخص حقیقی" = natural person |
| "Shall" (future tense) | خواهد شد | باید / مکلف است (for obligation) | "Shall" in legal English = obligation, not future |
| "Herein" | در اینجا | در این قرارداد / در این سند | Context-specific translation |
| "Force majeure" | قوای قاهره (plural) | قوه قاهره (singular) | Persian uses singular form |
| "Consideration" | ملاحظه | عوض | "Consideration" in contract law ≠ "consideration" in general English |
| "Execution" (of contract) | اعدام / اجرا | اجرای قرارداد | "Execution" ≠ "اعدام" (execution as in death penalty) |
| "Party" (to contract) | حزب (political party) | طرف قرارداد | "Party" in legal context ≠ political party |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### A. Iranian Legal System Overview
The Iranian legal system is a hybrid of:
1. **Civil law** (derived from the French Civil Code, introduced in the early 20th century).
2. **Islamic law** (Shia Jafari jurisprudence, the supreme source of law since 1979).
3. **Customary law** (tribal and local customs, particularly in family and property matters).

The Constitution (قانون اساسی) is the supreme law. The Guardian Council (شورای نگهبان) reviews legislation for compatibility with Islamic law and the Constitution.

### C. Translation Quality Markers
- **Acceptable**: Uses established Persian legal terminology; follows SOV order in legal sentences; correctly distinguishes between قرارداد (contract) and عقد (formal agreement).
- **Unacceptable**: Uses Arabic legal terms not used in Iranian law; translates "shall" as future tense instead of obligation; confuses حزب (political party) with طرف (contractual party).
- **Review Required**: Ambiguous terms that could be interpreted in multiple legal contexts; terms that differ between civil and criminal law; Islamic law terms with contested interpretations.
