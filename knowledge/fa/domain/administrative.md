# Domain: Administrative (Persian — FA)

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Iranian government officials (مدیران دولتی), administrative staff (کارکنان اداری), civil servants (کارمندان دولت)
- **Secondary Reader**: Municipal officers (شهرداران), provincial governors (استانداران), ministry directors (مدیران وزارتخانه‌ها)
- **Tertiary Reader**: Citizens interacting with government services, foreign diplomats, international organizations

### Reader Expectations
1. **Official Register**: Government correspondence uses a highly formal, structured register with specific templates and formulas.
2. **Bureaucratic Precision**: Administrative documents must follow prescribed formats; deviation may render documents invalid.
3. **Hierarchical Awareness**: Correspondence must properly address officials by rank and title.
4. **Legal Compliance**: Administrative decisions must cite relevant legislation and regulations.
5. **Persian Formal Language**: Use of formal Persian vocabulary and constructions, avoiding colloquialisms.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Official format compliance | Critical | Document rejection |
| Title and rank accuracy | Critical | Diplomatic/protocol offense |
| Legal citation accuracy | High | Administrative invalidity |
| Formal register maintenance | High | Loss of authority |
| Hierarchical protocol | High | Protocol violation |
| Consistent terminology | Medium | Confusion |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### A. Iranian Administrative System Overview
Iran's administrative system is characterized by:
1. **Centralized bureaucracy**: Government agencies follow hierarchical structures with clear chains of command.
2. **Legal framework**: Administrative procedures are governed by the Civil Service Management Law (قانون مدیریت خدمات کشوری).
3. **Calendar system**: All official dates use the Solar Hijri calendar (تقویم هجری شمسی).
4. **Official language**: Persian (Farsi) is the sole official language of administration.
5. **Seal and signature**: Official documents require organizational seals (مهر) and authorized signatures.

### B. Key Administrative Laws

| English | Persian | Scope |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Civil Service Management Law | قانون مدیریت خدمات کشوری | Public sector employment |
| Municipal Law | قانون شهرداری | Municipal governance |
| Provincial Governance Law | قانون تعاریف و وظایف و اختیارات استاندار | Provincial administration |
| Public Procurement Law | قانون برگزاری مناقصات | Government procurement |
| Access to Information Law | قانون انتشار و دسترسی آزاد به اطلاعات | Public information access |
| Official Holidays Law | قانون رسمی تعطیلات | Government holidays |

### D. Translation Quality Markers
- **Acceptable**: Uses official government titles correctly; follows نامه اداری format; uses Solar Hijri dates; maintains formal register throughout.
- **Unacceptable**: Uses informal register in official correspondence; mixes Solar Hijri and Gregorian dates without clarification; uses colloquial titles instead of official ranks.
- **Review Required**: Ambiguous title references; documents crossing multiple jurisdictions; correspondence involving international parties.
