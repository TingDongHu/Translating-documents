# Domain: Commercial (Persian — FA)

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Iranian business executives (مدیران اجرایی), trade specialists (متخصصان بازرگانی), marketing professionals (متخصصان بازاریابی)
- **Secondary Reader**: Chamber of Commerce members (اعضای اتاق بازرگانی), import/export operators (operators واردات/صادرات), procurement managers (مدیران خرید)
- **Tertiary Reader**: Customs officials (گمرک), trade regulators ( تنیم‌کنندگان تجاری), business lawyers (وکلای تجاری)

### Reader Expectations
1. **Professional Register**: Commercial documents use a semi-formal to formal register depending on context; marketing materials may use a more engaging, persuasive tone.
2. **Company Type Awareness**: Iranian commercial law recognizes specific company structures (شرکت سهامی, شرکت با مسئولیت محدود, etc.).
3. **Trade Terminology**: Import/export documents must use precise customs and trade terminology recognized by Iranian customs (گمرک ایران).
4. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Business correspondence must respect Iranian cultural norms around hospitality, indirectness, and relationship-building.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Commercial terminology accuracy | Critical | Transaction failure |
| Company type classification | Critical | Legal misclassification |
| Trade/customs terminology | High | Customs rejection |
| Cultural register appropriateness | High | Relationship damage |
| Professional formatting | Medium | Loss of credibility |
| Consistent brand terminology | Medium | Confusion |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### A. Iranian Commercial Landscape
Iran's commercial environment is shaped by:
1. **Sanctions impact**: International sanctions have limited Iran's integration into global trade, creating a dual economy (formal/informal channels).
2. **Free trade zones**: Iran has several free trade/industrial zones (Kish, Qeshm, Chabahar, etc.) with special commercial regulations.
3. **State-owned enterprises**: Significant state involvement in key sectors (oil, gas, banking, telecommunications).
4. **Private sector growth**: Growing private sector, particularly in technology, manufacturing, and services.

### B. Key Commercial Institutions

| English | Persian | Role |
|---------|---------|------|
| Iran Chamber of Commerce | اتاق بازرگانی ایران | Business advocacy |
| Iran Chamber of Industries | اتاق صنایع و معادن | Industrial advocacy |
| Iran Customs Administration | گمرک جمهوری اسلامی ایران | Trade facilitation |
| Organization for Investment | سازمان سرمایه‌گذاری خارجی | Foreign investment |
| Trade Promotion Organization | سازمان توسعه تجارت ایران | Export promotion |

### C. Business Etiquette Notes for Translation
- Iranian business culture emphasizes **relationship-building** before transactions; correspondence should be warm and personal.
- **Hospitality** is important; business letters may include well-wishes and pleasantries.
- **Indirect communication** is common; direct refusals are avoided in favor of softer expressions.
- **Hierarchy** matters; address senior people with appropriate titles.
- **Time flexibility**: Iranian business culture may have a different relationship with deadlines compared to Western cultures; translation should convey the intended urgency without sounding harsh.
