# Domain: Financial (Persian — FA)

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Iranian financial analysts, bank officers (کارمندان بانک), auditors (حسابرسان), investment advisors (مشاوران سرمایه‌گذاری)
- **Secondary Reader**: Central Bank of Iran (بانک مرکزی ایران) officials, stock exchange (بورس) participants, insurance professionals
- **Tertiary Reader**: Corporate CFOs, treasury departments, tax authorities (سازمان امور مالیاتی)

### Reader Expectations
1. **Terminological Precision**: Financial terms must use the officially recognized Persian equivalents from the Central Bank of Iran and Iran's financial regulations.
2. **Currency Handling**: Accurate rial/toman distinction; proper conversion references; awareness of sanctions-related financial terminology.
3. **Regulatory Compliance**: Financial documents must comply with Iranian banking regulations (مقررات بانکی) and accounting standards (استانداردهای حسابداری ایران).
4. **Numerical Accuracy**: Financial figures must be precisely rendered; no rounding errors; consistent decimal/thousands formatting.
5. **International Context**: Awareness of international financial terminology alongside Iranian equivalents.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Financial terminology accuracy | Critical | Misunderstanding, regulatory violation |
| Currency format (rial/toman) | Critical | Financial miscalculation |
| Numerical precision | Critical | Quantitative errors |
| Regulatory compliance | High | Legal exposure |
| Sanctions-related terms | High | Political/legal sensitivity |
| Accounting standard alignment | High | Audit failure |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### A. Iranian Financial System Overview
Iran's financial system is characterized by:
1. **Dual banking system**: A mix of state-owned and private banks, overseen by the Central Bank of Iran.
2. **Interest-free banking (بانکداری بدون بهره)**: Islamic banking principles applied, though in practice many operations mirror conventional banking.
3. **Sanctions environment**: International sanctions have significantly impacted Iran's financial sector, creating unique terminology around compliance, workarounds, and alternative payment systems.
4. **Currency volatility**: The rial/toman dual system and exchange rate fluctuations require careful handling in financial documents.

### B. Key Regulatory Bodies

| English | Persian | Role |
|---------|---------|------|
| Central Bank of Iran | بانک مرکزی ایران | Monetary policy, banking regulation |
| Securities and Exchange Organization | سازمان بورس و اوراق بهادار | Capital market regulation |
| Tax Authority | سازمان امور مالیاتی کشور | Tax collection and administration |
| Insurance Regulatory Authority | بیمه مرکزی ایران | Insurance regulation |
| Supreme Audit Court | دیوان محاسبات کشور | Government financial oversight |

### C. Translation Quality Markers
- **Acceptable**: Correctly distinguishes بهره (loan interest) from سود (profit/dividend); uses ریال in financial statements; accurately renders numerical values with proper formatting.
- **Unacceptable**: Confuses ریال and تومان without clarification; translates "interest" ambiguously; uses Arabic financial terms not used in Iranian Persian.
- **Review Required**: Sanctions-related terminology (politically sensitive); terms that differ between Islamic and conventional banking; numerical values involving large amounts.
