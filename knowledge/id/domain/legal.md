# Domain: Legal (Hukum) — Indonesian

## 1. Reader Model: How to Read Indonesian Legal Documents

### 1.1 Macro-Level Architecture

Indonesian legal documents follow a rigidly prescribed structure derived from Dutch colonial civil law (Roman-Dutch tradition) layered with post-independence statutory reforms. The primary codification is the **Kitab Undang-Undang Hukum Perdata (KUHPerdata)** — the Indonesian Civil Code, itself a direct translation of the *Burgerlijk Wetboek* (BW) of the Netherlands.

The typical document hierarchy for legislation (Undang-Undang):

```
    [Judul / Title] — Format: "Undang-Undang Nomor X Tahun YYYY tentang [Subjek]"
        → [Menimbang / Considering] — Recitals: factual and legal considerations
            → [Mengingat / In View Of] — Legal basis: constitutional and statutory references
                → [Dengan Persetujuan Bersama / With Joint Approval] — DPR + President formula
                    → [Pasal 1 / Article 1] — Ketentuan Umum (General Provisions): definitions
                        → [Pasal 2–N / Articles 2–N] — Materi Pokok (Main Substance)
                            → [Pasal N+1 / Article N+1] — Ketentuan Pidana (Criminal Provisions, if any)
                                → [Pasal N+2 / Article N+2] — Ketentuan Peralihan (Transitional Provisions)
                                    → [Pasal N+3 / Article N+3] — Ketentuan Penutup (Closing Provisions)
                                        → [Tanda Tangan / Signatures] — President + counter-signatures
                                            → [Penjelasan / Elucidation] — Official commentary (not always present)
```

For contracts (Perjanjian):

```
    [Judul Perjanjian / Contract Title]
        → [Pembukaan / Preamble / Recitals] — Para Pihak (Parties), Latar Belakang (Background)
            → [Pasal 1 / Article 1] — Definisi (Definitions)
                → [Pasal 2–N / Articles 2–N] — Hak dan Kewajiban (Rights and Obligations)
                    → [Pasal N+1 / Article N+1] — Jangka Waktu (Term / Duration)
                        → [Pasal N+2 / Article N+2] — Harga dan Pembayaran (Price and Payment)
                            → [Pasal N+3 / Article N+3] — Keadaan Kahar (Force Majeure)
                                → [Pasal N+4 / Article N+4] — Penyelesaian Sengketa (Dispute Resolution)
                                    → [Pasal N+5 / Article N+5] — Hukum yang Berlaku (Governing Law)
                                        → [Pasal N+6 / Article N+6] — Penutup (Closing)
                                            → [Tanda Tangan / Signatures] — Materai (stamp duty)
```

### 1.2 Meso-Level Patterns

**Move 1: Preamble/Recitals (Menimbang/Mengingat)**
- Formulaic language, highly templated
- Each recital begins with "bahwa" (that): "Menimbang bahwa..." → "Considering that..."
- "Mengingat:" followed by numbered legal bases: "1. Pasal ... Undang-Undang Dasar Negara Republik Indonesia Tahun 1945"

**Move 2: Normative Sentences (Norma Hukum)**
- Four types:
  - *Larangan* (Prohibition): "Setiap orang dilarang..." → "Every person is prohibited from..."
  - *Perintah* (Command): "Setiap orang wajib..." → "Every person shall..."
  - *Kebolehan* (Permission): "Dapat..." → "May..."
  - *Pengaturan* (Regulation): "Diatur dengan..." → "Shall be regulated by..."

**Move 3: Definition Clauses (Ketentuan Umum)**
- "Yang dimaksud dengan [...] adalah [...]" → "What is meant by [...] is [...]"
- Always in Article 1 of legislation
- Each defined term numbered: "1. [...] 2. [...] 3. [...]"

### 1.3 Micro-Level Patterns

**Deeply nested sub-articles:**
```
Pasal 5
(1) ... [paragraph]
    a. ... [sub-paragraph]
        i. ... [item]
            aa. ... [sub-item]
```

Translation posture: Flatten nested structures where English convention differs (use (i), (ii), (iii) or (a), (b), (c) as appropriate per style guide).

**Materai (Stamp Duty):**
- Documents are stamped with materai elektronik (e-meterai) or materai tempel (physical stamp)
- Current rate: Rp 10,000
- Translation note: Note "dibubuhi materai" / "bermaterai cukup" → "duly stamped"

## 3. Standards

### 3.1 The Indonesian Legal System — Core Sources

1. **Undang-Undang Dasar Negara Republik Indonesia Tahun 1945 (UUD 1945)** — Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia
   - Original enactment: 1945
   - Amendments: 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002 (four amendments)

2. **Kitab Undang-Undang Hukum Perdata (KUHPerdata)** — Indonesian Civil Code
   - Dutch original: *Burgerlijk Wetboek* (1847)
   - Applies to all Indonesian citizens (with customary law exceptions for certain groups)
   - Structure: Four Books
     - Book I: Van Personen (Perihal Orang) — Persons and Family Law
     - Book II: Van Zaken (Perihal Benda) — Property
     - Book III: Van Verbintenissen (Perihal Perikatan) — Obligations/Contracts
     - Book IV: Van Bewijs en Verjaring (Perihal Pembuktian dan Daluwarsa) — Evidence and Prescription

3. **Kitab Undang-Undang Hukum Pidana (KUHP)** — Criminal Code
   - Reformed in 2023 (UU No. 1/2023); effective 2026

4. **Kitab Undang-Undang Hukum Dagang (KUHD)** — Commercial Code
   - *Wetboek van Koophandel* (1847)
   - Covers: merchants, shipping, insurance, negotiable instruments

### 3.2 Hierarchy of Regulations (Hierarki Peraturan Perundang-undangan)

Per UU No. 12/2011 as amended by UU No. 15/2019:

| Level | Instrument | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | UUD 1945 (Constitution) | People/Sovereign |
| 2 | Tap MPR (MPR Decrees) | People's Consultative Assembly |
| 3 | UU/Perppu (Laws/Government Regs in Lieu of Law) | DPR + President / President |
| 4 | PP (Government Regulations) | President |
| 5 | Perpres (Presidential Regulations) | President |
| 6 | Perda Provinsi (Provincial Regulations) | Provincial DPRD + Governor |
| 7 | Perda Kabupaten/Kota (Regency/City Regulations) | Regency/City DPRD + Mayor/Regent |

### 3.3 Legal Translation Conventions

- **Gender neutrality:** Indonesian does not mark gender. In English legal translation, use "the Party," "the Person," "such person" — avoid "he/she" constructions. Prefer "they" for singular antecedent where natural.
- **Capitalization:** Follow English legal convention: defined terms capitalized ("the Seller," "the Buyer," "the Agreement").
- **Serial comma (Oxford comma):** Use consistently in legal translations for clarity, especially in definition clauses.
- **Latin maxims:** Latin legal phrases common in Indonesian (e.g., *ex aequo et bono*, *audi et alteram partem*, *lex specialis derogat legi generali*) should be preserved in Latin with an English gloss.
- **Pasal references:** "Pasal 5 ayat (2) huruf a" → "Article 5 paragraph (2) letter a" or "Article 5(2)(a)"
- **Defined terms in contracts:** Indonesian often writes "selanjutnya disebut" → "hereinafter referred to as" — preserve this formality.
