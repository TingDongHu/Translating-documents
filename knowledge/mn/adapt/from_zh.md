## Word Order Restructuring: SVO → SOV

This is the single most important adaptation rule. Chinese SVO word order must be restructured to Mongolian SOV.

```
Chinese (SVO):    我们 (S) 生产 (V) 果汁 (O)
Mongolian (SOV):  Бид (S) жимсний шүүс (O) үйлдвэрлэдэг (V)
```

### Patterns requiring restructuring:

1. **Simple SVO** → SOV: Move verb to clause end
2. **Serial verb constructions** → Use converbs (-ж/-ч, -аад/-ээд) or connect with бөгөөд
3. **Topic-comment** → Subject + Object + [modifiers] + Verb
4. **Condition-first (如果...就)** → Use -вал/-вэл suffix on condition verb
5. **Long comma-separated parallel clauses** → Break into 2-3 Mongolian sentences with explicit connectors

## Formality and Address

- Chinese 您 (formal "you") → Mongolian Та (formal second person pronoun)
- Chinese formal register → Mongolian formal register (use predicative markers: юм, байна, болно)
- Chinese official closings → Mongolian: Хүндэтгэсэн (Respectfully)
- Topic marker: Chinese uses word order; Mongolian uses нь/бол after the subject

## Number and Date Conventions

- Chinese 万 (10,000) → Convert to numeric: 35万 → 350,000 (or 350 000 in Mongolian)
- Chinese 亿 (100,000,000) → Convert to numeric: 35亿 → 3,500,000,000 (or 3.5 тэрбум)
- Chinese 年/月/日 → Mongolian: YYYY оны MM сарын DD өдөр (formal) or YYYY.MM.DD (business)
- Year references: 1998年 → 1998 онд (dative-locative for "in 1998")

## Chinese Measure Words → Mongolian

Mongolian has NO systematic classifier/measure word system. Simply use: **Numeral + Noun**.

| Chinese | Mongolian | Note |
|---------|-----------|------|
| 三个品牌 | гурван брэнд | No classifier needed |
| 一条生产线 | нэг үйлдвэрлэлийн шүүс | No classifier |
| 一种果汁 | нэг төрлийн жүүс | Add төрөл only if "type/kind" is semantically important |
| 多项认证 | олон баталгаажуулалт | Plural form, no classifier |
| 一家公司 | нэг компани | No classifier |

## Cultural Adaptation Notes

### Concepts to Contextualize
- **百年企业 (100-year enterprise):** The Chinese aspiration toward century-old brands may need framing — use "урт хугацааны" (long-term) rather than literal "100 жилийн" if it feels forced
- **让中国人喝上更好的果汁:** If targeting Mongolian market, consider adapting "Хятад хүн" → broader "хүмүүс" or "хэрэглэгчид" depending on target audience
- **匠人 (craftsman):** Translates well — "гар урчууд" carries positive connotations in Mongolian culture
- **健康中国:** This is a Chinese government initiative; add brief context on first mention

### Government/Institutional References
- Chinese provincial/city names: use standard Cyrillic transliterations (浙江 → Жөжян, 杭州 → Ханжоу)
- Chinese government entities: translate function, keep Chinese name in parentheses if needed
- Awards and certifications: translate the meaning, keep the original name in parentheses

## Structural Adaptation Checklist

When adapting Chinese text to Mongolian:

1. ☐ Restructure all SVO clauses to SOV
2. ☐ Add case suffixes to all nouns except indefinite objects
3. ☐ Mark definite objects with accusative (-ыг/-ийг/-г)
4. ☐ Replace all Chinese punctuation with Cyrillic/Mongolian equivalents  
5. ☐ Convert 万/亿 to numeric values
6. ☐ Convert dates from YYYY年MM月DD日 to Mongolian format
7. ☐ Translate 成语 by meaning, not literally
8. ☐ Transliterate proper nouns from Pinyin to Cyrillic
9. ☐ Add бөгөөд, ба, болон for parallel clause connections
10. ☐ Add predicative markers (юм, байна, болно) at sentence endings
11. ☐ Keep Latin acronyms and IMAGE tags as-is
12. ☐ Ensure vowel harmony in native Mongolian words (loanwords exempt)

## Space Planning

Mongolian translations typically expand 30-50% vs. Chinese source. For PPT/visual documents:
- Plan for text expansion in layout
- Use abbreviations where conventionally accepted
- Mongolian allows shorter versions by dropping some predicative markers in bullet points
