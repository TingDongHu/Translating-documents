## General Principles

- Mongolian business writing is more direct than Chinese — less ornate, more factual
- Stronger emphasis on certifications, concrete achievements, and numbers
- Company introductions follow a standard structure: name/founding → scope → products → capacity → quality → market position → contact
- Brand names: keep Latin form + add Cyrillic transliteration on first mention
- Marketing content: localize for Mongolian cultural context, not just translate

## Company Introduction Structure

Standard Mongolian company profile order:
1. Компанийн нэр, байгуулагдсан он (Company name, founding year)
2. Үйл ажиллагааны чиглэл (Business scope)
3. Гол бүтээгдэхүүн, үйлчилгээ (Main products/services)
4. Үйлдвэрлэлийн хүчин чадал (Production capacity)
5. Чанарын баталгаажуулалт, гэрчилгээ (Quality, certifications)
6. Зах зээл дэх байр суурь (Market position)
7. Холбоо барих мэдээлэл (Contact information)

## Development Timeline

For company history/timeline sections:
- Year format: `1998 онд` (dative-locative case, meaning "in 1998")
- Milestone structure: `[Year] онд — [Event description]`
- Verb form: use past tense suffix -сан/-сэн/-сон/-сөн or -в for completed events

## Brand and Product Names

### Brand Transliteration Rules

Chinese brand names → Mongolian Cyrillic (via Pinyin):
- 乐源 (Lèyuán) → Лө Юань (keep "Leyuan" Latin form as well)
- 零度果坊 (Língdù Guǒfáng) → Линдү Гуо Фан
- 菓蔬秘诀 (Guǒshū Mìjué) → Гуо Шү Ми Жүэ

## Advertising and Marketing Copy

### Core Principles
- Mongolian advertising is typically more direct and less hyperbolic than Chinese
- Slogans: recreate the impact in Mongolian, do not translate literally
- Keep brand names in their original/transliterated form
- Product names: transliterate unique names; translate descriptive names

## Business Culture Notes

- Mongolian business partners value directness more than Chinese style
- Numbers and certifications carry significant weight in business credibility
- "百年企业" (100-year enterprise) concept may need contextual framing for Mongolian readers
- Chinese government certifications (省级、国家级) should include brief explanation of significance
- "匠人" (craftsman) resonates well with Mongolian cultural appreciation for craftsmanship
