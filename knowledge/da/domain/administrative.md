# Administrative Domain: Danish Translation Standards

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** Danish civil servant (embedsmand/ansat i det offentlige), municipal officer (kommunemedarbejder), or ministry official (ministeriumsmedarbejder). The reader expects formal administrative language (forvaltningssprog) that is clear, precise, and follows established Danish government conventions. They are familiar with the Danish parliamentary system, municipal governance, and EU administrative frameworks.

**Secondary Reader:** Citizen (borger) or business reading government correspondence, permits, or regulatory documents. They expect clear, accessible Danish that explains administrative processes without unnecessary jargon.

**Tertiary Reader:** International organisation or foreign government official reading Danish administrative documents. Consistency in administrative terminology and institutional naming is essential.

**Reader Priorities:**
1. Formal but accessible Danish administrative register
2. Correct institutional naming (ministerier, styrelser, kommuner)
3. Precise administrative terminology (not colloquial alternatives)
4. Consistent use of De-address in formal correspondence
5. Correct formatting for official documents (referencer, journalnumre)
6. Appropriate passive voice usage (common in administrative Danish)

## Error Pattern Library

### Common Translation Errors

| Error Type | Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Ministry name | Literal translation | Established Danish names | Use official Danish ministry names |
| Register | Informal tone in official letters | Formal De-address | Government correspondence requires formal register |
| Passive voice | Overly active (American style) | Appropriate passive | Administrative Danish uses passive voice naturally |
| Term | "lov" (law) when meaning "regulation" | "bekendtgørelse" | "lov" = statute; "bekendtgørelse" = regulation |
| Abbreviation | Translated abbreviations | Preserve Danish abbreviations | CVR, CPR, etc. are standard |
| Date | "Effective from January 15" | "Træder i kraft den 15. januar" | Use standard Danish administrative phrasing |
| Reference | Missing reference formatting | "J.nr. XX-XXXX" | Official documents need proper reference numbers |

### Ambiguous Terms Requiring Context

| English | Danish Options | When to Use |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| "authority" | "myndighed" (government) / "organ" (organisational) | "myndighed" for government bodies; "organ" for EU bodies |
| "ministry" | "ministerium" (national) / "forvaltning" (municipal) | "ministerium" for national; "forvaltning" for local |
| "department" | "afdeling" (organisational) / "ministerium" (government) | Context-dependent |
| "regulation" | "bekendtgørelse" (Danish) / "forordning" (EU) | "bekendtgørelse" for national; "forordning" for EU |
| "guidance" | "vejledning" (non-binding) / "retningslinje" (policy) | "vejledning" for guidance; "retningslinje" for policy |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### Danish Administrative Law Framework

**Forvaltningsloven (Administration Act):** The primary statute governing administrative procedure:
- § 2: Duty to investigate (undersøgelsespligt)
- § 3: Reasoned decisions (begrundelsespligt)
- § 7: Duty to advise (vejledningspligt)
- § 19: Hearing (høring)
- § 21-26: Appeals (klageadgang)

**Offentlighedsloven (Freedom of Information Act):** Governs public access to documents:
- § 3: Right of access (aktindsigt)
- § 7: Exemptions from access

**Data Protection:** Danish implementation of GDPR via Datatilsynet (DPA).

### Municipal Governance Structure

Danish municipalities (kommuner) operate under:
- **Kommunalbestyrelse** (municipal council) — elected body
- **Borgmester** (mayor) — head of council
- **Magistraten** (magistrate) — executive body in larger municipalities
- **Forvaltninger** (administrative departments) — led by direktører

**Regions** (5 regions) handle healthcare and regional development:
- Region Hovedstaden
- Region Sjælland
- Region Syddanmark
- Region Midtjylland
- Region Nordjylland
