# Adaptation to Norwegian from English (en)

## 1. Formality and Register

| English | Norwegian (Bokmal) | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| You | du / deg / din | Universal — Norway completed du-reformen in 1970s. NEVER use "De". |
| Dear Mr. Smith | Vaer sa venlig, hr. Smith | Standard formal opening |
| Dear Ms. Smith | Vaer sa venlig, fru Smith | "Froenn" is outdated; use "fru" |
| Dear Sir/Madam | Vaer sa venlig | Standard |
| To whom it may concern | Til den det ma anga | Standard |
| Sincerely | Med vennlig hilsen | Standard closing |
| Best regards | Med vennlig hilsen | Same as "Sincerely" |
| Kind regards | Vennlig hilsen | Slightly less formal |
| Hello / Hi | Hei | Informal (use for "du" contexts) |
| Hey | Hei / Hallo | Very informal |
| Please | Vaer sa snill / Vennligst | "Vennligst" more formal |
| Thank you | Takk / Tusen takk | "Tusen takk" more emphatic |
| You're welcome | Varsa god | Standard response |
| Excuse me | Unnskyld | For apologies or getting attention |
| I am writing to | Jeg skriver til / Jeg henvender meg til | Formal letter opening |
| We are pleased to | Vi har gleden av a | Formal business phrase |
| I look forward to | Jeg gleder meg til / Jeg ser frem til | "Gleder meg" more natural |
| Please do not hesitate to | Ta gjerne kontakt | More natural than literal translation |
| Could you please | Kunne du vaere sa snill a / Kan du | Polite request |
| We regret to inform you | Vi beklager a ma informere deg | Formal bad news |

### 1.1 Germanic Register Alignment

English and Norwegian share Germanic roots, which creates natural alignment in many areas:

| Feature | English | Norwegian | Alignment |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Sentence structure | SVO | SVO | Same word order |
| Compound words | Limited | Extensive | Norwegian compounds freely |
| Articles | "the", "a/an" | "en/et" (indef.), suffixed (def.) | Different mechanism |
| Possessive | "my book" | "boka mi" | Possessive follows noun |
| Adjective position | "the red book" | "den rode boka" | Before noun, with agreement |
| Negation | "not" | "ikke" | Same position (after verb) |
| Questions | Inversion | Inversion | Same mechanism |

### 1.2 Business Correspondence Patterns

| English Pattern | Norwegian Pattern | Notes |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| "Dear John," | "Hei John," | First name basis is standard |
| "I hope this email finds you well" | (omit) | Norwegians skip pleasantries |
| "Please find attached" | "Vedlagt" | One word suffices |
| "Looking forward to hearing from you" | "Ser frem til svar" | Shorter |
| "Please do not hesitate to contact us" | "Ta gjerne kontakt" | More natural |
| "We would like to inform you" | "Vi onsker a informere deg" | Direct |
| "Thank you for your prompt response" | "Takk for raskt svar" | Simple |
| "As per our conversation" | "Som avtalt" / "I henhold til var samtale" | "Som avtalt" if agreement |
| "With reference to" | "I henhold til" / "Vedrørende" | Standard |
| "In reply to your letter" | "I svar pa deres brev" | Formal |

## 2. Dates, Numbers, and Currency

| English | Norwegian | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| May 25, 2024 | 25.05.2024 | DD.MM.YYYY — NOT MM/DD/YYYY |
| 05/25/2024 | 25/05/2024 | Swap month and day |
| May 2024 | mai 2024 | Month in lowercase |
| the 1st of May | 1. mai | Ordinal with period |
| the 2nd of May | 2. mai | Ordinal with period |
| 10:00 AM | 10:00 | 24-hour format; no AM/PM |
| 3:30 PM | 15:30 | Convert to 24-hour |
| 12:00 PM / noon | 12:00 / middag | "Middag" for noon |
| 12:00 AM / midnight | 00:00 / midnatt | "Midnatt" for midnight |
| 10:45 AM | 10:45 | No AM/PM |
| 2 hours | 2 timer | "Timer" is plural of "time" |
| $1,000.50 | 1 000,50 $ | Space thousands, comma decimal, symbol after |
| $1.5 million | 1,5 millioner dollar | Comma decimal, "millioner" plural |
| 1,000 | 1 000 | Space separator, not comma |
| 1,000,000 | 1 000 000 | Space separator |
| 0.5 | 0,5 | Decimal comma |
| 2.5% | 2,5 % | Decimal comma, space before % |
| 10 °C | 10 °C | Space between number and symbol |
| 10 x 20 cm | 10 x 20 cm | Same |

### 2.1 Number Reading in Text

| English | Norwegian | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| "one hundred" | "hundre" | No "ett" before "hundre" |
| "one thousand" | "tusen" | No "ett" before "tusen" |
| "twenty-one" | "tjueen" | Compound number |
| "one hundred and twenty" | "hundre og tjue" | "og" between hundreds and tens |
| "first" | "forst" | Ordinal |
| "second" | "annen" / "andre" | Ordinal |
| "third" | "tredje" | Ordinal |

## 6. Punctuation and Formatting

| English | Norwegian | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| "Hello" | "Hei" | Same straight quotes |
| 'Hello' | 'Hei' | Same single quotes |
| «Hello» | «Hei» | Guillemets in formal/academic texts |
