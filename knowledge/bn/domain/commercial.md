## Reader Model

**Who reads this document and what do they expect?**

Commercial document readers in Bengali are business owners, managers, procurement officers, trade professionals, and corporate secretaries. They read the document to understand business relationships, terms of trade, and corporate obligations. They expect:

- **Professional clarity** — business language must be clear, direct, and free of ambiguity.
- **Consistent terminology** — the same business concept must use the same Bengali term throughout.
- **Appropriate register** — formal but accessible. Not legalistic, but not casual.
- **Cultural appropriateness** — business correspondence follows Bengali conventions of courtesy and formality.
- **Standard formats** — invoices, purchase orders, and business letters follow expected structures.

**What would break their trust?**
- A mistranslated business term that changes the meaning of a commercial obligation.
- Overly literal translations that sound unnatural in Bengali business contexts.
- Inconsistent use of formal/informal address in business correspondence.
- Missing or altered company type designations that change legal standing.

## Decision Framework

### 1. Company Types and Legal Structures

| Source Type | Bengali Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Limited Company (Ltd) | লিমিটেড (Ltd.) — preserve abbreviation |
| Private Limited (Pvt Ltd) | প্রাইভেট লিমিটেড (Pvt Ltd.) — preserve abbreviation |
| Public Limited Company | পাবলিক লিমিটেড কোম্পানি |
| Partnership | অংশীদারিত্ব / পার্টনারশিপ |
| Sole Proprietorship | একক মালিকানা / সোল প্রোপ্রাইটরশিপ |
| Joint Venture | যৌথ উদ্যোগ |
| Multinational Corporation | বহুজাতিক কোম্পানি |
| NGO | এনজিও / অরাজকতামূলক সংস্থা |

**Decision**: Legal entity types are generally NOT translated into Bengali equivalents. Preserve the original designation with Bengali explanation on first use.

### 2. Business Correspondence Register

| Source Register | Bengali Decision |
|---|---|
| Formal business letter | Use আপনি register throughout. Use formal opening and closing formulas. |
| Email (professional) | Semi-formal: আপনি register, but slightly less rigid than letters. |
| Marketing material | May use তুমি register for consumer-facing content; আপনি for B2B. |
| Internal memo | Context-dependent: আপনি for large organizations, তুমি for small teams. |

### 3. Business Letter Structure

| Element | Bengali Convention |
|---|---|
| Sender's address | Top of letter, left-aligned or right-aligned |
| Date | DD/MM/YYYY format |
| Recipient's address | Below date, left-aligned |
| Subject line | `বিষয়:` followed by concise subject |
| Salutation | `সম্মানিত জনাব/জনাবা [Name],` or `সম্মানিত মহোদয়/মহোদয়া,` |
| Body | Formal, clear, concise paragraphs |
| Closing | `বিশ্বাসী,` or `সাদর বিনম্র,` |
| Signature | Signatory name and title below closing |

## Error Pattern Library

### Critical Errors (zero tolerance)

| Error | Why It Is Critical |
|---|---|
| **Translating company type abbreviations** | Changing "Ltd" to a Bengali equivalent alters the legal entity type. Preserve the original abbreviation. |
| **Inconsistent formal address** | Switching between আপনি and তুমি in the same business document is a major error. |
| **Altering invoice line items** | Changing product descriptions, quantities, or prices in commercial documents changes the transaction. |
| **Wrong currency for jurisdiction** | Using `₹` for a Bangladeshi transaction or `৳` for an Indian transaction misrepresents the currency. |

### Common Errors (frequent in practice)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Overly literal business phrases** | "Please find attached" should be translated idiomatically, not literally. |
| **Missing subject line in business letters** | Bengali business letters require `বিষয়:` (Subject:) — always include it. |
| **Register inconsistency in correspondence** | Business letters must maintain formal register throughout. |

### Subtle Errors (easy to miss)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **False friends in trade terms** | "Credit" in trade contexts means something different from accounting credit. Use the correct Bengali term for each context. |
| **Missing explanatory note on foreign terms** | International trade terms (Incoterms, etc.) should include the English term in parentheses on first use. |
| **Date format inconsistency** | Mix of DD/MM/YYYY and other formats in the same document creates confusion. |

```
[স্বাক্ষর]
[নাম]
[পদবি]
```
