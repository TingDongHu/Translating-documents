# Domain: Administrative (Spanish — ES)

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** Funcionario / Empleado público (Civil servant) in a Spanish *Administración General del Estado*, *Comunidad Autónoma*, or *Entidad Local*. Expects strict compliance with *Ley 39/2015* (LPACAP — *Ley del Procedimiento Administrativo Común de las Administraciones Públicas*) and *Ley 40/2015* (LRJSP — *Régimen Jurídico del Sector Público*). Reads for procedural correctness and legal validity.  
**Secondary Reader:** Ciudadano / Administrado (Citizen or legal person submitting an *instancia* or *solicitud*). Needs clear instructions, correct forms, and accessible but formal language. Polite register (*usted*) mandatory. Expects to be addressed as *el interesado / la interesada*.  
**Tertiary Reader:** Tribunal administrativo or Juzgado de lo Contencioso-Administrativo that may review the administrative act on appeal. Expects correct citation of *artículos* from LPACAP, LRJSP, and the relevant sectoral regulations.

**Reader expectations:**
- All official documents must bear a *Número de registro de entrada/salida*, date stamp, and identification of the *órgano administrativo*.
- Language is formal, impersonal, and procedural. Use *se informa*, *se resuelve*, *se acuerda*, *se notifica*.
- Address the recipient as *Vd.* (usted) or *el/la interesado/a*. Never use *tú*.
- Citations use the full law name on first reference, then abbreviated.
- Documents submitted by citizens are called *instancias* or *solicitudes*; the administration's response is a *resolución*.
- Administrative silence (*silencio administrativo*) has specific legal effects (*positivo / negativo*).

## Standard Conventions

1. **LPACAP compliance:** All administrative documents must reference the applicable articles of *Ley 39/2015, de 1 de octubre, del Procedimiento Administrativo Común de las Administraciones Públicas* (LPACAP) for procedural matters, and *Ley 40/2015, de 1 de octubre, de Régimen Jurídico del Sector Público* (LRJSP) for organisational matters. Use the full title on first citation; thereafter *LPACAP* and *LRJSP*.
2. **Structure of an application (instancia/solicitud):** Must contain: identification of the applicant (name, NIF, address), identification of the addressee (órgano), a clear statement of the facts (*Hechos*), the legal basis (*Fundamentos de Derecho*), the specific request (*Solicita*), place, date, signature, and list of attachments. See LPACAP art. 66.
3. **Administrative silence:** If the administration does not resolve within the deadline (usually 3 months), the application is deemed granted (*silencio positivo*) or denied (*silencio negativo*) depending on the specific procedure. This must be disclosed in the appeals notice of every resolution.
4. **Formal register:** Use *usted* exclusively. Use *el/la interesado/a* for the citizen. Use impersonal constructions: *se informa*, *se resuelve*, *se acuerda*, *se notifica*. Never use colloquial or abbreviated language. Official documents use *Excm./Ilmo.* for high authorities (*Excelentísimo/Ilustrísimo*).
5. **Date and signature format:** Documents end with *En [ciudad], a [DD] de [mes] de [AAAA]*. The signature line includes name, position, and body. The official stamp (*sello*) must cover part of the signature.
6. **Registration of documents:** Every document entering or leaving the administration must bear: *N.º de registro, fecha y hora de registro, órgano, departamento*. Electronic registration is governed by LPACAP art. 16.
7. **Appeals notice:** Every administrative resolution must inform the recipient of: the type of appeal available, the body it is addressed to, the deadline, and whether the act suspends execution. Formula: *Contra la presente resolución, que agota la vía administrativa, cabe interponer recurso potestativo de reposición ante [órgano] en el plazo de un mes, o recurso contencioso-administrativo ante [juzgado] en el plazo de dos meses.*
8. **Electronic administration:** Since *Ley 39/2015*, electronic interaction is mandatory for legal persons, and increasingly for citizens. Key terms: *sede electrónica, registro electrónico, notificación electrónica, firma electrónica, certificado digital*. The *Carpeta Ciudadana* is the citizen's unified portal.
9. **Citation hierarchy for laws:** Full name and publication reference on first citation: *Ley 39/2015, de 1 de octubre, del Procedimiento Administrativo Común de las Administraciones Públicas*. Thereafter: *LPACAP*. Articles: *art. 24 LPACAP*, subsections: *art. 24.3 LPACAP*, paragraphs: *art. 24.3.a) LPACAP*.
10. **Gender-neutral language in official documents:** The administration is increasingly using paired forms (*el/la interesado/a*, *el/la funcionario/a*, *el ciudadano o la ciudadana*) or collective nouns (*la ciudadanía*, *la persona interesada*, *el personal funcionario*). Avoid the slash (*/@*) in formal documents; use paired forms or gender-neutral collectives instead.
