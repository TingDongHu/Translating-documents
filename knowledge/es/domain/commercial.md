# Domain: Commercial (Spanish — ES)

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** Director comercial / Gerente de ventas (Sales Manager) in a Spanish or Spanish-speaking company, 35–55 years old. Expects business correspondence to follow standard formulae (*Estimado Sr. X / Muy señor mío*), clear and polite register, and correct use of *usted*. Reads quickly — key information (pricing, deadlines, quantities) must be prominent.  
**Secondary Reader:** Responsable de administración / Contable who processes invoices, credit notes, and payment reminders. Requires exact numeric accuracy (decimal comma, period for thousands), correct VAT/IVA treatment, and unambiguous payment term expressions (*a 30 días fecha factura*).  
**Tertiary Reader:** Cliente / Proveedor (customer or supplier) in a B2B or B2C relationship. Expects professional tone, correct addressing, and legally compliant document structure (especially for *facturas* which must meet Spanish *Reglamento de facturación* requirements).

**Reader expectations:**
- *Estimado/a* + title + surname for first contact; *Estimado/a* + first name for ongoing correspondence.
- *Muy señor mío / Muy señores nuestros* is very formal, used in legal notices or when the recipient is unknown.
- All monetary amounts use EUR symbol after the amount with NBSP: *1.250,00 EUR*.
- Dates follow the format *DD/MM/AAAA* or *a DD de MM de AAAA*.
- IVA (VAT) treatment must be explicit: *IVA al 21 % incluido/excluido*.
- The *Mercantil / Commercial Register* is the *Registro Mercantil*.

## Standard Conventions

1. **Form of address:** *Estimado Sr. [Apellido]* (male), *Estimada Sra. [Apellido]* (female). If gender is unknown, use full name: *Estimada/o [Nombre Apellido]*. Use *Estimado/a + nombre* only after an established relationship. Never use *tú* in B2B correspondence unless the relationship is explicitly informal (startup culture, creative industries).
2. **Invoice compliance:** Spanish invoices must include: sequential number, issue date, supplier NIF, customer NIF, description of goods/services, *base imponible*, IVA rate and amount, total, and due date. For invoices subject to *IRPF withholding*, the *retención* must be shown separately. Reference *RD 1619/2012, de 30 de noviembre*.
3. **Currency format:** EUR amounts use a period for thousands and comma for decimals: *1.234,50 EUR*. Place EUR after the number with an NBSP. For cents use two decimal places.
4. **Date format:** Use *DD/MM/AAAA* in tables and forms. Use *a DD de MM de AAAA* in correspondence: *a 15 de marzo de 2024*. Never use the US format MM/DD/AAAA.
5. **Commercial correspondence structure:** (1) Letterhead with *NIF* and company details; (2) Date and place; (3) Reference line (*N/Ref.* / *S/Ref.*); (4) Subject (*Asunto:*); (5) Salutation; (6) Body text — brief, structured paragraphs; (7) Closing formula; (8) Signature block with name, position, company; (9) Enclosure notation (*Adj.:*).
6. **Payment term expressions:** *30 días fecha factura* means 30 days from invoice date. *60 días fin de mes* means 60 days from month-end. The late payment interest rate is set by *Ley 3/2004* (transposing EU Directive 2011/7). For intra-community transactions, include *Operación intracomunitaria / IVA exento (art. 22 LIVA)*.
7. **Quantity and unit conventions:** Use *Ud.* (unidad) for unit, *Kg.* for kilograms, *m* for metres. In price lists, *PVP* = *Precio de Venta al Público* (retail price); *PVR* = *Precio de Venta Recomendado*.
8. **Commercial abbreviations:** *S.L.* (Sociedad Limitada), *S.A.* (Sociedad Anónima), *S.L.U.* (Sociedad Limitada Unipersonal), *S.A.U.* (Sociedad Anónima Unipersonal). Capitalisation is always with periods: *S.L.* not *SL*.
9. **Cultural note on directness:** Spanish commercial communication is more formal and indirect than US/UK business English. Avoid blunt refusals; use softening phrases: *Lamentamos comunicarle...*, *Sentimos no poder atender su solicitud en esta ocasión...*. Direct "no" is considered rude.
10. **Telephone and email:** Telephone numbers use *Tel.:* or *Tfno.:*. Email uses *Correo electrónico:* or *E-mail:*. Website uses *Web:*. The format +34 XXX XXX XXX for Spain. Always include the country code for international.
