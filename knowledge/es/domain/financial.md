# Domain: Financial (Spanish — ES)

## Reader Model

**Primary Reader:** Contable / Auditor (Accountant or Auditor), 30–55 years old, chartered (*Censor Jurado de Cuentas* or *Economista*). Deeply familiar with the *Plan General de Contabilidad* (PGC) and its sectoral adaptations. Expects exact account-number references (*Cuenta 430: Clientes*), correct use of *debe/haber* (debit/credit), and balance-sheet structure per PGC.  
**Secondary Reader:** Director financiero / CFO who reviews annual accounts (*cuentas anuales*) for board presentation. Expects the standard three-statement package: *Balance de situación*, *Cuenta de pérdidas y ganancias*, and *Memoria*. Uses *EBITDA*, *cash flow*, and ratios but expects Spanish labels in statutory filings.  
**Tertiary Reader:** Inspector de Hacienda (Tax Inspector) or auditor externo who verifies compliance with *PGC* and *Ley del Impuesto sobre Sociedades* (LIS). Scrutinises the *Memoria* (notes) for completeness.

**Reader expectations:**
- The chart of accounts (*Cuadro de cuentas*) follows the PGC 7-digit structure (Groups 1–7 for B/S and P&L; Groups 8 and 9 for equity changes and budget).
- EUR is the sole currency. Amounts are formatted with period for thousands and comma for decimals: *1.234.567,89 EUR*.
- Debit/Credit: *Debe* (Dr) / *Haber* (Cr). *Cargo* and *abono* are also used (verb: *cargar / abonar una cuenta*).
- Tax terms are specific to the Spanish system: *IS* (Impuesto sobre Sociedades), *IVA* (VAT), *IRPF* (Personal Income Tax).
- Financial statements are prepared under *imagen fiel* (true and fair view) principle.

## Standard Conventions

1. **PGC compliance:** Any financial statement translation should reference PGC account numbers in parentheses on first mention, e.g., *Clientes (430)*. The PGC is mandatory for all Spanish companies per *RD 1514/2007* (general) and *RD 1515/2007* (PYMES). For groups applying IFRS, the *NIIF* standards as adopted by the EU are used.
2. **Currency formatting:** Amounts in EUR use the format *X.XXX,XX EUR* (period for thousands, comma for decimals). The EUR symbol is placed after the amount with NBSP. For financial tables, right-align all numeric columns.
3. **Debit/Credit notation:** *Debe* (Dr) on the left, *Haber* (Cr) on the right. Use *cargar* = to debit, *abonar* = to credit. The abbreviation *Cta.* stands for *Cuenta* (Account).
4. **Date format:** Financial statements always use *DD/MM/AAAA*. Fiscal year (*ejercicio económico*) runs from 1 January to 31 December unless otherwise specified. *Ejercicio* = fiscal year.
5. **Tax identification:** All Spanish companies have a *NIF* (formerly *CIF*). Individuals use *DNI* or *NIE*. In financial documents the tax ID always appears in the format: *NIF: B-12345678* (for companies).
6. **Financial statement package:** The standard statutory set comprises: (1) *Balance de situación*, (2) *Cuenta de pérdidas y ganancias*, (3) *Estado de cambios en el patrimonio neto*, (4) *Estado de flujos de efectivo*, (5) *Memoria*. All five are mandatory for normal PGC; PYMES file abbreviated versions.
7. **Translation of IFRS standards:** The Spanish official translation published by ICAC must be used. If no official translation exists, use the draft from AECA (Asociación Española de Contabilidad y Administración de Empresas). Never back-translate IFRS terms from EN to ES without checking ICAC's official glossary.
8. **Negative amounts:** Use parentheses for negative figures in financial tables: *(1.234,00 EUR)*. In running text, use *negativo* or *pérdida de* for losses, e.g., *resultado negativo del ejercicio*.
9. **Audit report structure:** The Spanish audit report (*informe de auditoría*) follows a standard structure: (1) *Opinión*, (2) *Fundamento de la opinión*, (3) *Aspectos clave de la auditoría*, (4) *Otra información*, (5) *Responsabilidad de los administradores*, (6) *Responsabilidad del auditor*. This matches ISA 700 format.
10. **Common acronyms:** *BAII* (EBIT), *BAI* (profit before tax), *BPA* (EPS), *DFN* (net financial debt), *EBITDA* (preserved as-is), *PGC* (Plan General de Contabilidad), *FdC* (Fondo de comercio), *NIIF* (IFRS), *PYME* (SME). Always define acronyms on first use in each document.
