## Formality and Address — Nghi thức xưng hô

### English "You" → Vietnamese Formal/Informal Pronouns

Vietnamese has a complex kinship-based pronoun system with no single equivalent of English "you". The correct pronoun depends on age, social status, relationship, and formality level.

- **English "you" (formal, singular, unknown addressee)** → Vietnamese Quý vị / Ông / Bà
  - Source: "You are required to submit..." → Target: "Quý vị có nghĩa vụ nộp..." (formal, generic)
  - Source: "You may proceed" → Target: "Quý vị có thể tiếp tục" (formal register)
  - Source: "We invite you to attend" → Target: "Kính mời Ông/Bà tham dự" (formal invitation)
  - Source: "Thank you for your interest" → Target: "Cảm ơn sự quan tâm của Quý vị"
  - Source: "Dear Sir/Madam" → Target: "Kính thưa Ông/Bà" / "Thưa Ông/Bà"

- **English "you" (formal, male)** → Vietnamese Ông
  - Source: "We have received your application" → Target: "Chúng tôi đã nhận được đơn của Ông"

- **English "you" (formal, female)** → Vietnamese Bà
  - Source: "Your order has been confirmed" → Target: "Đơn hàng của Bà đã được xác nhận"

- **English "you" (informal, peer/friend)** → Vietnamese bạn
  - Source: "How are you?" → Target: "Bạn có khỏe không?"
  - Source: "I will send you the file" → Target: "Tôi sẽ gửi bạn file"

- **English "you" (informal, older → younger)** → Vietnamese em / cháu
  - Source: "You did a good job" → Target: "Em đã làm tốt lắm" (if speaker is older)
  - Source: "Please sit down" → Target: "Em ngồi xuống đi" (familiar, older to younger)

- **English "you" (informal, younger → older)** → Vietnamese anh / chị
  - Source: "Thank you for your help" → Target: "Cảm ơn anh đã giúp đỡ" (male addressee)
  - Source: "I agree with you" → Target: "Em đồng ý với anh" (female speaker to male)

- **English "you" (plural)** → Vietnamese các bạn / quý vị / các anh / các chị
  - Source: "You are all invited" → Target: "Tất cả các bạn đều được mời"
  - Source: "Thank you all for coming" → Target: "Cảm ơn tất cả quý vị đã đến"

### English Imperatives → Vietnamese Polite Forms

- **English imperative mood**: softer in Vietnamese using xin / hãy / đề nghị
  - Source: "Click here" → Target: "Nhấp vào đây" / "Xin nhấp vào đây" (polite)
  - Source: "Enter your password" → Target: "Nhập mật khẩu của bạn" / "Vui lòng nhập mật khẩu"
  - Source: "Please confirm" → Target: "Vui lòng xác nhận" / "Xin vui lòng xác nhận"
  - Source: "Submit the form" → Target: "Đề nghị nộp đơn" (formal instruction) or "Hãy nộp đơn" (neutral)

- **English "please"** → Vietnamese xin / vui lòng / làm ơn (graded by formality)
  - Source: "Please find attached..." → Target: "Khoản đính kèm là..." / "Xin vui lòng xem file đính kèm"
  - Source: "Please let us know" → Target: "Xin vui lòng cho chúng tôi biết"
  - Source: "Please be advised that..." → Target: "Xin thông báo rằng..." / "Trân trọng thông báo rằng"
  - Source: "Please do not hesitate to contact us" → Target: "Xin đừng ngần ngại liên hệ với chúng tôi"

### English Modal Verbs → Vietnamese Equivalents

- **English "must"** → Vietnamese phải / bắt buộc (strong obligation)
  - Source: "You must comply with regulations" → Target: "Quý vị phải tuân thủ các quy định"
  - Source: "This field must be filled" → Target: "Trường này bắt buộc phải điền"

- **English "should"** → Vietnamese nên (recommendation)
  - Source: "You should check the document" → Target: "Bạn nên kiểm tra tài liệu"
  - Source: "This should be done carefully" → Target: "Việc này nên được thực hiện cẩn thận"

- **English "may"** → Vietnamese có thể (permission/possibility)
  - Source: "You may proceed" → Target: "Bạn có thể tiếp tục"
  - Source: "This may cause errors" → Target: "Điều này có thể gây ra lỗi"

- **English "shall" (legal)** → Vietnamese có nghĩa vụ / phải (obligation, not future tense)
  - Source: "The Buyer shall pay..." → Target: "Bên Mua có nghĩa vụ thanh toán..." (not "sẽ thanh toán")
  - Source: "The Company shall provide..." → Target: "Công ty phải cung cấp..." (legal obligation)
  - Source: "No modification shall be made without..." → Target: "Không được sửa đổi nếu không có..."

### Titles and Address Forms

- **English titles (Mr/Ms/Mrs/Dr)** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "Mr. Smith" → Target: "Ông Smith" (keeps surname, adds Vietnamese title)
  - Source: "Ms. Johnson" → Target: "Bà Johnson" / "Cô Johnson" (context-dependent: bà for married, cô for unmarried)
  - Source: "Dr. Brown" → Target: "Tiến sĩ Brown" (academic title preserved)
  - Source: "Professor Lee" → Target: "Giáo sư Lee"
  - Source: "Dear Mr. Smith" → Target: "Kính thưa Ông Smith," (comma in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "Dear Sirs" → Target: "Kính thưa Quý vị," / "Thưa các Ông,"

- **English "To whom it may concern"** → Vietnamese equivalent
  - Source: "To whom it may concern" → Target: "Kính gửi các cá nhân/tổ chức có liên quan" / "Kính gửi Quý cơ quan hữu quan"

- **English business email openings** → Vietnamese conventions
  - Source: "Dear All" → Target: "Kính gửi tất cả" / "Chào tất cả" (less formal)
  - Source: "Hi Team" → Target: "Chào cả nhóm" / "Chào các bạn" (informal)
  - Source: "Good morning" → Target: "Chào buổi sáng" (Vietnamese does not use time-of-day greetings in formal emails; jump straight to addressing the recipient)

- **English letter closings** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "Sincerely," → Target: "Trân trọng," (standard formal closing)
  - Source: "Best regards," → Target: "Trân trọng," / "Thân ái," (formal vs semi-formal)
  - Source: "Yours faithfully," → Target: "Trân trọng," (same universal formal closing)
  - Source: "Yours sincerely," → Target: "Trân trọng," (same in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "Regards," → Target: "Trân trọng," / "Thân mến" (semi-formal)
  - Note: Vietnamese does not have the English distinction between "Yours faithfully" vs. "Yours sincerely" — use "Trân trọng" universally for formal correspondence

### English Conversational Style in Formal Writing

- **English direct conversational style** → Vietnamese more reserved/formal in business writing
  - Source: "Let's get started" → Target: "Chúng ta hãy bắt đầu" (neutral, not too familiar)
  - Source: "I think we should..." → Target: "Tôi cho rằng chúng ta nên..." (formal "cho rằng" rather than "nghĩ")
  - Source: "Sounds great!" → Target: "Nghe có vẻ tốt!" / "Tuyệt vời!" (tone depends on relationship)
  - Source: "No problem" → Target: "Không vấn đề gì" / "Không sao" / "Không có gì" (context-dependent)
  - Source: "Feel free to contact me" → Target: "Xin đừng ngần ngại liên hệ với tôi" (formal) or "Cứ liên hệ với tôi nhé" (informal)

## Number and Date Conventions — Quy ước số và ngày tháng

### Decimal and Thousands Separators

- **English decimal point (.) → Vietnamese comma (,)**
  - Source: "3.14" → Target: "3,14"
  - Source: "99.9%" → Target: "99,9%"
  - Source: "0.5" → Target: "0,5"
  - Source: "average of 2.5" → Target: "trung bình 2,5"
  - This is the single most common numeric error in English→Vietnamese translation — ZERO TOLERANCE

- **English thousands separator (,) → Vietnamese period (.) or space**
  - Source: "12,345,678" → Target: "12.345.678"
  - Source: "1,000" → Target: "1.000"
  - Source: "10,000" → Target: "10.000" (⚠️ CRITICAL: 10,000 in English is 10.000 in Vietnamese; never confuse with Vietnamese "10.000" = English 10,000)
  - Source: "population: 1,200,000" → Target: "dân số: 1.200.000"

### Date Format Conversion

- **English date format: Month DD, YYYY or MM/DD/YYYY → Vietnamese: DD/MM/YYYY**
  - Source: "January 15, 2024" → Target: "15 tháng 1 năm 2024" / "15/01/2024"
  - Source: "December 31, 2023" → Target: "31 tháng 12 năm 2023"
  - Source: "03/05/2024" → Target: **CHECK CONTEXT**: In English this is March 5 or May 3. ALWAYS clarify ambiguous numeric dates. Vietnamese would read the English MM/DD/YYYY format correctly only if the month is 13+.
  - Source: "12/31/2023" → Target: "31/12/2023" (month and day swapped)
  - Source: "2023-12-31" → Target: "31/12/2023" (ISO format → Vietnamese numeric)
  - This is the second most common numeric error — ZERO TOLERANCE

- **English month names in text** → Vietnamese month names (use numbers, not month names, for compactness)
  - Source: "January" → Target: "tháng 1" (not "tháng Giêng" which is the traditional lunar name)
  - Source: "February" → Target: "tháng 2"
  - Source: "March" → Target: "tháng 3"
  - Source: "April" → Target: "tháng 4"
  - Source: "May" → Target: "tháng 5"
  - Source: "June" → Target: "tháng 6"
  - Source: "July" → Target: "tháng 7"
  - Source: "August" → Target: "tháng 8"
  - Source: "September" → Target: "tháng 9"
  - Source: "October" → Target: "tháng 10"
  - Source: "November" → Target: "tháng 11"
  - Source: "December" → Target: "tháng 12"
  - Note: Traditional Vietnamese names (tháng Giêng, tháng Chạp) exist for some months but modern administrative writing uses numbered months

### Time Conversion

- **English 12h time (AM/PM) → Vietnamese 24h time**
  - Source: "3:00 PM" → Target: "15:00" or "3 giờ chiều"
  - Source: "12:00 AM" → Target: "0:00" (midnight) or "12 giờ đêm"
  - Source: "12:00 PM" → Target: "12:00" (noon) or "12 giờ trưa"
  - Source: "9:30 AM" → Target: "9:30" or "9 giờ 30 phút sáng"
  - Source: "The meeting is at 2 PM" → Target: "Cuộc họp lúc 14:00"
  - Source: "office hours: 8 AM - 5 PM" → Target: "giờ làm việc: 8:00 – 17:00"

### Number Scales and Units

- **English number scales** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "1,000" → Target: "1.000" / "một nghìn"
  - Source: "10,000" → Target: "10.000" / "mười nghìn" (NOT "một vạn" in modern usage)
  - Source: "100,000" → Target: "100.000" / "một trăm nghìn"
  - Source: "1,000,000" → Target: "1.000.000" / "một triệu"
  - Source: "1,000,000,000" → Target: "1.000.000.000" / "một tỷ" (⚠️ Vietnamese "tỷ" = 10⁹, matches US billion)
  - Source: "1 trillion" → Target: "1.000.000.000.000" / "nghìn tỷ" (Vietnamese uses "nghìn tỷ" for 10¹²)
  - Source: "1 billion" → Target: "1 tỷ" (matches directly)
  - Source: "5 million" → Target: "5 triệu"
  - Source: "2.5 billion" → Target: "2,5 tỷ"

- **English measurement system (imperial)** → Vietnamese measurement system (metric)
  - Source: "5 miles" → Target: "8 km" (convert to metric; may add imperial in parentheses: "8 km (5 dặm)")
  - Source: "10 feet" → Target: "3,05 m"
  - Source: "1 gallon" → Target: "3,78 lít"
  - Source: "32°F" → Target: "0°C"
  - Source: "1 pound" → Target: "0,45 kg"
  - Source: "1 inch" → Target: "2,54 cm"
  - Source: "1 ounce" → Target: "28,35 g"
  - Source: "1 yard" → Target: "0,91 m"
  - Note: Metric conversion is mandatory for most technical, legal, and administrative documents. For literary texts, consider keeping imperial with metric in parentheses.

- **English currency** → Vietnamese currency equivalents
  - Source: "$100" → Target: "100 đô la Mỹ (USD)" / "100 USD"
  - Source: "€50" → Target: "50 euro"
  - Source: "£30" → Target: "30 bảng Anh (GBP)"
  - Source: "¥10,000" → Target: "10.000 yên Nhật (JPY)"
  - Source: "US$5 billion" → Target: "5 tỷ đô la Mỹ"

### Century, Decade, and Era References

- **English century format** → Vietnamese
  - Source: "21st century" → Target: "thế kỷ XXI" / "thế kỷ 21" (Roman numeral preferred in formal writing)
  - Source: "20th century" → Target: "thế kỷ XX"
  - Source: "early 19th century" → Target: "đầu thế kỷ XIX"
  - Source: "late 18th century" → Target: "cuối thế kỷ XVIII"

- **English decades** → Vietnamese
  - Source: "the 1990s" → Target: "những năm 1990" / "thập niên 1990"
  - Source: "the 2000s" → Target: "những năm 2000"
  - Source: "the early 1960s" → Target: "đầu những năm 1960"

- **English BC/AD** → Vietnamese trước CN / sau CN
  - Source: "500 BC" → Target: "500 năm trước Công nguyên (TCN)"
  - Source: "AD 2024" → Target: "2024 sau Công nguyên (SCN)" or simply "năm 2024"
  - Note: Vietnamese also uses "trước CN" and "sau CN" as equivalents

## Cultural References — Chuyển đổi văn hóa

### English Idioms → Vietnamese Equivalents

- **English idiomatic expressions** → Vietnamese equivalents with similar meaning (NOT literal translation)
  - Source: "It's raining cats and dogs" → Target: "Mưa như trút nước" (natural equivalent, not literal)
  - Source: "The ball is in your court" → Target: "Tùy bạn quyết định" / "Quyền quyết định thuộc về bạn"
  - Source: "To kill two birds with one stone" → Target: "Một công đôi việc" / "Mũi tên trúng hai đích"
  - Source: "Don't cry over spilled milk" → Target: "Đừng khóc vì sữa đổ" (direct equivalent exists) or "Việc đã rồi thì thôi"
  - Source: "To bite the bullet" → Target: "Cắn răng chịu đựng" (natural equivalent)
  - Source: "A piece of cake" → Target: "Dễ như trở bàn tay" / "Quá dễ" / "Chuyện nhỏ"
  - Source: "Once in a blue moon" → Target: "Hiếm khi lắm" / "Có một không hai" / "Có chăng thì hiếm"
  - Source: "To be on the same page" → Target: "Cùng quan điểm" / "Hiểu giống nhau" / "Đồng thuận"
  - Source: "To think outside the box" → Target: "Suy nghĩ sáng tạo" / "Nghĩ khác biệt" / "Tư duy phá cách"
  - Source: "To be a blessing in disguise" → Target: "Họa có phúc" / "Trong cái rủi có cái may"
  - Source: "Better late than never" → Target: "Thà muộn còn hơn không"
  - Source: "To get straight to the point" → Target: "Đi thẳng vào vấn đề" / "Nói thẳng"
  - Source: "To add fuel to the fire" → Target: "Đổ thêm dầu vào lửa" (same idiom exists)
  - Source: "The tip of the iceberg" → Target: "Phần nổi của tảng băng chìm" (same concept)
  - Source: "To see eye to eye" → Target: "Cùng quan điểm" / "Đồng tình" / "Hiệp ý"

### English Proverbs → Vietnamese Equivalents

- **English proverbs** → Vietnamese cultural equivalents
  - Source: "Actions speak louder than words" → Target: "Hành động có giá trị hơn lời nói" / "Trăm lời nói không bằng một việc làm"
  - Source: "Where there's smoke, there's fire" → Target: "Không có lửa làm sao có khói" (direct equivalent!)
  - Source: "All that glitters is not gold" → Target: "Không phải cái gì lấp lánh cũng là vàng" (direct equivalent!)
  - Source: "Practice makes perfect" → Target: "Có công mài sắt có ngày nên kim" (Vietnamese equivalent: "With effort, you can grind an iron rod into a needle")
  - Source: "When in Rome, do as the Romans do" → Target: "Nhập gia tùy tục" (Sino-Vietnamese proverb: "Enter a house, follow its customs")
  - Source: "Honesty is the best policy" → Target: "Thật thà là thượng sách" / "Ngay thật là tốt nhất"

### English Culturally-Specific Terms → Vietnamese Adaptation

  - Source: "Shareholder" → Target: "Cổ đông"
  - Source: "Stakeholder" → Target: "Bên liên quan" / "Các bên có quyền lợi liên quan"

- **English academic concepts** → Vietnamese adaptation

  - Source: "GPA" → Target: "Điểm trung bình (GPA)" (keep acronym, but explain: không tương đương trực tiếp với thang điểm của Việt Nam)
  - Source: "Freshman / Sophomore / Junior / Senior" → Target: "Năm thứ nhất / thứ hai / thứ ba / thứ tư (đại học)" (descriptive)

### English Names and Transliteration

- **English proper names** → Vietnamese transliteration (preserve Latin script with Vietnamese phonetic reading)
  - Source: "John Smith" → Target: "John Smith" (kept in Latin script; Vietnamese reads phonetically as "Giôn Xmít" but this is NOT written)
  - Source: "William Shakespeare" → Target: "William Shakespeare" (kept in Latin; rarely transliterated)
  - Source: "New York" → Target: "New York" (kept in Latin; Vietnamese reads phonetically)
  - Source: "Los Angeles" → Target: "Los Angeles"
  - Source: "London" → Target: "Luân Đôn" (historical name exists!) or "London" (modern preference)
  - Source: "Washington D.C." → Target: "Washington, D.C." (kept as is)
  - Note: Unlike Russian transliteration into Cyrillic, Vietnamese keeps English proper names in their original Latin script since Vietnamese uses Latin-based alphabet. Some historical place names have Sino-Vietnamese readings: 伦敦 → Luân Đôn, 纽约 → Nữu Ước, but these are now old-fashioned.

- **English company/brand names** → Keep original Latin script
  - Source: "Microsoft" → Target: "Microsoft" (kept in Latin; Vietnamese may add "công ty" clarification)
  - Source: "Apple Inc." → Target: "Apple Inc." / "Tập đoàn Apple"
  - Source: "Google" → Target: "Google"
  - Source: "Facebook" → Target: "Facebook" (though now Meta)
  - Source: "Toyota" → Target: "Toyota"
  - Source: "Samsung" → Target: "Samsung"

## Source-specific Terminology — Thuật ngữ chuyên ngành (Anh-Việt)

### English Technical Terminology → Vietnamese (Native vs. Loanword)

- **General IT terms: native Vietnamese vs. English loanwords**

### Passive Voice and Impersonal Constructions

- **English passive voice** → Vietnamese can use passive (bị/được) or active constructions
  - Source: "The report was submitted on time" → Target: "Báo cáo đã được nộp đúng hạn" (passive with "được")

## Punctuation Conversion — Chuyển đổi dấu câu

### Quotation Marks

- **English quotation marks "" → Vietnamese quotation marks "" (same shape, different typographic rules)**
  - Source: 'He said "This is important"' → Target: 'Anh ấy nói "Điều này rất quan trọng"'
  - Source: 'The term "interface" refers to...' → Target: 'Thuật ngữ "giao diện" đề cập đến...'
  - Note: Vietnamese uses the same double quotation marks as English, but the typographic treatment may differ: Vietnamese places punctuation outside quotation marks in some contexts where English places it inside.

- **English single quotation marks '' → Vietnamese single quotes '' (same usage for nested quotes)**
  - Source: 'She asked "What did he say? \'Go now\'?"' → Target: 'Cô ấy hỏi "Anh ấy đã nói gì? \'Đi ngay\' ạ?"'

### Vietnamese Quotation Alternative: Guillemets

- **Vietnamese guillemets «»** are also used in formal/publishing contexts
  - Source: 'He said "This is important"' → Target: 'Anh ấy nói: «Điều này rất quan trọng»'
  - Source: 'The report states "the project is on track"' → Target: 'Báo cáo nêu rõ «dự án đang đi đúng hướng»'
  - Rule: Double quotes " " are standard in most Vietnamese documents. Guillemets «» appear primarily in high-end publishing, academic texts, and some government documents.

### Period and Spacing

- **English period (.) → Vietnamese period (.) (same, half-width)**
  - No conversion needed — both use half-width period

- **English exclamation mark (!) → Vietnamese (!) (same)**
  - Same usage in both languages. Used less in formal Vietnamese than in English.

- **English question mark (?) → Vietnamese (?) (same)**
  - Same usage in both languages.

### Comma Usage Differences

- **English serial/Oxford comma** → Vietnamese comma (similar convention)
  - Source: "apples, oranges, and bananas" → Target: "táo, cam và chuối" (comma before "và" is optional in Vietnamese, mirroring Oxford comma usage)
  - Source: "red, white, and blue" → Target: "đỏ, trắng và xanh"
  - Rule: Be consistent within a document — either always use or never use comma before "và" (and).

### Colon and Semicolon

- **English colon (:) → Vietnamese colon (:) (same)**
  - Source: "The following items:" → Target: "Các mục sau:" (same usage)
  - Vietnamese uses colon in the same situations as English, with the same spacing: no space before, one space after.

- **English semicolon (;) → Vietnamese semicolon (;) (same)**
  - Source: "first item; second item; third item" → Target: "mục thứ nhất; mục thứ hai; mục thứ ba"
  - Vietnamese uses semicolons similarly to English — for separating complex list items and independent clauses.

### Em Dash and En Dash

- **English em dash (—) → Vietnamese em dash (—) with Vietnamese spacing rules**
  - English often uses em dash without spaces ("word—word") → Vietnamese prefers spaces around em dash ("từ — từ")
  - Source: "Three components—CPU, RAM, storage—are critical" → Target: "Ba thành phần — CPU, RAM, bộ nhớ — là quan trọng"

- **English en dash (–) for ranges → Vietnamese en dash (–) (same usage)**
  - Source: "pages 10–20" → Target: "trang 10–20" (no spaces around en dash)
  - Source: "2010–2020" → Target: "2010–2020"

### Ellipsis

- **English ellipsis ... (3 dots) → Vietnamese ... (3 dots, same)**
  - Source: "and so on..." → Target: "v.v..." (same number of dots)
  - Source: "continue..." → Target: "tiếp tục..."
  - Note: Both English and Vietnamese use 3 dots for ellipsis. No conversion needed.

### Slashes and Other Symbols

- **English slash (/) in alternatives** → Vietnamese / (same usage)
  - Source: "and/or" → Target: "và/hoặc" (Vietnamese uses "và/hoặc" as the standard equivalent of "and/or")
  - Source: "input/output" → Target: "đầu vào/đầu ra"
  - Source: "2020/2021 fiscal year" → Target: "năm tài khóa 2020/2021"

### Percent and Degrees

- **English percent sign with no space** → Vietnamese percent: same or with space
  - Source: "50%" → Target: "50%" (no space is standard in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "a 10% increase" → Target: "tăng 10%"
  - Note: Formal/scientific Vietnamese sometimes uses a non-breaking space before %, but no space is more common.

- **English degrees** → Vietnamese degrees (same)
  - Source: "25°C" → Target: "25°C" (no space) or "25 °C" (in some formal contexts)
  - Vietnamese reads this as "25 độ C"

### Hyphenation in Compounds

- **English hyphen (-) in compound words** → Vietnamese (no hyphens; compounds are separate words)
  - Source: "decision-making" → Target: "ra quyết định" / "việc ra quyết định"
  - Source: "state-of-the-art" → Target: "hiện đại nhất" / "tân tiến nhất"
  - Source: "well-known" → Target: "nổi tiếng" / "được biết đến rộng rãi"
  - Source: "high-quality" → Target: "chất lượng cao"
  - Source: "cost-effective" → Target: "hiệu quả về chi phí" / "tiết kiệm chi phí"
  - Note: Vietnamese does not use hyphens to join compound adjectives. Any hyphenated English term should be translated as separate Vietnamese words.

### Apostrophe

- **English apostrophe (') → Vietnamese (rarely used)**
  - Source: "don't" → Target: "không" / "đừng" (contractions don't exist in Vietnamese; write as complete words)
  - Source: "company's policy" → Target: "chính sách của công ty" (Vietnamese uses possessive "của" construction)
  - Source: "O'Brien" → Target: "O'Brien" (kept for foreign names, rare)
  - Source: "children's education" → Target: "giáo dục của trẻ em"

### Capitalization Differences

- **English capitalization rules** → Vietnamese capitalization rules (significant differences)
  - English capitalizes proper nouns, sentence starts, days, months, languages, nationalities → Vietnamese is more restrictive
  - Source: "English" → Target: "tiếng Anh" (language names lowercase except the specific word identifying the language)
  - Source: "Vietnamese" → Target: "tiếng Việt"
  - Source: "French" → Target: "tiếng Pháp"
  - Source: "Monday" → Target: "thứ Hai" (days of the week are NOT capitalized in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "January" → Target: "tháng 1" (months are NOT capitalized)
  - Source: "Vietnamese people" → Target: "người Việt Nam" (nationality adjective is not capitalized)
  - Source: "the United Nations" → Target: "Liên Hợp Quốc" (proper organization names ARE capitalized)
  - Source: "Chapter 5" → Target: "Chương 5" (chapter/section headings ARE capitalized in Vietnamese)
  - Rule: Vietnamese capitalizes only proper names and sentence beginnings. Languages, days, months, and nationalities are NOT capitalized unless they begin a sentence.

### Spacing Conventions

- **English space after punctuation** → Vietnamese (same: one space after commas, periods, etc.)
  - Both languages use a single space after punctuation marks. No change needed.
  - Source: "Hello, how are you?" → Target: "Chào bạn, bạn có khỏe không?"

- **English spacing in numbers** → Vietnamese uses period as thousands separator
  - Source: "12,500" → Target: "12.500"
  - Source: "$ 50,000" → Target: "50.000 USD" (note: currency symbol typically placed after amount in Vietnamese)

- **English no space before % or °C** → Vietnamese same convention (no space)
  - Source: "50%" → Target: "50%" (same)
  - Source: "100°C" → Target: "100°C" (same)

### Vietnamese-specific Typographic Notes

- **Vietnamese uses the Latin alphabet with diacritics** — ensure tone marks and diacritics are correctly placed
  - Common diacritics: ă, â, ê, ô, ơ, ư, đ — and their tone-marked variants: á, à, ả, ã, ạ
  - The position of tone marks follows strict Vietnamese rules (e.g., "hoà" not "hòa" is the standard for "hòa" in some regions — be consistent)
  - Punctuation never appears inside a word; always at word boundaries

- **Vietnamese "và" (and) usage**: Less frequent than English "and"; Vietnamese prefers juxtaposition without conjunction
  - Source: "development and reform" → Target: "phát triển cải cách" (without "và" when they form a natural pair) or "phát triển và cải cách"
  - Source: "research and development" → Target: "nghiên cứu và phát triển (R&D)" (commonly retains "và")
  - Source: "rules and regulations" → Target: "nội quy quy định" (often without "và") or "nội quy và quy định"

- **Vietnamese word order** can differ significantly from English, beyond punctuation
  - English: adjective + noun → Vietnamese: noun + adjective
  - English: "red car" → Vietnamese: "xe màu đỏ" (car red)
  - English: "Vietnamese language" → Vietnamese: "tiếng Việt" (language Vietnamese)
  - English: "important document" → Vietnamese: "tài liệu quan trọng" (document important)
  - This is a fundamental grammatical difference that affects how punctuation interacts with syntax
