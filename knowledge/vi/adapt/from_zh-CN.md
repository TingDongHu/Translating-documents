## Formality and Address — Nghi thức xưng hô và mức độ trang trọng

### Formal/Informal Pronouns

- **Formal address**: Chinese 您 → Vietnamese Quý vị / Ông / Bà
  - Source: "请您确认" → Target: "Kính đề nghị Quý vị xác nhận"
  - Source: "请您签字" → Target: "Kính mời Ông/Bà ký tên"
  - Context: "Quý vị" is used for group address (formal); "Ông" for males, "Bà" for females
  - Source: "您好" → Target: "Kính chào Ông/Bà" (formal letter opening)
  - Source: "您公司" → Target: "Quý công ty" / "công ty của Ông/Bà"
  - Note: Vietnamese uses "Quý" + noun as a formal possessive (Quý công ty, Quý cơ quan), corresponding to Chinese 贵/您

- **Informal address**: Chinese 你 → Vietnamese bạn / anh / chị
  - Source: "你好" → Target: "Chào bạn" / "Chào anh" / "Chào chị" (context-dependent)
  - Source: "你的建议" → Target: "ý kiến của bạn" / "đề xuất của anh"
  - Context: "bạn" is general-purpose informal; "anh/chị" implies familiarity or slightly more respect by age
  - Source: "谢谢你的帮助" → Target: "Cảm ơn sự giúp đỡ của bạn"
  - Source: "请你过来" → Target: "Mời bạn qua đây" / "Mời anh đến"

- **Chinese collective address 各位 → Vietnamese Kính thưa các / Quý + noun**
  - Source: "各位同事" → Target: "Kính thưa các đồng nghiệp" / "Thưa các đồng nghiệp"
  - Source: "各位代表" → Target: "Kính thưa các đại biểu"
  - Source: "各位领导" → Target: "Kính thưa các lãnh đạo" / "Thưa các vị lãnh đạo"
  - Source: "各位来宾" → Target: "Kính thưa quý vị đại biểu" / "Quý vị khách quý"

### Chinese Honorific and Bureaucratic Titles

- **Chinese honorific titles** → Vietnamese equivalent titles (keep surname + title order)
  - Source: "王总" → Target: "Tổng Giám đốc Vương" or "Anh Vương" (context-dependent)
  - Source: "李经理" → Target: "Giám đốc Lý"
  - Source: "张局长" → Target: "Cục trưởng Trương"
  - Source: "刘老师" → Target: "Thầy Lưu" / "Cô Lưu" (for teachers) or "nhà nghiên cứu Lưu" (academic)
  - Note: Vietnamese preserves surname + title order just like Chinese, making this a direct mapping

- **Chinese bureaucratic titles** → Vietnamese functional equivalents
  - Source: "董事长" → Target: "Chủ tịch Hội đồng quản trị"
  - Source: "总经理" → Target: "Tổng giám đốc"
  - Source: "处长" → Target: "Trưởng phòng" / "Cục trưởng" (context-dependent)
  - Source: "部长" → Target: "Bộ trưởng" (same Sino-Vietnamese reading)
  - Source: "主任" → Target: "Chủ nhiệm" / "Trưởng ban"
  - Source: "书记" → Target: "Bí thư" (same Sino-Vietnamese reading)

### Self-deprecation and Formal Expressions

- **Chinese self-deprecation in formal speech**: Vietnamese has a similar tradition of humble expressions
  - Source: "鄙人" → Target: "tôi" (neutral) or "kẻ hèn này" (very formal, literary)
  - Source: "拙见" → Target: "ý kiến của tôi" or "thiển ý" (Sino-Vietnamese: 浅 ý — shall opinion)
  - Source: "愚见" → Target: "ngu kiến" (Sino-Vietnamese reading, formal literary) or "theo tôi nghĩ"
  - Source: "寒舍" → Target: "nhà tôi" / "tệ xá" (Sino-Vietnamese, humble form for "my house")
  - Source: "贵公司" → Target: "Quý công ty" (formal "your company")

- **Chinese polite requests** → Vietnamese formal constructions
  - Source: "请" → Target: "xin" / "kính mời" / "đề nghị" (graded by formality level)
  - Source: "请稍候" → Target: "Xin vui lòng chờ một chút"
  - Source: "请多关照" → Target: "Mong được sự giúp đỡ" / "Kính mong sự chiếu cố"
  - Source: "请多指教" → Target: "Kính mong được chỉ giáo" / "Rất mong được học hỏi"
  - Source: "谢谢" → Target: "Cảm ơn" / "Xin cảm ơn" / "Trân trọng cảm ơn"
  - Source: "辛苦了" → Target: "Đã vất vả rồi" / "Cảm ơn sự cố gắng của anh/chị"
  - Source: "麻烦您了" → Target: "Làm phiền Ông/Bà quá" / "Xin lỗi đã làm phiền"

### Pronoun Avoidance and Vietnamese Kinship Terms

- **Vietnamese pronoun system is complex**: Unlike Chinese 你/您 dichotomy, Vietnamese has a rich kinship-based pronoun system
  - Source: "你" (to older person) → Target: "anh" / "chị" / "ông" / "bà" (based on age and relationship)
  - Source: "你" (to younger person) → Target: "em" / "cháu"
  - Source: "你" (to peer) → Target: "bạn" / "cậu" / "mình"
  - Source: "你" (formal unknown) → Target: "quý vị" (plural) or "anh/chị" (singular)
  - Key rule: Vietnamese never uses a single pronoun for all 2nd-person contexts; always choose based on relative age and social relationship

- **Vietnamese "mình" for collective we** → mapping Chinese 我们
  - Source: "我们" → Target: "chúng tôi" (exclusive: speaker's group, not including addressee)
  - Source: "我们" → Target: "chúng ta" (inclusive: including the addressee)
  - Source: "大家" → Target: "mọi người" / "tất cả mọi người"
  - Source: "咱们" → Target: "chúng mình" / "chúng ta" (informal inclusive)

- **Chinese 本人 / 本人 → Vietnamese tự cách / bản nhân**
  - Source: "本人同意" → Target: "Tôi đồng ý" / "bản thân tôi đồng ý" (legal context)
  - Source: "本人签字" → Target: "chữ ký của bản thân" or simply "chữ ký" (more natural)

### Greetings and Closings

- **Chinese letter opening greetings** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "尊敬的先生/女士：" → Target: "Kính thưa Ông/Bà," or "Thưa Ông/Bà," (comma in Vietnamese, not colon)
  - Source: "尊敬的领导" → Target: "Kính thưa lãnh đạo"
  - Source: "致有关人士" → Target: "Gửi các cá nhân/cơ quan hữu quan"

- **Chinese letter closings** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "此致 敬礼" → Target: "Xin trân trọng kính chào" / "Trân trọng"
  - Source: "顺祝商祺" → Target: "Kính chúc sức khỏe và hợp tác tốt đẹp" / "Trân trọng"
  - Source: "此致敬礼" → Target: "Kính thư" (formal closing)
  - Source: "祝好" → Target: "Chúc tốt đẹp" / "Chúc sức khỏe" (informal closing)
  - Note: Vietnamese formal letters typically end with "Trân trọng" (Respectfully) as the standard closing

## Number and Date Conventions — Quy ước số và ngày tháng

### Numeric Scale Units

- **Chinese 万 (10,000)** → Vietnamese "10.000" or "mười nghìn" (not "vạn" in most modern contexts)
  - Source: "五万" → Target: "50.000" (numeric) or "năm mươi nghìn"
  - Source: "人口十万" → Target: "dân số 100.000 người"
  - Source: "几十万" → Target: "hàng trăm nghìn"
  - Note: Classical/literary Vietnamese does use "vạn" (万 in Sino-Vietnamese), but modern Vietnamese prefers "mười nghìn" / "trăm nghìn" for 10⁴
  - Exception: Fixed expressions like "vạn nhất" (万一 — what if), "vạn sự" (万事 — everything)

- **Chinese 亿 (100,000,000)** → Vietnamese "100.000.000" or "một trăm triệu"
  - Source: "十四亿" → Target: "1.400.000.000" or "1,4 tỷ" (note: 14亿 = 1,4 tỷ in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "一亿" → Target: "100.000.000" or "một trăm triệu"
  - Source: "几亿人" → Target: "hàng trăm triệu người"
  - Note: Vietnamese uses "tỷ" (亿) differently: Chinese 亿 = 10⁸ (100 million), Vietnamese "tỷ" = 10⁹ (1 billion). This is a CRITICAL difference. Always convert: 1亿 = 100 triệu (not 1 tỷ), 10亿 = 1 tỷ.

- **Chinese 兆 (10¹² in modern usage)** → Vietnamese "triệu" (⚠️ different!)
  - Source: "几兆" → Target: Check context carefully: Chinese 兆 = 10¹² (modern), but Vietnamese "triệu" = 10⁶ (million!)
  - Source: "百万亿" → Target: Convert according to value: 百万亿 = 10¹⁴; Vietnamese would be "một trăm nghìn tỷ"
  - ⚠️ CRITICAL: Vietnamese "triệu" is 10⁶ (million), NOT the same as Chinese 兆 (10¹²). Never write Chinese 兆 as Vietnamese "triệu" — that would be wrong by a factor of 10⁶.

- **Chinese monetary units** → Vietnamese equivalents
  - Source: "人民币100元" → Target: "100 Nhân dân tệ (NDT)" / "100 NDT" / "100 RMB"
  - Source: "美元50元" → Target: "50 đô la Mỹ" / "50 USD"
  - Source: "欧元" → Target: "euro" (plural unchanged in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "港币" → Target: "đô la Hồng Kông" / "HKD"
  - Source: "日元" → Target: "yên Nhật" / "JPY"

### Decimal and Thousands Separators

- **Chinese decimal point (.)** → Vietnamese comma (,) — SAME AS CHINESE, no change needed
  - Source: "3.14" → Target: "3,14" (Vietnamese uses comma as decimal separator)
  - Source: "0.5%" → Target: "0,5%"
  - Note: This is actually the same in Chinese, but Vietnamese COMMA separates decimals unlike English. Both Chinese and Vietnamese use "." as decimal normally, but Vietnamese also uses "," in formal/scientific contexts.
  - CORRECTION: Modern Chinese uses "." as decimal. Vietnamese uses "," as decimal. So this IS a conversion: 3.14 → 3,14.

- **Chinese thousands separator (,)** → Vietnamese "." (period) or space
  - Source: "12,345" → Target: "12.345" (Vietnamese uses period as thousands separator)
  - Source: "12,345,678" → Target: "12.345.678"
  - Source: "1000000" → Target: "1.000.000" (Vietnamese adds separators even where Chinese omits them)
  - Alternative: "12 345" (space separator, less common but ISO-standard)

### Date Format Conversion

- **Chinese date format**: YYYY年M月D日 → Vietnamese: Ngày D tháng M năm YYYY or DD/MM/YYYY
  - Source: "2024年1月15日" → Target: "Ngày 15 tháng 01 năm 2024" or "15/01/2024"
  - Source: "2023年12月" → Target: "Tháng 12 năm 2023"
  - Source: "2024年第一季度" → Target: "Quý I năm 2024"
  - Note: Vietnamese date order is DD/MM/YYYY — same as European format, different from Chinese YYYY/MM/DD

- **Chinese numeric date 2024-01-15** → Vietnamese DD/MM/YYYY
  - Source: "2024-01-15" → Target: "15/01/2024" (always swap day-month order)
  - Source: "2024/01/15" → Target: "15/01/2024"

### Time References and Week Notation

- **Chinese week references** → Vietnamese days of the week
  - Source: "本周一" → Target: "Thứ Hai tuần này"
  - Source: "上周五" → Target: "Thứ Sáu tuần trước"
  - Source: "下周" → Target: "Tuần sau"
  - Source: "周三" → Target: "Thứ Tư"
  - Source: "周末" → Target: "Cuối tuần"

- **Chinese time format**: 24h → Vietnamese also 24h, direct mapping
  - Source: "下午3点" → Target: "15:00" / "3 giờ chiều" (both accepted, but 24h preferred in formal docs)
  - Source: "上午9时30分" → Target: "9:30" / "9 giờ 30 phút sáng"
  - Source: "晚上8点整" → Target: "20:00" / "8 giờ tối"
  - Note: Vietnamese uses "giờ" (hour), "phút" (minute), "giây" (second)

### Units and Measurement

- **Chinese measurement units** → Vietnamese (uses metric, SI units standard)
  - Source: "平方米" → Target: "mét vuông" / "m²"
  - Source: "公里" → Target: "ki-lô-mét" / "km"
  - Source: "公斤" → Target: "ki-lô-gam" / "kg"
  - Source: "斤" → Target: "cân" (1斤 = 500g = 0,5 kg) or "500 gam" (clarify if context requires)
  - Source: "亩" → Target: "mẫu Trung Quốc" (1亩 ≈ 666,67 m²; explain if needed)
  - Source: "寸" → Target: "thốn" (if literary) or "cm" with conversion note

### Financial Numerals

- **Chinese financial uppercase (壹贰叁)** → Vietnamese uses regular numerals or số từ (word form)
  - Source: "壹万元整" → Target: "Mười nghìn (10.000) đồng" / "Số tiền: 10.000 đồng"
  - Source: "人民币壹万元整" → Target: "10.000 Nhân dân tệ (NDT)" — Vietnamese does not have a parallel system of financial numerals; use standard digits + words in parentheses for clarity

- **Chinese percentage** → Vietnamese percentage (same % symbol)
  - Source: "百分之五十" → Target: "50%" / "năm mươi phần trăm"
  - Source: "年增长率百分之五" → Target: "tỷ lệ tăng trưởng hàng năm 5%"
  - Source: "千分之三" → Target: "3‰" / "ba phần nghìn" / "0,3%"

## Cultural References — Chuyển đổi văn hóa

### Chinese Idioms (成语) → Vietnamese Equivalents

- **Chinese 成语 → Vietnamese idiomatic equivalents (not literal)**
  - Source: "画蛇添足" (draw legs on a snake) → Target: "Vẽ rắn thêm chân" (direct loan, understood) or "Thừa thãi vô ích" (idiomatic meaning)
  - Source: "井底之蛙" (frog at bottom of well) → Target: "Ếch ngồi đáy giếng" (identical idiom exists in Vietnamese!)
  - Source: "对牛弹琴" (play music to a cow) → Target: "Đàn gảy tai trâu" (identical concept, different animal in Vietnamese: buffalo instead of cow)
  - Source: "如鱼得水" (like fish in water) → Target: "Như cá gặp nước" (identical)
  - Source: "半途而废" (give up halfway) → Target: "Bán đồ nhi phế" (Sino-Vietnamese) or "Bỏ dở nửa chừng" (native)
  - Source: "口是心非" (mouth says yes, heart says no) → Target: "Khẩu thị tâm phi" (Sino-Vietnamese) or "Nói một đằng làm một nẻo" (native)
  - Source: "居安思危" (in peace think of danger) → Target: "Cư an tư nguy" (Sino-Vietnamese, widely understood)
  - Source: "千军万马" (thousands of troops) → Target: "Thiên quân vạn mã" (Sino-Vietnamese reading, commonly used in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "独一无二" (unique, unmatched) → Target: "Độc nhất vô nhị" (Sino-Vietnamese, very common in Vietnamese)
  - Source: "四面楚歌" (besieged on all sides) → Target: "Tứ diện sở ca" (Sino-Vietnamese) or "Bị cô lập tứ phía" (native)
  - Source: "九牛一毛" (one hair from nine oxen) → Target: "Cửu ngưu nhất mao" (Sino-Vietnamese) or "Như muối bỏ biển" (native equivalent: like salt in the sea)
  - Source: "掩耳盗铃" (cover ears to steal bell) → Target: "Yểm nhĩ đạo linh" (Sino-Vietnamese) or "Tự lừa dối mình" (native meaning)

### Spacing and Typographic Conventions

- **Chinese spacing**: Chinese has no spaces between characters → Same for Vietnamese (no spaces between syllables in compound words)
  - Vietnamese uses spaces between words (each word can be multi-syllabic), which is fundamentally different from Chinese
  - Source: "国际贸易" → Target: "thương mại quốc tế" (two words, space between them)
  - Source: "经济发展" → Target: "phát triển kinh tế" (two words in reverse order)
  - Note: Vietnamese orthography separates words by spaces, so the target naturally inserts many spaces that were not present in Chinese

- **Chinese percent sign**: "50%" (no space) → Vietnamese "50%" (same, no space) or "50 %" (with space in some formal contexts)
  - Source: "50%" → Target: "50%" (general) or "50 %" (formal/technical)

- **Chinese number ranges**: "3-5天" → Vietnamese "3–5 ngày" (use en-dash for ranges)
  - Source: "2020-2023年" → Target: "Giai đoạn 2020–2023" / "từ năm 2020 đến năm 2023"

- **Chinese slash in dates**: "2020/01/15" → Vietnamese avoids slashes in text dates; use "15/01/2020" (slashes acceptable in numeric dates)

- **Chinese degrees**: "35°C" → Vietnamese "35°C" (same, but Vietnamese reads "độ C")

### Vietnamese-Specific Punctuation Notes

- **Vietnamese uses diacritics extensively**: Unlike Chinese, Vietnamese uses Latin script with 5 tone marks and various diacritics. Punctuation must be placed correctly relative to accented characters.
  - Rule: Periods, commas, and other punctuation should follow Vietnamese typographic conventions — same position as English/Latin text.
  - ư, ơ, â, ê, ô, ă, đ — these characters must be preserved in the output

- **Vietnamese word breaks**: Vietnamese is a multisyllabic language where words are separated by spaces (each word is typically 1-2 syllables). This is fundamentally different from Chinese character-level writing.
  - Source: "经济发展战略" → Target: "chiến lược phát triển kinh tế" (each Vietnamese word separated by space)
  - Source: "世界贸易组织" → Target: "Tổ chức Thương mại Thế giới (WTO)"

- **Vietnamese uses the comma before "và" (and)** similar to the Oxford comma — optional but consistent within document
  - Source: "苹果、香蕉和橘子" → Target: "táo, chuối và cam" (comma before "và" is optional)
  - Source: "北京、上海和广州" → Target: "Bắc Kinh, Thượng Hải và Quảng Châu"
