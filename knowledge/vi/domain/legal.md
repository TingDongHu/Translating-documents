## Reader Model

**Who reads this document and what do they expect?**

Legal document readers in the Vietnamese market are lawyers (luat su), judges (tham phan), corporate legal counsel (phap che doanh nghiep), notaries (cong chung vien), regulatory officials and contract managers. They read the document to determine legal rights, obligations, and consequences under Vietnamese law (phap luat Viet Nam). They expect:

- **Precision in legal terminology** — Vietnamese legal language is codified in the system of legislative documents (van ban quy pham phap luat). Terms must match those used in the Luat, Nghi dinh, and Thong tu governing the subject matter. A non-standard term can render a clause ambiguous or invalid.
- **Hierarchy-aware drafting** — Vietnamese law operates on a strict hierarchy: Hien phap > Luat > Nghi dinh > Thong tu. References to legal instruments must accurately reflect this hierarchy and cite the correct document type. Misclassifying a Thong tu as a Nghi dinh changes the legal weight of the reference.
- **Formal and respectful register** — Vietnamese legal language uses formal constructions, respectful address terms, and classical Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary (tu Han Viet) for legal concepts. Colloquial or informal language undermines the document's authority.
- **Structural integrity** — Vietnamese contracts and legal documents follow established structural conventions: dieu (articles), khoan (clauses), diem (sub-clauses). The numbering hierarchy must be preserved exactly, as cross-references within the document depend on it.
- **Notarization and certification awareness** — Many Vietnamese legal documents require notarization (cong chung) or certification (chung thuc) to be legally effective. The translation must preserve references to these formalities.

**What would break their trust?**
- A mistranslated obligation that changes which party bears risk or liability under Vietnamese law.
- An incorrect Luat/Nghi dinh/Thong tu reference that cites a wrong or non-existent legal instrument.
- A defined term used inconsistently throughout the document, creating ambiguity.
- An error in dates, contract value (written in words), or signature block that could invalidate the document or delay notarization.
- Use of a term from one area of law (e.g., term from Luat Dat dai) in a context governed by another area (e.g., Luat Doanh nghiep), creating legal confusion.

## Decision Framework

### 1. Vietnamese Legal Hierarchy (He thong van ban quy pham phap luat)

| Document Type | Vietnamese Term | Legal Force | Translation Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Constitution | Hien phap | Highest legal document | Always capitalize: "Hien phap". Translate as "Constitution" in English source context; preserve as "Hien phap" in Vietnamese output. |
| Law / Code | Luat / Bo luat | Passed by Quoc hoi (National Assembly) | "Luat" translates to "Law"; "Bo luat" to "Code" (e.g., Bo luat Dan su = Civil Code). Always use the official Vietnamese title. |
| Resolution (of National Assembly) | Nghi quyet | Passed by Quoc hoi | "Nghi quyet" translates to "Resolution". Cite with number and year. |
| Ordinance | Phap lenh | Passed by Uy ban Thuong vu Quoc hoi | Less common since 2016; used when National Assembly is not in session. |
| Decree | Nghi dinh | Issued by Chinh phu (Government) | "Nghi dinh" translates to "Decree". Detailed implementing regulations of laws. Format: Nghi dinh so XX/YYYY/ND-CP. |
| Decision (of Prime Minister) | Quyet dinh | Issued by Thu tuong Chinh phu | "Quyet dinh" translates to "Decision". Individual or normative decisions. |
| Circular | Thong tu | Issued by Bo truong (Minister) | "Thong tu" translates to "Circular". Detailed guidance from ministries. Format: Thong tu so XX/YYYY/TT-B... |
| Joint Circular | Thong tu lien tich | Issued by multiple ministries | "Thong tu lien tich" translates to "Joint Circular". Being phased out per 2015 law. |
| Provincial Decision | Quyet dinh (UBND) | Issued by UBND cap tinh | Provincial-level People's Committee decisions. |
| Communique / Official Dispatch | Cong van | Issued by administrative agencies | "Cong van" is an administrative document, not a legislative instrument. |

### 2. Obligation and Prohibition Language in Vietnamese Legal Context

| Source Intent | Vietnamese Decision | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Binding obligation (shall/must) | "phai" or "co nghia vu" | "Phai" for direct obligations. "Co nghia vu" for legal duty. "Co trach nhiem" for responsibility. |
| Strong prohibition (shall not) | "khong duoc" or "nghiem cam" | "Khong duoc" standard. "Nghiem cam" for absolute prohibitions in regulatory texts. |
| Permission / right (may) | "duoc" or "co quyen" | "Duoc" for passive permission. "Co quyen" for active right. |
| Entitlement | "duoc huong" or "co quyen huong" | Used for benefits, compensation, entitlements. |
| Conditional obligation | "với dieu kien la" or "neu...thi" | "Voi dieu kien rang" for "provided that". "Truong hop...thi" for "in case of". |
| Exemption / exception | "duoc mien tru" or "ngoai le" | "Mien tru" for exemption from obligation. "Ngoai le" for exception to a rule. |

### 6. References to Legal Instruments in Vietnamese

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Full citation of a Vietnamese law | Format: "Luat (Name) nam YYYY" (e.g., "Luat Doanh nghiep nam 2020"). Law numbers: "Luat so XX/YYYY/QH[term]" |
| Decree citation | Format: "Nghi dinh so XX/YYYY/ND-CP ngay DD/MM/YYYY cua Chinh phu quy dinh ve..." |
| Circular citation | Format: "Thong tu so XX/YYYY/TT-B... ngay DD/MM/YYYY cua Bo... quy dinh ve..." |
| Citation by abbreviated name only | Many Vietnamese laws are known by short names (e.g., "Luat Doanh nghiep"). Preserve the short name. First mention must include the full official title and number. |
| Reference to a specific article/clause | Format: "Dieu X Khoan Y Diem Z" (e.g., "Dieu 15 Khoan 2 Diem a"). This is the standard Vietnamese legal citation format. |
| Reference to an international treaty to which Vietnam is a party | Include the Vietnamese ratification document: "Cong uoc... ma Viet Nam la thanh vien theo Quyet dinh so..." |

## Error Pattern Library

### Critical Errors (zero tolerance)

| Error | Why It Is Critical |
|---|---|
| **Misclassifying the legal instrument type** | Calling a "Nghi dinh" a "Thong tu" (or vice versa) changes the legal authority of the reference. Vietnamese courts and regulators check the document type against the legal hierarchy. |
| **Mistranslating obligation level (phai/duoc/khong duoc)** | Swapping an obligation ("phai") for permission ("duoc") or prohibition ("khong duoc") reverses the legal effect of a clause. |
| **Incorrect Luat/Nghi dinh number or year** | Citing the wrong law number or year (e.g., "Luat Doanh nghiep 2014" instead of "Luat Doanh nghiep 2020") references a repealed or outdated law. |
| **Defined term inconsistency** | A defined term (e.g., "Ben A") must be rendered identically every time. Variation breaks the cross-reference system and creates ambiguity. |
| **Contract value in words differs from numerals** | In Vietnamese contracts, a value written in words (chu) and numerals (so) must match exactly. A discrepancy can be exploited to argue the contract is ambiguous. |
| **Signature block errors** | Omitting the title, legal representative designation, or company seal reference can prevent a Vietnamese contract from being validly executed. |

### Common Errors (frequent in practice)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Anglicized legal structures** | Vietnamese contracts do not use "Whereas" clauses. Use "Can cu" (Based on) to introduce the legal basis. Do not impose common law contract structure on a civil law document. |
| **Informal register** | Vietnamese legal language uses formal vocabulary (tu Han Viet). Avoid everyday Vietnamese synonyms. Use "thanh toan" not "tra tien", "thuc hien" not "lam", "uy quyen" not "nho". |
| **False equivalence of entity types** | "Cong ty TNHH" is NOT the same as "LLC" in all respects. Keep the Vietnamese entity type and explain in parentheses rather than substituting the English form. |
| **Inconsistent numbering format** | Vietnamese contracts use: Dieu -> Khoan -> Diem. Do not mix "Section" with "Dieu" or "Clause" with "Khoan" within the same document. |
| **Untranslated legal maxims** | Latin legal terms (force majeure, in rem, prima facie) are used in international contracts. When translating to Vietnamese, preserve the Latin term and add the Vietnamese equivalent in parentheses. |

### Subtle Errors (easy to miss)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Sino-Vietnamese vs. native Vietnamese register** | "Thuc hien" (Sino-Vietnamese) is legal; "lam" (native) is informal. Legal texts strongly prefer Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary. "Thanh toan" beats "tra tien". "Kien nghi" beats "de nghi" in formal documents. |
| **Date format in legal context** | Vietnamese date format: "ngay DD thang MM nam YYYY" (ngay 18 thang 5 nam 2026). Do not use the English order. Contract effective dates are critical. |
| **Singular vs. plural ambiguity** | Vietnamese does not mark plurals grammatically. Context determines quantity. In legal translation, clarify whether "Ben" means one party, all parties, or both parties when needed. |
| **Modifier placement in Vietnamese legal phrases** | Vietnamese modifiers follow the head noun. "Nghia vu thanh toan cua Ben A" = "Obligation to pay of Party A" rather than "Party A's obligation to pay." Preserve Vietnamese word order. |
| **Compound legal terms from overlapping regulations** | The same concept may be defined differently in Luat Dat dai and Luat Nha o. Use the definition consistent with the subject matter of the document. |
