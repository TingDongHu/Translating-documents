## Reader Model

**Who reads this document and what do they expect?**

Administrative document readers in the Vietnamese context are government officials (can bo cong chuc), civil servants (vien chuc nha nuoc), administrative officers (chanh van phong, thu ky), and managers in state-owned enterprises (doanh nghiep nha nuoc) and administrative agencies (co quan hanh chinh). They read the document for instruction, reporting, record-keeping, regulatory compliance, and administrative decision-making. They expect:

- **Rigorous adherence to Cong van format** — Vietnamese administrative documents follow a highly standardized format prescribed by the Government (Chinh phu) in Nghi dinh 30/2020/ND-CP on the promulgation of administrative document standards. The format includes the agency name (Ten co quan), document number (So cong van), date, recipient, subject (V/v), body, and signature block with seal (dau). Deviating from this format signals inexperience or lack of authority.
- **Formal and impersonal register** — Vietnamese administrative language (ngon ngu hanh chinh) is highly formal, impersonal, and uses standardized phrases. The language must be neutral, objective, and authoritative. Emotional or personal language is inappropriate.
- **Hierarchy awareness** — Administrative documents move between levels of government (cap tren, cap duoi). The language must respect the hierarchical relationship. Instructions from upper level (cap tren) use directive language; reports to upper level use respectful and formal language.
- **Precise reference to other documents** — Vietnamese administrative documents frequently reference other documents by their full official number and date (e.g., "Can cu cong van so 1234/UBND-NC ngay 15/5/2026"). These references must be rendered exactly.
- **Proper seal and signature conventions** — Vietnamese administrative documents have specific rules about where the seal (con dau) is placed, how signatures are formatted (da ky: signed; thay mat: on behalf of; thua lenh: by order of), and how multiple signatories are listed.

**What would break their trust?**
- An incorrect document number format that makes the document traceability impossible.
- A missing or misplaced "Kinh gui" (recipient) line that causes the document to be routed incorrectly.
- An informal or incorrect closing phrase that disrespects the hierarchical relationship.
- A signature block that does not show the correct authorizing position (chuc vu) or seal location.
- A reference to a law or regulation that uses the wrong instrument type (e.g., "Theo Luan" instead of "Theo Nghi dinh").

### 2. Opening and Closing Formulas for Administrative Documents

| Document Type | Opening Formula | Closing Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Official dispatch / Cong van | "Kinh gui: ..." (Recipient) | "Tran trong." / "Kinh bao." / "De nghi Quy vi..." | Standard for all administrative documents |
| Report from lower to upper level | "Kinh gui: ..." | "Tran trong bao cao." / "Xin trinh." | Highly respectful language |
| Directive from upper level | "Can cu..." (legal basis) then directive | "De nghi Thuy truong... chi dao thuc hien." | Authoritative, clear instructions |
| Response / Reply | "Tra loi cong van so..." | "Tran trong thong bao." / "Tran trong." | Acknowledges received document |
| Notification | "Nham thong bao ve..." | "Tran trong thong bao." / "De nghi cac don vi..." | Information dissemination |
| Proposal / Request | "Nham... (purpose), kinh de nghi..." | "Kinh mong Quy vi xem xet, quyet dinh." | Respectful, deferential |

### 3. Recipient Address Hierarchy (Kinh gui conventions)

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Document addressed to multiple agencies | List recipients in hierarchical order: higher-level agencies first, then lower-level, internal units last. Example: "Kinh gui: - Bo Noi vu; - So Noi vu tinh..." |
| Document addressed to an individual position | Use their official title: "Kinh gui: Giam doc So Tai chinh" or "Kinh gui: Ong Giam doc So Tai chinh". |
| Internal distribution within agency | "Kinh gui: - Lanh dao co quan; - Cac phong, ban; - Luu: VT" |
| Urgent document marking | Top of document: "CONG VAN HOI KHA" (Urgent) or "CONG VAN MAT" (Confidential) — these are standardized markings |
| CC / Carbon copy distribution | "Noi nhan:" at the bottom of the document lists where copies are sent |

### 6. Signature and Seal Conventions

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Document signed directly by the authorized person | "CHU TICH" / "THU TRUONG" / title in all caps. Signature above the name. Under the signature: position title, full name. |
| Document signed on behalf of the leader ("Thay mat") | "TM." before the title. E.g., "TM. UY BAN NHAN DAN". Indicates the signatory acts on behalf of the body. |
| Document signed by order ("Thua lenh") | "TL." before the title. E.g., "TL. CHU TICH". Lower-level signing by delegation. |
| Document signed by deputy ("Phó") | "KT. [Title]" + signed position "(Đã ký)" or "Pho [Title]". E.g., "KT. CHU TICH - PHO CHU TICH" |
| Document approved through e-signature | "(Đa ky)" or "(Đa ky so)" to indicate electronic signature |
| Seal (con dau) placement | The seal is placed covering the left portion of the signature, overlapping the signatory's name. The seal must be clearly legible. |
| Seal type | "Dau mo" (wet ink stamp) or "Dau so" (digital seal). Vietnamese law recognizes both. |
| Seal registration | Each agency's seal is registered with the police (Cong an). The seal registration number (So dang ky mau dau) may be referenced. |
| No seal on copy | Copies: "Van ban sao" (certified copy) marked at the top. "SAO Y BAN CHINH" for exact copy certification. |

### 7. Legal Basis Citations in Administrative Documents

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Citing the legal authority for the document | "Can cu" (Based on) + full law/decree reference. This is the standard opening for Vietnamese administrative documents. |
| Multi-level legal basis | List from highest to lowest: Can cu Hien phap -> Can cu Luat -> Can cu Nghi dinh -> Can cu Thong tu -> Can cu Quyet dinh |
| Citing multiple documents in one opening | Number them: "Can cu Luat To chuc Chinh phu nam 2015; Can cu Nghi dinh so XX/YYYY/ND-CP..." |
| Reference to the document being responded to | "Thuc hien cong van so... ngay... cua..." or "Tra loi cong van so..." |
| Reference to an attached document | "Kem theo: ..." (Attached: ...). Attachments are listed by number and title. |
| Effective date of a cited document | Always include the date of the document being cited: "Cong van so 1234/UBND-KT ngay 15/5/2026" |

### 12. Urgent and Confidential Document Handling

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Document marked "Khan" (Urgent) | Preserve the marking at the top. Vietnamese: "CONG VAN KHAN" in bold. The response timeline is implied: urgent documents must be processed within 24 hours. |
| Document marked "Mat" (Confidential) | Preserve the classification. Do not translate or downgrade the classification level. Vietnamese agencies use specific classification colors (red for secret, etc.). |
| Document with restricted distribution | "Luu hanh noi bo" (Internal circulation) must be preserved. These documents are not for public disclosure. |
| Encrypted or digitally signed document | References to "chu ky so" (digital signature) or "ma hoa" (encryption) must be preserved. |
| Document with access list | "Danh sach nguoi duoc phep xem" (List of authorized viewers) if present, must be meticulously preserved. |

## Error Pattern Library

### Critical Errors (zero tolerance)

| Error | Why It Is Critical |
|---|---|
| **Misplaced or missing seal notation** | In Vietnamese administrative documents, the seal (con dau) is a legal requirement. A document without a seal or with the seal notation wrong may be considered invalid (Khong co gia tri phap ly). |
| **Incorrect recipient in "Kinh gui"** | Sending an administrative document to the wrong recipient causes it to be routed incorrectly, missed, or returned. The "Kinh gui" line is the only routing instruction. |
| **Formal register breached with informal language** | Vietnamese administrative documents demand impersonal, formal register. Using "cac ban" instead of "quy vi" or "gui" instead of "kinh gui" signals incompetence. |
| **Document number format wrong** | The "So: XXX/..." format is the official document identifier used for filing, tracking, and reference. An incorrect number makes the document untraceable in the Vietnamese administrative system. |
| **"Can cu" (legal basis) reference incorrect** | The legal basis stated in "Can cu" is the authority for the entire document. If the referenced law or decree is wrong, expired, or misquoted, the document has no legal foundation. |
| **Signature block position/title omitted** | Every Vietnamese administrative document must show the signatory's official title in the signature block. Without the title, the document lacks authorization. |

### Common Errors (frequent in practice)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Informal closing of a formal document** | Closing with "Cam on" (Thank you) is too informal for most administrative documents. Use "Tran trong." or "Kinh bao." instead. |
| **Abbreviation of document type not recognized** | "CV" (Cong van), "QD" (Quyet dinh), "BC" (Bao cao) are standard administrative abbreviations. Use them consistently in document numbers and references. |
| **Order of recipients incorrect** | List higher-level agencies first, then peers, then subordinate agencies. The Vietnamese administrative culture is hierarchy-sensitive. |
| **Tone inappropriate for upper-level communication** | Documents sent UP the hierarchy should use more deferential language. "Kinh trinh" instead of "Yeu cau". Use "kinh mong" for requests. |
| **Missing "Noi nhan" (distribution) block** | Every administrative document must show where copies are sent: "- Nhu tren, - Bo phan..., - Luu: VT". Without this, the filing department cannot properly distribute the document. |

### Subtle Errors (easy to miss)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **TL. / TM. / KT. prefix in signature block** | "TL." (Thua lenh - by order), "TM." (Thay mat - on behalf of), "KT." (Ky thay - signed for) are different delegation types. Using the wrong prefix misrepresents the signatory's authority. |
| **"Da ky" notation for electronic signatures** | "(Da ky)" or "(Da ky so)" indicates that the document was signed electronically. This should NOT be marked as a physical signature. |
| **Quotation conventions in document citation** | Vietnamese administrative documents cite references in a specific way. "Cong van so X ngay Y" uses "ngay" not "ngay thang nam" in abbreviated form. |
| **Vietnamese diacritics in official names** | All official agency names (Ten co quan) must be written with full Vietnamese diacritics (tonal marks). "UBND Thanh pho Ha Noi" not "UBND thanh pho ha noi". |
| **Redundancy in subject line** | The subject line (V/v:) should be concise: "V/v: Bao cao ket qua cong tac thang 5/2026". Do not add "Ve viec ve" (double subject marking) or other redundant phrases. |
| **Date in "ngay/thang/nam" format** | Vietnamese administrative documents use the format "ngay ... thang ... nam ..." (in words, with no leading zeros). Never use numeric slashes for the date in the document header. |
