## General Principles

- Adapt tone to English business conventions — direct, professional, concise
- Marketing content should be localized, not just translated
- Preserve brand names and trademarks exactly
- Convert measurements and currencies to English conventions where appropriate
- Preserve contact details (phone numbers, addresses, email) in original form

## Business Correspondence

- Use standard English business letter format
- "Dear Sir or Madam" for salutations when the recipient is unknown
- "Best regards" / "Kind regards" for closings
- Translate formal letterhead/company registration info accurately

## Marketing and Proposals

- Adapt idioms and slogans — do not translate literally
- Maintain persuasive tone while being culturally appropriate
- Convert any local references to internationally understandable equivalents
- Feature-benefit structure: translate features exactly, adapt benefits for target market

## Advertising Copy

### Core Principle
Advertising translation = transcreation, NOT literal translation.
Goal: produce the same emotional impact and persuasiveness in the target market.

### Common Scenarios
1. **Product efficacy claims**: Preserve the force of hyperbole, but comply with target market advertising regulations
2. **User reviews / testimonials**: Keep colloquial, emotional tone — do not formalize
3. **Promotional offers**: Numbers and discounts must be exact; copy style can be localized
4. **Brand stories**: Adapt narrative style to target culture; core brand values remain unchanged

### Brand Name Strategy
- Keep established brand names in their original form (Apple, Nike, Haier, etc.)
- For region-specific brand names: use the established English form if one exists, otherwise keep original
- Brand slogans: Recreate in target language, never translate literally

### Homophonic Puns in Source Text
If the source text contains homophonic wordplay (common in Chinese advertising):
1. Decode the intended meaning (not the surface characters)
2. Preserve the brand name via transliteration
3. Rewrite the advertising message using equivalent rhetorical devices in the target language
4. See also: rules/base.md "Creative Language Strategies"

## Commercial Contracts

### Key Clause Types
- "包括但不限于" → "including but not limited to"
- "在合理可行的范围内" → "to the extent reasonably practicable"
- "在不影响...的前提下" → "subject to" / "provided that"
- "尽管有上述规定" → "notwithstanding the foregoing"

### Price / Payment Terms
- Net / Gross: translate based on whether tax is included or excluded
- Payment terms: "net 30", "due on receipt", etc.
- Discounts: "early payment discount", "volume discount"
- Penalties: "late payment penalties"

### Warranty / Guarantee
- Translate warranty periods and conditions exactly
- "保修" in product context → "warranty" (not "guarantee")
- Service contact information: preserve in original

## E-commerce / Digital Marketing

### Product Descriptions
- Feature lists: keep concise, punchy, scannable
- SEO keywords: find equivalent high-search-volume terms in the target language
- User reviews: preserve colloquial style, do not formalize

### Platform-specific Language
- Product titles: follow target platform character limits and conventions
- Bullet points: start with benefit, not feature
- Call-to-action: adapt to target market conventions ("Shop now")
