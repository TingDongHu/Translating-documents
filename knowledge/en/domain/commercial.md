## Reader Model

**Who reads this document and what do they expect?**

Commercial document readers are business executives, procurement officers, sales managers, and legal representatives who make decisions based on the content. They expect:

- **Clarity on obligations** — who must do what, by when, and at what cost. Ambiguity in commitments leads to disputes.
- **Precision on deadlines** — delivery dates, payment terms, and validity periods must be exact and unambiguous.
- **Clarity on financial commitments** — pricing, discounts, penalties, and currency must be consistent and unmistakable.
- **Professional tone** — direct, concise, and respectful. Business readers have no patience for inflated or vague language.
- **Brand integrity** — marketing materials must preserve brand voice and persuasive impact while being culturally appropriate.

**What would break their trust?**
- A mistranslated Incoterm that changes risk allocation between buyer and seller.
- An inconsistent price or quantity that suggests a calculation error.
- A missing or ambiguous delivery deadline.
- A translated brand name or slogan that dilutes brand recognition.

## Decision Framework

### 1. Obligation and Requirement Language

| Source Pattern | Decision |
|---|---|
| Strong obligation (equivalent of "shall") | Use "shall" — this establishes a binding contractual duty. |
| Medium obligation (equivalent of "must") | Use "must" for obligations imposed by external requirements (laws, standards). |
| Permission (equivalent of "may") | Use "may" — this grants discretion, not a duty. |
| Prohibition (equivalent of "shall not") | Use "shall not" or "must not" depending on obligation strength. |
| Recommendation (equivalent of "should") | Use "should" — this is aspirational, not binding. |

### 2. Company Legal Forms

| Source Entity Type | English Treatment |
|---|---|
| Limited liability entity | Use "Limited" or "Ltd." after the company name. |
| Joint-stock entity | Use "Corporation" or "Inc." or the closest recognized equivalent. |
| Partnership entity | Use "Partnership" or "LLP" as appropriate. |
| Sole proprietorship | Use "Sole Proprietor" or describe according to business structure. |
| Entity type abbreviation with no direct English equivalent | Keep the original abbreviation and add the English description in parentheses on first use (e.g., "AB (joint-stock company)"). |

### 3. Commercial Quantities

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Quantity expressed in original unit | Preserve the exact quantity. Convert unit only if the commercial standard in the target market uses a different unit and the client has authorized conversion. |
| Packaging quantities (dozen, ream, gross) | Use the equivalent English commercial term. The numerical value must remain exact. |
| Volume-based pricing (per ton, per cubic meter) | Preserve the unit. Add the equivalent in the target market convention in parentheses if requested. |

### 4. Incoterms

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Incoterm appears in the source | Preserve the Incoterm exactly as it appears (e.g., FOB, CIF, EXW). Incoterms are standardized and must not be translated. |
| Incoterm includes a location | Preserve the location exactly. Do not translate port or place names. |
| Incoterm version year is specified | Preserve the year reference (e.g., "Incoterms 2020"). |

## Error Pattern Library

### Critical Errors (zero tolerance)

| Error | Why It Is Critical |
|---|---|
| **Translating legal entity types** | Converting a foreign legal entity abbreviation to a domestic equivalent changes the legal nature of the entity. Keep the original abbreviation; explain in parentheses. |
| **Incorrect Incoterm** | Incoterms allocate risk, cost, and responsibility. Mistranslation can lead to financial loss. |
| **Inconsistent pricing information** | Any discrepancy in unit price, total, discount, or currency between sections makes the document unreliable. |
| **Missing or ambiguous delivery date** | A commercial contract without a clear deadline is unenforceable. |

### Common Errors (frequent in practice)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Inconsistent use of "shall / must / may"** | Use "shall" for contractual duties, "must" for external requirements, "may" for permission. Do not treat them as interchangeable. |
| **Oversimplified transcreation** | Marketing copy needs creative adaptation, not word-for-word translation. But distinguishing between a translation error and a creative choice requires explicit marking. |
| **Mixed register in business correspondence** | Business letters should maintain consistent formality. Do not switch between "Dear Sir or Madam" and a casual opening. |

### Subtle Errors (easy to miss)

| Error | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Date format leading to ambiguity** | Use unambiguous date formats: "30 April 2025" rather than "04/05/2025" which is interpreted differently across locales. |
| **Currency confusion (symbol vs code)** | Use the three-letter ISO currency code (USD, EUR, GBP) in formal documents. The symbol ($, EUR, GBP) may be ambiguous ($ could be USD, CAD, AUD, etc.). |
| **Decimal and thousands separator in pricing** | A price written as 1.500 in some locales means one thousand five hundred; in others it means one point five. Convert unambiguously per English conventions. |

## Domain-Specific Reference

### Standard Incoterms 2020

| Code | Full Name | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| EXW | Ex Works | Seller makes goods available at own premises |
| FCA | Free Carrier | Seller delivers goods to carrier at named place |
| FAS | Free Alongside Ship | Seller places goods alongside vessel |
| FOB | Free On Board | Seller loads goods on board vessel |
| CFR | Cost and Freight | Seller covers cost and freight to destination port |
| CIF | Cost, Insurance and Freight | Seller covers cost, freight, and insurance |
| CPT | Carriage Paid To | Seller pays carriage to named destination |
| CIP | Carriage and Insurance Paid To | Seller pays carriage and insurance to destination |
| DAP | Delivered at Place | Seller delivers to named place, buyer handles import |
| DPU | Delivered at Place Unloaded | Seller delivers and unloads at named place |
| DDP | Delivered Duty Paid | Seller delivers cleared for import |

### Payment Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Net 30 | Payment due 30 days after invoice date |
| Net 60 | Payment due 60 days after invoice date |
| Due on receipt | Payment due immediately upon receipt of invoice |
| Cash in advance (CIA) | Payment required before goods are shipped |
| Cash on delivery (COD) | Payment due at time of delivery |
| Letter of credit (L/C) | Bank guarantees payment upon presentation of documents |
| Documents against payment (D/P) | Documents released to buyer upon payment |
| Documents against acceptance (D/A) | Documents released upon acceptance of draft |
