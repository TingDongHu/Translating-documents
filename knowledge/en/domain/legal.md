## General Principles

- Use formal register throughout
- Prefer precise legal terminology over common-language equivalents
- Maintain the logical structure of legal arguments (whereas, therefore, provided that)
- Preserve numbered/clause references exactly (Article 1, Section 2.3, etc.)
- Use "shall" for obligations, "may" for permissions, "shall not" for prohibitions

## Business Entity Types

- Translate legal entity types to closest English equivalent:
  - "有限责任公司" → "Limited Liability Company (LLC)"
  - "股份有限公司" → "Joint-Stock Limited Company"
  - "合伙企业" → "Partnership"
  - Note: exact equivalents may not exist across jurisdictions — add note on first mention
- Keep registration numbers in original form

## Signature Blocks

- Translate signatory titles: "法定代表人" → "Legal Representative"
- "签字" → "Signature", "日期" → "Date"
- "代表公司" → "For and on behalf of the company"
- Signature lines: preserve format, do not translate names

## Dates in Legal Context

- Always preserve exact dates
- "自...起生效" → "effective from" / "effective as of"
- "期限为" → "for a period of"
- Translate date format per target locale

## Legal Ambiguity Handling

### Polysemous Terms
Legal terms often have multiple meanings — determine from context:
- "条款" → clause / term / provision (depends on context)
- "责任" → liability / responsibility / duty
- "权利人" → right holder / obligee
- "义务人" → obligor

Strategy:
1. Analyze the legal intent of the clause
2. Check other usages in the same document for consistency
3. If truly ambiguous, keep the original + note possible meanings

### Jurisdiction-specific Concepts
The same concept may not be equivalent across legal systems:
- Chinese "有限责任公司" ≠ French "SARL" (different management rules)
- Chinese "法定代表人" is a China-specific concept

Strategy:
1. Use the closest equivalent term in the target language
2. On first occurrence, add a note explaining the difference
3. Do NOT invent legal concepts

### Bilingual Contract Formatting
- Preserve clause numbering correspondence across both languages
- "以英文为准" / "如中英文版本有歧义，以英文为准" → "the English version shall prevail"
- Defined terms must be strictly consistent across both language versions
- Cross-references must match the target language clause numbering
