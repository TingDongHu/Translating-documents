## Formality and Address

- Chinese formal address (您) → English does not distinguish formal/informal "you"
- Chinese formal closings → English formal closings ("Yours sincerely")
- Chinese titles → English equivalents

## Business Culture

- Chinese business writing is often indirect — make English version more direct
- Chinese passive constructions are rare — restructure for natural English
- Chinese bureaucratic style → English professional style

## Number and Date Conventions

- Chinese 万 (10,000) → "10,000" or "ten thousand"
- Chinese 亿 (100,000,000) → "100,000,000" or "one hundred million"
- Chinese date format (年月日) → English format (DD/MM/YYYY or Month DD, YYYY)

## Cultural References

- Chinese holidays, cultural events → keep as-is, add brief explanation if needed
- Chinese geographic references → use standard English spelling (Beijing, Shanghai)
- Chinese historical references → keep as-is, use established English translations

## Creative Language Transcoding

### Homophonic Puns (谐音梗)
Chinese homophonic puns have no direct English equivalent. Translation strategies:
- **Brand names**: Transliterate + explanatory translation
- **Advertising slogans**: Recreate using English pun / rhyme / alliteration
- **Cultural memes**: Keep original + cultural annotation

### Chinese Rhetorical Devices → English Equivalents
- Parallelism (对仗) → English parallel structure
- Repetition (排比) → English anaphora
- Hyperbole (夸张) → Preserve the force, use English equivalent expressions
- Classical allusions (引经据典) → If the target reader is unfamiliar, annotate or substitute with an English allusion

### Chinese Business Culture
- "关系" (guanxi) → business relationships / connections (do NOT simply translate as "relationship" — it implies a deeper network of mutual obligation)
- "面子" (mianzi) → reputation / face — understand its social weight in business contexts
- "酒桌文化" → business dining culture — handle only when it appears in the document

### China-specific Terminology
- "双碳目标" → dual carbon goals (carbon peak + carbon neutrality)
- "乡村振兴" → rural revitalization
- "一带一路" → Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)
- "内卷" → involution / rat race (choose based on register)
- "出海" → going global / overseas expansion (business context)
- "新质生产力" → new quality productive forces
- "共同富裕" → common prosperity

## Chinese Administrative Language

- "特此通知" → "We hereby inform you"
- "经研究决定" → "After deliberation, it has been decided that"
- "望周知" → "Please be informed"
- "遵照执行" → "Comply and implement"
- "有关部门" → "relevant authorities"

## Chinese Legal/Official Style

- Chinese legal documents use parallel structure extensively — maintain this in English
- Chinese formal documents often state the authority first → English reorders for clarity
- "根据...规定" → "In accordance with..." / "Pursuant to..."
- "鉴于" → "Whereas" / "Given that"
- "特此" → "hereby" (formal legal English)
