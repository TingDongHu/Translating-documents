## Formality and Address

- German "Sie" (formal) → English does not distinguish formal/informal "you"
- German formal closings ("Mit freundlichen Grüßen") → "Yours sincerely" / "Kind regards"
- German titles (Herr, Frau) → Mr., Mrs., Ms.

## Business Culture

- German compound nouns → break into English phrases
- German bureaucratic style → English direct style
- German passive constructions → prefer English active voice

## Legal References

- German legal system (BGB, HGB) → keep German abbreviations, add English explanation
- "GmbH" → "Limited Liability Company" or keep "GmbH"
- "AG" → "Corporation" or keep "AG"

## Cultural References

- German geographic references → use standard English spelling
- German cultural events → keep as-is

## Compound Nouns (Komposita)

German forms long compound nouns that must be broken into English phrases:
- "Betriebsunterbrechungsversicherung" → "business interruption insurance"
- "Handelsregisterauszug" → "trade register extract"
- "Gewerbeanmeldung" → "business registration"

Strategy: Identify the root noun and its modifiers, then construct a natural English phrase. Do NOT create English compound nouns.

## Engineering and Technical Communication Style

German technical writing is extremely precise and structured:
- Long, nested sentences are normal — break into shorter English sentences
- Passive voice is preferred in specifications — maintain this in English
- Numbered paragraphs (§) are standard in contracts and regulations
- DIN standard references should be preserved exactly (e.g., "DIN EN ISO 9001")

## Regional Variations

- Austrian German (Österreichisches Deutsch): Some terms differ from German German
  - "Jänner" → "January" (Austrian) vs "Januar" (German)
  - "Erdapfel" → "potato" (Austrian) vs "Kartoffel" (German)
- Swiss German (Schweizerdeutsch): Written documents use Standard German (Hochdeutsch)
- Translate based on the source country, not a generic "German"

## German Administrative Language

- "Hiermit bescheinigen wir" → "We hereby certify"
- "Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren" → "Dear Sir or Madam"
- "Anlage" → "Enclosure" / "Attachment"
- "Betreff" → "Subject" / "Re:"
- "Zeichen" → "Reference" / "File reference"
- "Freistellung" → "exemption" (tax context) / "release" (employment context)

## Number and Date Conventions

- German number format (1.234,56) → English format (1,234.56)
- German date format (DD.MM.YYYY) → English format (MM/DD/YYYY or Month DD, YYYY)
- German decimal comma → English decimal point
- German uses period as thousands separator → English comma
