# Finnish Administrative Domain Translation

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Finnish government officials (virkamiehet), ministry staff (ministeriön virkamiehet), municipal administrators
- **Secondary Reader**: EU institution staff, public sector managers, civil servants (virkamiehet)
- **Tertiary Reader**: Citizens (kansalaiset), businesses interacting with government, international organizations

### Reader Expectations
1. **Official Language Style**: Finnish administrative language (virastokieli) uses formal, precise constructions with passive voice.
2. **Legislative References**: All references to laws must use correct Finnish titles and section numbers.
3. **EU Correspondence**: EU documents follow bilingual (Finnish/Swedish) Finnish conventions; EU terminology must align with IATE (Inter-Active Terminology for Europe).
4. **Administrative Precision**: Titles, ranks, and positions must use correct Finnish forms.
5. **Bilingual Awareness**: Finland is officially bilingual (Finnish/Swedish); some documents require awareness of this.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|-------------|------------|---------------------|
| Official language style compliance | Critical | Rejection by authorities |
| Correct law references | Critical | Legal ambiguity |
| EU terminology alignment | High | Non-compliance with EU standards |
| Accurate title/position translation | High | Protocol violations |
| Passive voice in formal texts | Medium | Unnatural administrative text |
| Municipal/regional accuracy | Medium | Incorrect jurisdiction reference |

## Error Pattern Library

### EP-A1: Virastokieli (Bureaucratic Language) Errors
- **Common Error**: Using informal or active voice where Finnish administrative convention requires passive.
- **Correct**: `Hakemus käsitellään 30 päivän kuluessa.` (The application will be processed within 30 days.)
- **Rule**: Administrative Finnish uses passive voice extensively; do not force active voice.

### EP-A2: Law Reference Errors
- **Common Error**: Incorrect Finnish law titles or section numbering.
- **Correct**: Use exact Finnish law titles: `Työsopimuslaki (55/2001)`, not "Employment Contracts Act" translated literally.
- **Rule**: Always use official Finnish law titles with their statute numbers.

### EP-A3: EU Terminology Mismatch
- **Common Error**: Using non-standard Finnish EU terminology.
- **Correct**: Follow IATE database Finnish entries for EU-specific terms.
- **Rule**: Consult IATE (iate.europa.eu) for Finnish EU terminology.

### EP-A4: Title/Position Confusion
- **Common Error**: Translating Finnish titles into English equivalents that don't match the actual rank.
- **Correct**: `neuvos` ≠ "counsel" — it is a specific Finnish civil service rank. `ylijohtaja` ≠ "director" — it is "director general."
- **Rule**: Verify Finnish civil service rank before translating to/from English.

## Domain-Specific Reference

### Finnish Administrative System Overview
Finland has a **parliamentary democracy** with a president (head of state) and prime minister (head of government). The administrative system includes:
1. **Government** (hallitus) — cabinet of ministers.
2. **Parliament** (eduskunta) — 200 members, unicameral.
3. **Ministries** (ministeriöt) — 12 ministries as of 2024.
4. **Government agencies** (virastot) — execute policy in specific areas.
5. **Municipalities** (kunnat) — 309 municipalities with self-governance.

### Key Finnish Administrative Terms
- `Virkamies` = civil servant (general term).
- `Virka` = office/position (permanent government position).
- `Määräaikainen virka` = fixed-term position.
- `Virkanimike` = official title.
- `Ylin virkanimike` = highest official title in an agency.
- `Esittelijä` = case officer/preparer of decisions.
- `Päätöksenteko` = decision-making process.
- `Hallintolaki` (434/2003) = Administrative Procedure Act.
- `Asiointikieli` = language of correspondence (Finnish or Swedish).

### Finnish Bilingual Administration
Finland is officially **bilingual** (Finnish and Swedish). All central government documents must be available in both languages. In bilingual municipalities (23 municipalities), both languages have equal status. This affects:
- Document translation requirements.
- Legal terminology (Swedish terms may appear alongside Finnish).
- EU correspondence (Finnish and Swedish are both official EU languages).
