# Finnish Commercial Domain Translation

## Reader Model

### Target Reader Profile
- **Primary Reader**: Finnish business professionals, company directors (toimitusjohtajat), marketing managers, sales managers
- **Secondary Reader**: Business consultants, trade association representatives, chamber of commerce officials
- **Tertiary Reader**: Consumers, potential investors, business partners in international trade

### Reader Expectations
1. **Business Correspondence Format**: Finnish business letters follow established conventions (lähettäjän tiedot, vastaanottajan tiedot, päivämäärä, aihe, tervehdys, teksti, lopetus, allekirjoitus).
2. **Company Type Accuracy**: Correct use of Finnish company types (Oy, Oyj, Tmi, etc.).
3. **Register Consistency**: Finnish business language is generally less formal than many other European business languages; `sinä` is common even in semi-professional contexts.
4. **Marketing Language**: Finnish marketing text should be natural and idiomatic, not a calque of English.
5. **Contract Terms**: Commercial contract terms must use established Finnish legal and business vocabulary.

### Reader Expectation Matrix

| Expectation | Importance | Consequence if Unmet |
|-------------|------------|---------------------|
| Finnish business letter format | High | Unprofessional appearance |

## Error Pattern Library

### EP-C1: Company Type Confusion
- **Common Error**: Translating `Ltd.` as `Oyj` (public) instead of `Oy` (private).
- **Correct**: `Ltd.` = `Oy` (private limited). `Oyj` = public limited company (listed).
- **Rule**: Verify source company type before translating.

### EP-C2: Register Mismatch
- **Common Error**: Using overly formal `Te` register in marketing or casual business text.
- **Correct**: Finnish business culture is generally informal; `sinä` is common in marketing, customer-facing materials, and even some correspondence.
- **Rule**: Match register to document purpose, not source language formality.

### EP-C3: Literal Translation of Marketing Slogans
- **Common Error**: Translating English marketing slogans word-for-word.
- **Correct**: Create natural Finnish marketing copy that conveys the same meaning and appeal.
- **Rule**: Marketing translation requires localization, not translation. Create Finnish-native copy.

### EP-C4: Wrong Partitive Case
- **Common Error**: Using nominative where partitive is required for quantities.
- **Correct**: `5 tuotetta` (partitive), not `5 tuote`. Partitive required after numerals.
- **Rule**: After numerals in Finnish, always use partitive case for the counted noun.

## Domain-Specific Reference

### Finnish Business Culture
- Finnish business culture values **directness** and **efficiency**.
- First-name basis is common even in formal business settings after initial introduction.
- Punctuality is highly valued; being late is disrespectful.
- Business meetings are typically formal at first, becoming more relaxed over time.
- Decisions are made **consensus-based** in many Finnish companies.

### Finnish Business Letter Format
Standard Finnish business letter structure:
1. **Lähettäjän tiedot** (Sender information) — company name, address, Y-tunnus.
2. **Päivämäärä** (Date) — DD.MM.YYYY.
3. **Vastaanottajan tiedot** (Recipient information) — name, company, address.
4. **Aihe** (Subject) — brief description.
5. **Tervehdys** (Salutation) — `Arvoisa`, `Hyvä`, or specific name.
6. **Teksti** (Body) — clear, concise Finnish text.
7. **Lopetus** (Closing) — `Ystävällisin terveisin`, `Parhain terveisin`.
8. **Allekirjoitus** (Signature) — name, title, contact.

### Finnish Trade Terminology
- `Vienti` (export) and `tuonti` (import) are key trade terms.
- `Tulli` (customs) handles import/export regulations.
- `Finpro` (now part of Business Finland) assists with internationalization.
- `Kauppakamari` (Chamber of Commerce) provides business services.
- `Elinkeinoelämän keskusliitto EK` (Confederation of Finnish Industries) — major employer organization.
