## 1. Formality and Address (JA→HR)

Japanese has an extremely complex honorific system (keigo) with multiple levels of formality. Croatian has a simpler but still important T-V distinction (Vi/ti). The key challenge is mapping Japanese keigo levels to appropriate Croatian formality registers.

### 1.1 Second Person Pronoun Conversion

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | 貴社（きしゃ）/ 御社（おんしゃ） | Vaša tvrtka / Vaša firma | Referring to a company | "Vaša tvrtka" for writing, same for speech |
| 2 | お客様（おきゃくさま） | Kupac / Mušterija / Vi | Referring to a customer/client | "Vi" (formal) most common in service contexts |
| 3 | 様（さま） | g. / gđa. / poštovani/a | Attached to a personal name | Surname + gospodin/gospođa is standard formal address |
| 4 | さん | g. / gđa. / poštovani/a | Casual/equal relationship | Used in semi-formal Croatian contexts |
| 5 | （omit） | （omit） | When subject is clear | Croatian frequently omits the subject |
| 6 | 皆様（みなさま） | Poštovani / Svi prisutni | Multiple recipients | "Poštovani" for formal plural address |
| 7 | ご担当者様 / 各位 | Odgovorna osoba / Poštovani | Group address | "Poštovani" is standard for written notices |
| 8 | 拝啓（はいけい） | Poštovani / Poštovani gospodine | Letter opening | Croatian uses "Poštovani" as standard opening |
| 9 | 関係者各位（かんけいしゃかくい） | Svi zainteresirani / Poštovani | General notice | Standard for circular notices |

## 2. Dates, Times, and Numbers (JA→HR)

Japanese and Croatian use different date formats, number systems, and time notation. The conversion requires careful attention to the reversal of date components and the replacement of Japanese large-number units (万/億/兆) with standard numeric notation.

## 5. Administrative/Legal Style (JA→HR)

Japanese administrative, legal, and official documents use specific phrase patterns, formal grammar, and set expressions. Croatian legal and administrative writing has its own formal conventions that must be followed.

### 5.1 Legal "Shall" and Obligation

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | ～しなければならない | dužan je / mora | Obligation | "Shall" = legal obligation, not future tense |
| 2 | ～するものとする | obvezuje se da će | Stipulation | "Obvezuje se" for legal obligations |
| 3 | ～てはならない | ne smije | Prohibition | "Ne smije" for prohibition |
| 4 | 超えてはならない | ne smije prelaziti | Prohibition limit | Standard legal negative obligation |
| 5 | みなす | smatra se | Legal presumption | "Smatra se" standard |
| 6 | ～する権利を有する | ima pravo na | Entitlement | "Ima pravo" standard |
| 7 | ～について責任を負う | odgovoran je za | Responsibility | "Odgovoran je" standard |
| 8 | ～に準拠する | u skladu s | Governing law | "U skladu s" standard |

## 6. Punctuation (JA→HR)

Japanese and Croatian use different punctuation systems. Japanese full-width punctuation marks must be converted to half-width Croatian equivalents. The overall spacing and typesetting conventions also differ significantly.

### 6.1 Period and Comma

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | 。（句点） | . (točka) | End of sentence | Japanese full stop → Croatian period |
| 2 | 、（読点） | , (zarez) | Clause separator | Japanese reading mark → Croatian comma |
| 3 | , (Oxford comma) | zarez ispred "i" | Serial list | Croatian uses Oxford comma |
| 4 | , (in numbers) | 既に変換済み | Thousand separator | Keep converted format |
| 5 | . (in numbers) | 既に変換済み | Decimal point | Keep converted format |
| 6 | ； / 文分割 | ; (točka-zarez) | Clause junction | Use Croatian semicolon |
| 7 | ： | : (dvotočka) | List/explanation | Use Croatian colon |

### 6.2 Quotation Marks

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | 「」（かぎ括弧） | "..." (navodnici) | Standard quoted text | "Hello" → "Bok" |
| 2 | 『』（二重かぎ括弧） | '...' (polunavnici) | Quote within quote | Innermost quotes become '...' |
| 3 | 「」 | "..." | Irony/special use | Scare quotes become "..." |
| 4 | 「」for titles | "..." or *...* | Book/movie titles | Japanese brackets → Croatian quotes or italics |
| 5 | Italic (for emphasis) | *...* / kurziv | Emphasized word | Japanese uses dots (傍点) → Croatian uses italics |
| 6 | 「」technical terms | "..." | First-use term | Technical terms use Croatian quotes |
| 7 | 「」dialogue | "..." | Speech | Dialogue quotation marks |

### 6.3 Dashes and Hyphens

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | ——（2本ダッシュ） | — (crtica) | Parenthetical break | Japanese uses double dash → Croatian uses em dash |
| 2 | 〜（波ダッシュ） | – (crtica) / do | Range indication | "pages 10–20" → "stranice 10–20" or "10 do 20" |
| 3 | 〜 / – | – (crtica) | Connection | "Tokyo–London" → "Tokio–London" |
| 4 | - (hyphen, compound) | - (crtica) / restructure | Compound words | "state-of-the-art" → "najnoviji" (restructure) |
| 5 | - (hyphen, prefix) | - (crtica) / restructure | Prefixes | "ex-president" → "bivši predsjednik" (omit hyphen) |
| 6 | - (hyphen, katakana) | - / prema potrebi | Long vowel in katakana | "player" → "igrač" (native) |
| 7 | ・（中黒） | - / razmak | Katakana word boundary | "New York" → "New York" (no boundary needed) |

### 6.4 Ellipsis and Other Marks

| # | Japanese (Source) | Croatian (Target) | Condition | Notes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | …（三点リーダー） | ... (trotočka) | Omission/pause | Same count (3 dots) in Croatian |
| 2 | ……（6 dots） | ... (trotočka) | Trailing off | NEVER use 6 dots in Croatian |
| 3 | 長音 / 省略 | apostrof / produženje | Contraction/possession | "don't" → Croatian has no contraction; "O'Brien" → keep |
| 4 | ／（全角） | / (poluširina) | Alternatives | "and/or" → "ili" or "i/ili" |
| 5 | ％（全角） | % (poluširina) | Percentage | Use half-width percent sign |
| 6 | および / と / ＆ | i / te | Conjunction | Usually expanded to Croatian word |

### 6.5 Spacing and Typesetting Rules

| # | Rule | Description | Example |
|---|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | No spaces between Croatian text | Croatian text does not use spaces between words in the same way as Japanese | "Ovo je hrvatski tekst" |
| 2 | Half-width space between Croatian and Latin | Standard spacing in Croatian text | "Windows sustav" (with space) |
| 3 | Half-width punctuation | Use half-width punctuation marks in Croatian text | "." "," ":" are half-width |
| 4 | Half-width numbers and letters | Use half-width for digits and Latin letters | "2024.", "Windows" with half-width |
| 5 | No line-beginning punctuation | Do not place "," or "." at the start of a line | Wrap previous line to include them |
| 6 | Consistent bracket nesting | Match Croatian bracket conventions | "..." for nested quotes |
| 7 | No space before % in Croatian | In Croatian text, no space before percent sign | "50%" not "50 %" |
| 8 | Space after colon in Croatian | Croatian colon usage: space after colon | "Slijedeće: opis" |
