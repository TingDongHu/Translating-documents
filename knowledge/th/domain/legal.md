# Thai Legal Domain (กฎหมาย) — Domain Knowledge for Thai Legal Document Translation

---

## Reader Model

### Primary Reader: Thai Legal Practitioner (ทนายความ / นิติกร)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | ทนายความ (Lawyer), นิติกร (Legal Officer), ผู้พิพากษา (Judge), ตุลาการ (Justice) |
| **Education** | LL.B. (นิติศาสตรบัณฑิต) from Thai law school; Thai Bar exam for judicial roles |
| **Key Concern** | Legal precision paramount — a single mistranslated term changes legal effect. Thai legal language is highly formalized with fixed phrasings; deviations are unacceptable |
| **Work Context** | Law firm (สํานักงานกฎหมาย), corporate legal (ฝ่ายกฎหมาย), government legal office, court (ศาล) |
| **Expectations** | Exact correspondence of legal terms; preservation of code/article numbering; formal register matching Thai legal drafting |

### Secondary Reader: Judge / Court Official (ผู้พิพากษา / เจ้าหน้าที่ศาล)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Adjudicates disputes based on translated documents; reviews for fidelity |
| **Expectations** | Absolute accuracy; any ambiguity construed against submitting party; prefers certified translations (คําแปลรับรอง) |

| Thai | English | Authority Level |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| รัฐธรรมนูญ | Constitution | Highest law |
| พระราชบัญญัติ | Act | Primary legislation (Parliament) |
| พระราชกําหนด | Emergency Decree | Urgency, Article 172 |
| พระราชกฤษฎีกา | Royal Decree | Implementation |

### 9c. Court Filing — Black and Red Case Numbers

- **เลขคดีดํา** (Black case number) = assigned upon filing a lawsuit
- **เลขคดีแดง** (Red case number) = assigned when court renders judgment
- Both numbers must appear on all subsequent filings in the same case

## Standard Conventions

### 1. Citation Format for Thai Legislation

- **Form**: [Instrument Type] [Name] พ.ศ. [BE Year]
- **Example**: พระราชบัญญัติคุ้มครองข้อมูลส่วนบุคคล พ.ศ. 2562
- **English**: Personal Data Protection Act, B.E. 2562 (2019)
- **Section**: "Section 420" (capitalized, Section not "Art.")
- **Subsection**: Section 15(1)
- **Paragraph**: "paragraph two" or (2)
- Always include BE year and CE year in parentheses at first citation.

### 2. BE (Buddhist Era) Year Conversion

- BE to CE: subtract 543. CE to BE: add 543.
- Common: 2540 (1997), 2550 (2007), 2560 (2017), 2562 (2019), 2565 (2022)

### 6. Legal Language Register

- **Highest formal** (ภาษาแบบแผน): Statutes, court judgments, royal decrees; complex Pali-Sanskrit vocabulary; fixed phrasing; no contractions; elaborate honorifics
- **Standard formal** (ภาษากึ่งแบบแผน): Contracts, legal notices, office documents; formal but less archaic; includes defined terms; structured paragraphs
- **Semi-formal** (ภาษาทางการ): Legal correspondence, memos; moderate formality; direct phrasing
- Avoid casual register entirely in legal documents

**Key characteristics of formal Thai legal language**:
- Heavy use of Pali and Sanskrit derivatives (e.g., "นิติ" for legal)
- Compound verbs (กระทํา, มีสิทธิ, ได้รับ)
- Nominalization with การ/ความ (การกระทํา, ความยินยอม)
- Elaborate connective phrases (ทั้งนี้, อนึ่ง, ภายใต้บังคับแห่ง)
- Honorifics and formal pronouns (กระผม, ข้าพเจ้า, ท่าน)
- No contractions, no slang, no colloquialisms

### 7. Signature / Witness Block

ลงชื่อ......................................... (Party)

### 9. Translation Notes Convention

- Include translator's notes in brackets for concepts without direct equivalents: **[Translator's note: ...]**
- For Pali/Sanskrit terms: provide etymology when relevant
- For culturally specific concepts: brief explanation

### 10. Translation for Court Filing

- Translator signs every page (ลงชื่อกํากับทุกหน้า)
- Attach copy of translator's ID card (สําเนาบัตรประชาชน)
- Attach original document or explain absence
- Statement: "คําแปลนี้ถูกต้องและครบถ้วนตรงตามต้นฉบับ"
- Binding and seal if from law office; notarization for foreign use
