## Reader Model

### Primary Reader: Thai CPA / Accountant (ผู้สอบบัญชี / นักบัญชี)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | ผู้สอบบัญชีรับอนุญาต (CPA), นักบัญชี (Accountant) |
| **Education** | B.Acc. (ปริญญาตรี สาขาการบัญชี); CPA from สภาวิชาชีพบัญชี (FAP) |
| **Key Concern** | Terminology must match TFRS precisely; incorrect mapping leads to audit findings and regulatory sanctions |
| **Work Context** | Accounting firm (สํานักงานบัญชี), corporate accounting, government audit (สตง.) |
| **Expectations** | Strict FAP-approved terminology; standardized formatting per Thai Accounting Standards; accurate tax references |

### Secondary Reader: Revenue Department Tax Auditor (เจ้าหน้าที่สรรพากร)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role** | Audits financial documents for CIT, VAT, and WHT compliance |
| **Key Concern** | Correct classification of income/expenses; proper tax base calculation; arm's length pricing |

### Tertiary Reader: SET Investor / Financial Analyst

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Profile** | Stock Exchange of Thailand investor, fund manager |
| **Need** | Accurate understanding of Thai company financials for investment decisions |

## Decision Framework

### 1. Currency — Thai Baht (บาท — ฿)

| Element | Format |
|---------|--------|
| Symbol | ฿ or THB |
| Position | ฿100 or 100 บาท |
| Decimal | ฿100.50 (100 บาท 50 สตางค์) |
| Thousands | ฿1,000,000 |
| Formal words | จํานวนหนึ่งล้านบาทถ้วน = One Million Baht only |
| Subunit | 100 สตางค์ = 1 บาท |

**Rule**: Always specify THB in bilingual documents for clarity.

## Standard Conventions

### 1. Financial Statement Format (Thai Style)

1. **Header**: Company name / Statement type / "สิ้นสุดวันที่ [date]"
2. **Comparison**: Current and prior period side by side
3. **Notes cross-reference**: Each line item has "หมายเหตุ [X]"
4. **Totals**: Bolded with "รวม" prefix
5. **Rounding header**: พันบาท (Thousand Baht) or ล้านบาท (Million Baht)
6. **Period labels**: งวดปัจจุบัน = Current, งวดก่อน = Prior; ณ วันที่ = As at

### 2. Auditor's Report — Opinion Phrasing

"ข้าพเจ้าเห็นว่า งบการเงินข้างต้นนี้แสดงฐานะการเงินของบริษัท... อย่างถูกต้องตามที่ควรในสาระสําคัญตามมาตรฐานการรายงานทางการเงิน"

Translation: "In our opinion, the accompanying financial statements present fairly, in all material respects, ..."

### 3. Baht Unit Formatting

|---------|--------|

### 4. Tax Filing Deadlines

| Filing | Form | Due |
|--------|------|-----|
| Half-year CIT | ภ.ง.ด. 51 | 2 months after half-year end |
| Annual CIT | ภ.ง.ด. 50 | 150 days after year end |
| Monthly WHT | ภ.ง.ด. 3/53 | 7th of following month |
| Monthly VAT | ภ.พ. 30 | 15th of following month |
| Annual PIT | ภ.ง.ด. 90/91 | March 31 |

### 5. Notable Thai Accounting Practices

- **Legal reserve**: 5% of net profit annually until 10% of registered capital (per CCC)
- **BOI incentives**: Tax holidays (8 yrs CIT exemption + 50% reduction 5 yrs)
- **Transfer pricing**: Documentation required per Revenue Code Section 71 ter
- **Revenue Code** governs tax; TFRS governs financial reporting

### 9. Translation Conventions for Financial Statements

1. Translate line items exactly per FAP glossary — no synonyms
2. Notes must correspond to Thai numbering (หมายเหตุ 1-30+)
3. Always include "THB" or "บาท" in translated headers
4. Maintain two-column (current/prior) format
5. Note rounding: "in Thousands of Baht"
6. Auditor's opinion: translate verbatim, do not paraphrase
7. Never convert or round differently than the original
8. If "สินทรัพย์รวม" = "Total Assets" once, use same term everywhere
9. Translate all notes faithfully — omissions = audit qualification risk
10. Legal reserve: translate explicitly; do not omit explanation
